## Current Metagame Snapshot

### Overall Health
The March 2026 B&R announcement made **no changes** to Legacy. The format is considered healthy based on ~6,433 deck entries covering ~35,000 matches since the [Entomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Entomb%22) ban in November 2025. Average weekly participation: ~446 entries.

## Key Format Trends

### 1. Dimir Tempo Dominance

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Orcish+Bowmasters&format=image&version=normal&set=ltr" alt="Orcish Bowmasters" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Tamiyo%2C+Inquisitive+Student&format=image&version=normal&set=dsk" alt="Tamiyo, Inquisitive Student" width="217">

At 14.6% meta share, Dimir Tempo is the most played deck by a wide margin. Built around [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22), Tamiyo Inquisitive Student, [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22), and the blue cantrip/[Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) shell. Its 52.7% win rate is strong but not oppressive — the format has answers.

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Thoughtseize&format=image&version=normal&set=lrw" alt="Thoughtseize" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Force+of+Will&format=image&version=normal&set=all" alt="Force of Will" width="217">

**Why it's good**: Bowmasters punishes opposing cantrips, [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) proactively strips combo pieces, and [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) handles everything else. The deck is flexible and has no truly terrible matchups.

**How to beat it**: Go bigger (Sneak and Show, Lands), go under (Oops, Belcher), or go over (4c Control with more card advantage).

### 2. Energy Cards Reshape Aggro

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Ocelot+Pride&format=image&version=normal&set=mh3" alt="Ocelot Pride" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Guide+of+Souls&format=image&version=normal&set=mh3" alt="Guide of Souls" width="217">

MH3 energy cards ([Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22), [Guide of Souls](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Guide+of+Souls%22), [Amped Raptor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Amped+Raptor%22), Ajani Nacatl Pariah) created viable non-blue aggressive strategies. Ocelot Pride Midrange at 8.5% and Mardu Energy at ~3% represent a new class of Legacy deck that doesn't rely on [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22).

**Impact**: The format is less "blue or bust" than any time in recent memory. Non-blue aggro/midrange has real teeth.

### 3. Combo Diversity
Unlike many Legacy eras where one combo deck dominates (Reanimator in 2024, Storm in 2018), the current meta has combo spread across many archetypes: Oops (8.2%), Sneak and Show (6.7%), TES (3.5%), [Mystic Forge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mystic+Forge%22) (3.0%), [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) (2.7%), Painter (4.1%), [Aluren](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Aluren%22) (1.2%), and more.

**Impact**: You can't just pack one type of hate and cover all combos. Graveyard hate doesn't stop [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22). Counterspells don't stop Chalice decks. This diversity rewards versatile sideboards and flexible maindecks.

### 4. Post-Entomb Format
The November 2025 [Entomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Entomb%22) ban removed BR Reanimator as a tier 1 strategy. The deck still exists using Faithful Mending and other discard outlets, but it's slower and less consistent. The meta share previously occupied by Reanimator has been absorbed primarily by Oops! All Spells and Sneak and Show — other ways to cheat big creatures into play.

### 5. Artifact Strategies Thriving

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Chalice+of+the+Void&format=image&version=normal&set=mrd" alt="Chalice of the Void" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Urza%27s+Saga&format=image&version=normal&set=mh2" alt="Urza's Saga" width="217">

[Mystic Forge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mystic+Forge%22) Combo (3.0%), Affinity Stompy (2.4%), Blue Artifacts (1.1%), Painter (4.1%), and 8-Cast variants collectively represent ~15% of the field. [Urza's Saga](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Urza%27s+Saga%22) is the glue card enabling many of these strategies. [Chalice of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chalice+of+the+Void%22) on 1 remains one of the best cards against the cantrip-heavy metagame.

### Cost Drivers
The overwhelming cost driver is **Reserved List cards**, especially dual lands:
- [Underground Sea](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Underground+Sea%22): $600-800 (Dimir Tempo needs 3-4)
- [Volcanic Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Volcanic+Island%22): $500-700 (Izzet Delver needs 2-3)
- [Tropical Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tropical+Island%22): $400-600
- [The Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22The+Tabernacle+at+Pendrell+Vale%22): $5,000+ (Lands only)
- [Gaea's Cradle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Gaea%27s+Cradle%22): $800-1,000 (Elves, Cradle Control)
- [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond%22): $300-400 (Storm, Dredge)

Decks that avoid dual lands entirely (Oops, Eldrazi, Burn, Belcher, Red Stompy) are dramatically cheaper.

## Deep Dive: Tier 1 Archetypes

### Dimir Tempo (14.6% — The Deck to Beat)

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Murktide+Regent&format=image&version=normal&set=mh2" alt="Murktide Regent" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Barrowgoyf&format=image&version=normal&set=mh3" alt="Barrowgoyf" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Daze&format=image&version=normal&set=nem" alt="Daze" width="217">

**Core**: [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22), Tamiyo Inquisitive Student, [Murktide Regent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Murktide+Regent%22), [Barrowgoyf](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Barrowgoyf%22), [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22), [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22), [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22), [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22), [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22), [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22)

**Why it's #1**: The deck combines the best proactive disruption ([Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22)) with the best reactive disruption ([Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22), [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22)) and threats that generate value passively (Bowmasters punishes every cantrip the opponent casts). It has no auto-loss matchups and can adapt its role — aggro vs. combo, control vs. aggro.

**Key matchup dynamics**:
- vs. [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) Midrange: Slightly favored. [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) disrupts their curve. Bowmasters is excellent against their token-generating creatures. Post-board [Hydroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Hydroblast%22) and [Toxic Deluge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Toxic+Deluge%22) help.
- vs. Oops! All Spells: Even to slightly unfavorable game 1 (they're faster). Post-board with [Leyline of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Leyline+of+the+Void%22) and [Surgical Extraction](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Surgical+Extraction%22), the matchup flips to favorable.
- vs. Sneak and Show: Even. [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) and [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) counter [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22), but [Sneak Attack](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sneak+Attack%22) is harder to interact with. [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) helps see what they have.
- vs. Lands: Unfavorable. [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22)-proof mana base, [Maze of Ith](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Maze+of+Ith%22) blanks creatures, and [Punishing Fire](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Punishing+Fire%22) grinds out small threats. [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Depths%22) combo threatens a sudden 20/20.
- vs. Storm: Favorable. [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) + [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) is the exact disruption package Storm hates. Clock them fast before they rebuild.

**Sideboard priorities**: [Leyline of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Leyline+of+the+Void%22) (graveyard combo), [Hydroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Hydroblast%22) (red decks, [Pyroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyroblast%22) wars), [Surgical Extraction](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Surgical+Extraction%22) (specific combo pieces), [Toxic Deluge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Toxic+Deluge%22) (go-wide strategies).

### Ocelot Pride Midrange (8.5% — The New Wave)

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Ocelot+Pride&format=image&version=normal&set=mh3" alt="Ocelot Pride" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Guide+of+Souls&format=image&version=normal&set=mh3" alt="Guide of Souls" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Solitude&format=image&version=normal&set=mh2" alt="Solitude" width="217">

**Core**: [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22), Ajani Nacatl Pariah, [Guide of Souls](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Guide+of+Souls%22), Phelia Exuberant Shepherd, [Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22), [Solitude](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Solitude%22), [Broadside Bombardiers](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Broadside+Bombardiers%22)

**Why it's rising**: Energy cards from MH3 created a critical mass of efficient non-blue threats. The deck doesn't need [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22) or [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) — it wins through raw card quality and board presence. [Guide of Souls](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Guide+of+Souls%22) generates life and energy, [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) creates tokens on every non-combat damage trigger, and the deck snowballs quickly.

**Key matchup dynamics**:
- vs. Dimir Tempo: Slightly unfavorable. Bowmasters and [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) are effective, but the deck can grind through disruption.
- vs. Combo: Unfavorable without blue countermagic. Relies on [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben%22) effects and fast clocks.
- vs. Prison/Stompy: Favorable. Goes wide around [Chalice of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chalice+of+the+Void%22).
- vs. Lands: Even. Can race the Depths combo with a fast enough board.

### Oops! All Spells (8.2% — The Fastest Combo)

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Balustrade+Spy&format=image&version=normal&set=gtc" alt="Balustrade Spy" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Thassa%27s+Oracle&format=image&version=normal&set=thb" alt="Thassa's Oracle" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Dark+Ritual&format=image&version=normal&set=lea" alt="Dark Ritual" width="217">

**Core**: [Balustrade Spy](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Balustrade+Spy%22), [Undercity Informer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Undercity+Informer%22), [Lotus Petal](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lotus+Petal%22), [Dark Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Ritual%22), Elvish/[Simian Spirit Guide](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Simian+Spirit+Guide%22), [Pact of Negation](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pact+of+Negation%22), [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22), [Narcomoeba](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Narcomoeba%22), [Dread Return](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dread+Return%22), [Thassa's Oracle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thassa%27s+Oracle%22)

**How it works**: The deck runs zero lands. Cast [Balustrade Spy](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Balustrade+Spy%22) or [Undercity Informer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Undercity+Informer%22) targeting yourself — your entire library goes to the graveyard. Narcomoebas trigger, sacrifice them to [Dread Return](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dread+Return%22) targeting [Thassa's Oracle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thassa%27s+Oracle%22). With zero cards in library, Oracle wins the game. Can win turn 1.

**Why it's tier 1**: Post-[Entomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Entomb%22) ban, this became the premier "cheat" combo deck. It's faster than Reanimator ever was and has [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) + [Pact of Negation](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pact+of+Negation%22) for protection. The deck is also extremely budget-friendly (~$1,100).

**Key matchup dynamics**:
- vs. Fair Blue (Dimir, Delver): Unfavorable game 1 against Force + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) + [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22). Post-board with Leyline hate, it gets worse.
- vs. Fair Nonblue (D&T, Stompy): Favorable. They lack free countermagic. Chalice on 0 helps but doesn't stop everything.
- vs. Lands: Very favorable. Lands can't interact with the combo at instant speed.

**Weaknesses**: Extremely vulnerable to [Leyline of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Leyline+of+the+Void%22) (36% of decks play it), [Surgical Extraction](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Surgical+Extraction%22), and [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22). Mulligans aggressively and folds to its own inconsistency.

### Sneak and Show (6.7% — The Classic Big Mana Combo)

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Show+and+Tell&format=image&version=normal&set=usg" alt="Show and Tell" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn&format=image&version=normal&set=roe" alt="Emrakul, the Aeons Torn" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Ancient+Tomb&format=image&version=normal&set=tmp" alt="Ancient Tomb" width="217">

**Core**: [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22), [Sneak Attack](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sneak+Attack%22), Emrakul the Aeons Torn, Atraxa Grand Unifier, [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22), [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22), [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22), [Lotus Petal](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lotus+Petal%22), [Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22)

**How it works**: [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) puts any permanent into play for 3 mana. [Sneak Attack](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sneak+Attack%22) gives any creature haste for R. The targets are game-ending: Emrakul (15/15, flying, annihilator 6, extra turn) and Atraxa (7/7, draws 10+ cards on ETB). Either one typically wins the game immediately.

**Why it endures**: The deck has existed since 2011 and consistently posts results because the combo is fast (turn 2-3), hard to interact with ([Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) can't be countered by creature removal), and backed by [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) for protection.

## Deep Dive: Tier 2 Highlights

### Painter (4.1%)

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Painter%27s+Servant&format=image&version=normal&set=shm" alt="Painter's Servant" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Grindstone&format=image&version=normal&set=tmp" alt="Grindstone" width="217">

Two-card combo: [Painter's Servant](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Painter%27s+Servant%22) (names a color) + [Grindstone](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grindstone%22) (mills 2 cards at a time, repeating if they share a color — they always do when everything is the named color). Wins by milling the opponent's entire library. [Red Elemental Blast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Red+Elemental+Blast%22) becomes unconditional removal/counterspell when everything is "blue." Backed by [Goblin Welder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Goblin+Welder%22) and [Goblin Engineer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Goblin+Engineer%22) for artifact recursion. [Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22) for acceleration.

### Lands (3.8%)

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Life+from+the+Loam&format=image&version=normal&set=rav" alt="Life from the Loam" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Dark+Depths&format=image&version=normal&set=csp" alt="Dark Depths" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Exploration&format=image&version=normal&set=usg" alt="Exploration" width="217">

The most unique deck in Legacy. Wins through land-based strategies: [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Life+from+the+Loam%22) + [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) locks opponents out of mana. [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Depths%22) + [Thespian's Stage](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thespian%27s+Stage%22) creates Marit Lage (a 20/20 indestructible token). [Exploration](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Exploration%22) and [Mox Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mox+Diamond%22) enable explosive starts. [Maze of Ith](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Maze+of+Ith%22) neutralizes attackers. [Punishing Fire](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Punishing+Fire%22) recurs from the graveyard with [Grove of the Burnwillows](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grove+of+the+Burnwillows%22). The deck plays almost entirely at instant speed using land abilities. Paper cost: $8,284 ([The Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22The+Tabernacle+at+Pendrell+Vale%22) alone is $5,000+).

### The EPIC Storm (3.5%)

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Burning+Wish&format=image&version=normal&set=jud" alt="Burning Wish" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond&format=image&version=normal&set=mir" alt="Lion's Eye Diamond" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Tendrils+of+Agony&format=image&version=normal&set=scg" alt="Tendrils of Agony" width="217">

Storm combo using [Burning Wish](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Burning+Wish%22) (tutors sorceries from sideboard), [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond%22) (3 mana at instant speed), [Dark Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Ritual%22), and [Rite of Flame](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rite+of+Flame%22) to generate a lethal storm count. Finishes with [Tendrils of Agony](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tendrils+of+Agony%22) (each copy drains 2 life, with 10+ storm copies that's 20+ damage) or [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Empty+the+Warrens%22) (20+ goblin tokens). More resilient than ANT variants due to Burning Wish's flexibility.

### Eldrazi (3.1%)

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Thought-Knot+Seer&format=image&version=normal&set=ogw" alt="Thought-Knot Seer" width="217">

Colorless prison-aggro using [Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22), [City of Traitors](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22City+of+Traitors%22), and [Eldrazi Temple](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Eldrazi+Temple%22) for fast mana. Deploys [Chalice of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chalice+of+the+Void%22) on 1 (locks out cantrips and most cheap spells), then follows with [Thought-Knot Seer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thought-Knot+Seer%22), [Reality Smasher](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Reality+Smasher%22), and [Endbringer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endbringer%22). Kozilek's Command provides flexible disruption. Punishes blue cantrip decks hard but folds to combo that doesn't care about Chalice.

### Death and Taxes (2.0%)

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben&format=image&version=normal&set=dka" alt="Thalia, Guardian of Thraben" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Stoneforge+Mystic&format=image&version=normal&set=wwk" alt="Stoneforge Mystic" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Rishadan+Port&format=image&version=normal&set=mmq" alt="Rishadan Port" width="217">

White-based hatebear deck using [Aether Vial](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Aether+Vial%22) to cheat creatures into play at instant speed. [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben%22) taxes noncreature spells. [Flickerwisp](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Flickerwisp%22) and [Skyclave Apparition](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Skyclave+Apparition%22) handle problem permanents. [Rishadan Port](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rishadan+Port%22) and [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) deny mana. [Stoneforge Mystic](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stoneforge+Mystic%22) fetches equipment ([Batterskull](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Batterskull%22), [Kaldra Compleat](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Kaldra+Compleat%22)). Current builds often splash black (Yorion companion) for [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22) and [Plague Engineer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Plague+Engineer%22).

### Doomsday (2.7%)

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Doomsday&format=image&version=normal&set=wth" alt="Doomsday" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Ponder&format=image&version=normal&set=lrw" alt="Ponder" width="217">

The most skill-intensive combo deck in Legacy. Resolving [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) (BBB, 3 mana) lets you search your library for 5 cards and stack them as your new library. You then draw through the pile to win with [Thassa's Oracle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thassa%27s+Oracle%22) (0 cards in library = win). The puzzle is constructing a pile that works through the opponent's disruption. Requires deep format knowledge to know what to play around.

## Historical Meta Evolution

### How the Meta Shifted Over Major Eras

**2011-2013: The First Golden Age**

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Delver+of+Secrets&format=image&version=normal&set=isd" alt="Delver of Secrets" width="217">

- Dominated by RUG Delver, Miracles, and BUG Delver
- [Deathrite Shaman](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Deathrite+Shaman%22) enabled 3-4 color fair decks
- [True-Name Nemesis](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22True-Name+Nemesis%22) (Nov 2013) shook up creature strategies

**2014-2016: [Treasure Cruise](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Treasure+Cruise%22) and Eldrazi Winter**

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Treasure+Cruise&format=image&version=normal&set=ktk" alt="Treasure Cruise" width="217">

- [Treasure Cruise](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Treasure+Cruise%22) briefly broke the format (banned Jan 2015)
- Miracles peaked at ~15% meta share with [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sensei%27s+Divining+Top%22) + [Counterbalance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Counterbalance%22)
- Eldrazi (OGW) created a brief period of dominance before settling

**2017-2018: The Great Unbundling**
- [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sensei%27s+Divining+Top%22) banned (April 2017) — Miracles gutted, format diversifies
- [Deathrite Shaman](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Deathrite+Shaman%22) and Gitaxian Probe banned (July 2018) — 4-color piles collapse
- Czech Pile (4c Control) era ends, fair blue becomes 1-2 color decks again

**2019-2020: Modern Horizons 1 and Companion Era**

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Wrenn+and+Six&format=image&version=normal&set=mh1" alt="Wrenn and Six" width="217">

- [Wrenn and Six](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wrenn+and+Six%22) dominated RUG Delver (banned Nov 2019)
- Underworld Breach combo (banned March 2020)
- Arcum's Astrolabe enabled 4-5 color decks too easily (banned June 2020)
- Lurrus briefly warped companion rules before errata

**2021-2022: Modern Horizons 2 Revolution**

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Dragon%27s+Rage+Channeler&format=image&version=normal&set=mh2" alt="Dragon's Rage Channeler" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=The+One+Ring&format=image&version=normal&set=ltr" alt="The One Ring" width="217">

- [Dragon's Rage Channeler](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dragon%27s+Rage+Channeler%22), [Murktide Regent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Murktide+Regent%22), Ragavan, [Urza's Saga](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Urza%27s+Saga%22) all entered at once
- UR Delver became the best deck — Ragavan on turn 1, Murktide on turn 2-3
- Initiative creatures ([Caves of Chaos Adventurer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Caves+of+Chaos+Adventurer%22)) created White Stompy
- Most transformative single set for Legacy since original dual lands

**2023-2024: Lord of the Rings and MH3**
- [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22) (June 2023) — format-warping. Punishes every cantrip, kills small creatures
- [The One Ring](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22The+One+Ring%22) — unkillable card advantage engine
- [Grief](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grief%22) + Scam strategies emerge (Grief later banned)
- MH3 (2024): Energy cards create non-blue aggro. Nadu banned quickly. [Psychic Frog](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Psychic+Frog%22) banned.

**2025-2026: Post-[Entomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Entomb%22) Era**
- [Entomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Entomb%22) banned November 2025 — Reanimator drops from tier 1
- Dimir Tempo rises to dominance at 14.6%
- Energy cards mature into established archetypes ([Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22), Mardu)
- Format considered healthy — most diverse era in Legacy history with 54+ archetypes

## Brewing Opportunities

### Underplayed but High Win-Rate Strategies
Based on the data, these archetypes have competitive win rates but low meta shares — potential opportunities for brewers:

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Aluren&format=image&version=normal&set=tmp" alt="Aluren" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Gaea%27s+Cradle&format=image&version=normal&set=usg" alt="Gaea's Cradle" width="217">

1. **Aluren (1.2%)**: Won the Legacy Showcase Qualifier on April 5, 2026. Combo-control hybrid that's hard to hate out. Low meta share means opponents aren't prepared.
2. **Cradle Control (2.3%)**: Growing archetype using [Gaea's Cradle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Gaea%27s+Cradle%22) for absurd mana generation. [Green Sun's Zenith](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Green+Sun%27s+Zenith%22) toolbox gives flexibility.
3. **Cloudpost Ramp (~1%)**: Posts consistent 5-0s. Most opponents don't know the matchup well.
4. **Esper Vial (~0.2%)**: Appeared in recent Challenge top 8s. [Aether Vial](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Aether+Vial%22) + hatebears in 3 colors with [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22).

### Meta Gaps to Exploit
- 14.6% Dimir Tempo means decks that consistently beat UB get a free 15% of matchups
- Lands is favored against most fair decks but underplayed (3.8%) — likely due to cost ($8,284)
- Artifact hate ([Force of Vigor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Vigor%22), [Null Rod](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Null+Rod%22)) is at ~20% — artifact strategies thrive when hate drops below 25%
- Graveyard hate is high (Leyline 36%) but most of it is narrowly targeted — decks that use the graveyard incidentally (not as primary plan) dodge it

## Format Entry Recommendations

### For New Legacy Players
1. **Start on MTGO** — much cheaper, active leagues, immediate matchmaking
2. **Pick one deck and learn it deeply** — Legacy rewards format knowledge over deck-hopping
3. **Read archetype primers on The Source** — community knowledge is invaluable
4. **Goldfish your deck extensively** — know your sequencing before playing against opponents
5. **Learn what the top 10 decks do** — you need to know what you're fighting before you can fight it
6. **Use Moxfield for brewing** — visual tools help with mana base construction

### For Experienced Players Looking to Brew
1. **Check mtgdecks.net/Legacy/winrates** — find archetypes with high win rates but low meta share (underplayed but strong)
2. **Study the MTGTop8 archetype pages** — see how decks have evolved over time
3. **Track MTGGoldfish 5-0 lists** — unique or innovative lists often show up in league results
4. **Identify meta weaknesses** — if 14.6% of the field is Dimir Tempo, build something that beats it
5. **Budget your sideboard slots** — with 54+ archetypes, you can't hate on everything. Target the top 5-8 decks.
