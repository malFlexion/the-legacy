- Detailed matchup guides and sideboard strategies
- Community-driven card evaluation for specific archetypes
- Meta-discussion and format health debates
- Invaluable for learning the nuances of a specific deck

### Moxfield — [moxfield.com](https://www.moxfield.com)
**Best for**: Viewing and brewing decklists with clean UI and visual tools.
- Visual deckbuilding with card images and mana curve charts
- Primer/description fields for published decklists

- Playtest mode for goldfishing
- Community-shared lists with tags for format/archetype

## Key Format Trends

### 1. Dimir Tempo Dominance
At 14.6% meta share, Dimir Tempo is the most played deck by a wide margin. Built around Orcish Bowmasters, Tamiyo Inquisitive Student, Thoughtseize, and the blue cantrip/Force of Will shell. Its 52.7% win rate is strong but not oppressive — the format has answers.

**Why it's good**: Bowmasters punishes opposing cantrips, Thoughtseize proactively strips combo pieces, and Force of Will handles everything else. The deck is flexible and has no truly terrible matchups.

**How to beat it**: Go bigger (Sneak and Show, Lands), go under (Oops, Belcher), or go over (4c Control with more card advantage).

### 2. Energy Cards Reshape Aggro
MH3 energy cards (Ocelot Pride, Guide of Souls, Amped Raptor, Ajani Nacatl Pariah) created viable non-blue aggressive strategies. Ocelot Pride Midrange at 8.5% and Mardu Energy at ~3% represent a new class of Legacy deck that doesn't rely on Brainstorm.

**Impact**: The format is less "blue or bust" than any time in recent memory. Non-blue aggro/midrange has real teeth.

### 3. Combo Diversity
Unlike many Legacy eras where one combo deck dominates (Reanimator in 2024, Storm in 2018), the current meta has combo spread across many archetypes: Oops (8.2%), Sneak and Show (6.7%), TES (3.5%), Mystic Forge (3.0%), Doomsday (2.7%), Painter (4.1%), Aluren (1.2%), and more.

**Impact**: You can't just pack one type of hate and cover all combos. Graveyard hate doesn't stop Show and Tell. Counterspells don't stop Chalice decks. This diversity rewards versatile sideboards and flexible maindecks.

### 4. Post-Entomb Format
The November 2025 Entomb ban removed BR Reanimator as a tier 1 strategy. The deck still exists using Faithful Mending and other discard outlets, but it's slower and less consistent. The meta share previously occupied by Reanimator has been absorbed primarily by Oops! All Spells and Sneak and Show — other ways to cheat big creatures into play.

### 5. Artifact Strategies Thriving
Mystic Forge Combo (3.0%), Affinity Stompy (2.4%), Blue Artifacts (1.1%), Painter (4.1%), and 8-Cast variants collectively represent ~15% of the field. Urza's Saga is the glue card enabling many of these strategies. Chalice of the Void on 1 remains one of the best cards against the cantrip-heavy metagame.

### Paper Price Ranges (April 2026)
| Tier | Range | Examples |
|------|-------|---------|
| Budget | $800-$1,500 | Oops! All Spells ($1,102), Burn ($1,500), Belcher ($1,200) |
| Mid | $2,000-$4,000 | Eldrazi ($2,128), Painter ($2,481), Ocelot Pride ($2,569), Izzet Delver ($3,794) |
| Expensive | $4,000-$6,000 | Dimir Tempo ($4,955), Doomsday ($5,285), TES ($5,657), Sneak and Show ($4,370) |
| Very Expensive | $6,000+ | Lands ($8,284), Colorless Stompy ($13,328) |

### Cost Drivers
The overwhelming cost driver is **Reserved List cards**, especially dual lands:
- Underground Sea: $600-800 (Dimir Tempo needs 3-4)
- Volcanic Island: $500-700 (Izzet Delver needs 2-3)
- Tropical Island: $400-600
- The Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale: $5,000+ (Lands only)
- Gaea's Cradle: $800-1,000 (Elves, Cradle Control)
- Lion's Eye Diamond: $300-400 (Storm, Dredge)

Decks that avoid dual lands entirely (Oops, Eldrazi, Burn, Belcher, Red Stompy) are dramatically cheaper.

## Format Entry Recommendations

### For New Legacy Players
1. **Start on MTGO** — much cheaper, active leagues, immediate matchmaking
2. **Pick one deck and learn it deeply** — Legacy rewards format knowledge over deck-hopping
3. **Read archetype primers on The Source** — community knowledge is invaluable
4. **Goldfish your deck extensively** — know your sequencing before playing against opponents
5. **Learn what the top 10 decks do** — you need to know what you're fighting before you can fight it
6. **Use Moxfield for brewing** — visual tools help with mana base construction

### For Experienced Players Looking to Brew
1. **Check mtgdecks.net/Legacy/winrates** — find archetypes with high win rates but low meta share (underplayed but strong)
2. **Study the MTGTop8 archetype pages** — see how decks have evolved over time
3. **Track MTGGoldfish 5-0 lists** — unique or innovative lists often show up in league results
4. **Identify meta weaknesses** — if 14.6% of the field is Dimir Tempo, build something that beats it
5. **Budget your sideboard slots** — with 54+ archetypes, you can't hate on everything. Target the top 5-8 decks.
