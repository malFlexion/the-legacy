## Gameplay Terms

**basic**: A basic land (Plains, Island, Swamp, Mountain, Forest). In Legacy, running at least a few basics protects against Wasteland and Blood Moon. 'Fetch for basic' means getting a basic land to play around land destruction. *(See also: fetch land, Blood Moon, Wasteland.)*

**beater**: A creature whose primary purpose is to deal combat damage efficiently. 'Murktide is a great beater.' Implies a strong power/toughness-to-cost ratio without significant utility abilities. *(See also: threat, clock.)*

**blank**: A card in hand that is useless in the current matchup. 'My Swords to Plowshares are blanks against Storm.' Post-sideboard, you replace blanks with relevant cards. *(See also: brick, dead card, sideboard.)*

**blow out**: A play that devastates the opponent, usually catching them off guard. 'Surgical Extraction on their Reanimator target was a total blowout.' The opponent overcommits to a line that gets punished. *(See also: punish, gotcha.)*

**board wipe**: Synonym for sweeper and wrath. A spell that destroys all creatures. In Legacy, Terminus is the most efficient due to its miracle cost of W. *(Related cards: Terminus, Toxic Deluge. See also: wrath, sweeper.)*

**bolt**: Lightning Bolt, or any 3-damage instant-speed removal spell. Used as a verb: 'bolt the bird' means kill the opponent's mana creature with a burn spell. The gold standard for efficient removal in Legacy. *(Related cards: Lightning Bolt. See also: burn, removal, bolt the bird.)*

**bomb**: A powerful card that can win the game on its own if unanswered. More commonly used in Limited, but in Legacy, cards like Jace the Mind Sculptor or Uro can be bombs. 'That Murktide is a bomb.' *(See also: threat, finisher.)*

**bounce**: To return a permanent to its owner's hand. Karakas bounces legendary creatures. Jace's -1 bounces any creature. Teferi bounces any permanent. In Legacy, bounce is removal that doesn't give card advantage. *(Related cards: Karakas, Jace, the Mind Sculptor, Teferi, Time Raveler. See also: Karakas bounce, removal.)*

**brew**: To create an original deck design rather than copying an established list. A 'brewer' is someone who enjoys building new decks. 'I'm brewing a new Aluren list.' Can be a noun: 'that's an interesting brew.' *(See also: netdeck, spice, jank.)*

**brick**: To draw a card that is useless in the current game state. 'I bricked on my Brainstorm, drew three lands.' The opposite of drawing gas. *(See also: whiff, flood, gas.)*

**cantrip**: A spell that draws a card as part of its effect, essentially replacing itself. Legacy is defined by its powerful cantrips (Brainstorm, Ponder, Preordain) which provide unmatched consistency and card selection. *(Related cards: Brainstorm, Ponder, Preordain, Consider. See also: card advantage, card selection, brainstorm.)*

**cascade**: A triggered ability that exiles cards from the top of your library until you hit one with a lower mana value, then casts it for free. Shardless Agent cascading into Ancestral Vision or Hymn to Tourach is a powerful Legacy play. *(Related cards: Shardless Agent. See also: value.)*

**chump block**: Blocking with a small creature that will die just to prevent damage. 'Chump block the Marit Lage with a token.' A desperation play that trades a creature for one turn of survival. *(See also: block.)*

**clock**: How many turns it takes a threat to kill the opponent. 'Murktide Regent is a 3-turn clock.' Also used as a verb: 'I need to clock them before they assemble the combo.' *(See also: threat, finisher, goldfish.)*

**color screw**: Having enough lands but not the right colors of mana. Less common in Legacy due to dual lands and fetch lands, but Blood Moon and Wasteland can create color screw. *(Related cards: Blood Moon, Wasteland. See also: screw, flood.)*

**color screw vs color flood**: Color screw: right number of lands but wrong colors. Color flood: drawing too many lands of one color you don't need. Legacy's manabase of duals + fetches minimizes color issues but Wasteland/Blood Moon create them artificially. *(See also: screw, flood, Blood Moon.)*

**combat trick**: An instant-speed effect that changes the outcome of combat, such as a pump spell or protection effect. Less common in Legacy than other formats, but Berserk in Infect is a notable combat trick. *(Related cards: Berserk. See also: Infect, pump.)*

**crack**: To activate and sacrifice a fetch land (or any permanent with a sacrifice ability). 'Crack the fetch in response to Brainstorm' is a fundamental Legacy play that lets you shuffle away unwanted cards. *(Related cards: Flooded Strand, Polluted Delta, Misty Rainforest, Scalding Tarn, Verdant Catacombs, Windswept Heath, Wooded Foothills, Bloodstained Mire, Marsh Flats, Arid Mesa. See also: fetch land, brainstorm, shuffle effect.)*

**curve out**: Playing a spell on each successive turn that uses all available mana efficiently. 'They curved out perfectly: turn 1 Delver, turn 2 Daze your spell, turn 3 flip Delver and hold up Spell Pierce.' Indicates an ideal draw sequence. *(See also: mana curve, tempo, nut draw.)*

**dead card**: A card that has no useful application in the current game state. Similar to 'blank' but can also mean a card that was relevant but no longer is. 'My removal is dead against their creatureless deck.' *(See also: blank, brick.)*

**delve**: A keyword that lets you exile cards from your graveyard to reduce a spell's mana cost. In Legacy, Murktide Regent and Gurmag Angler use delve to become extremely cheap large creatures. Treasure Cruise and Dig Through Time (both banned) were too-powerful delve spells. *(Related cards: Murktide Regent, Gurmag Angler. See also: Murktide.)*

**dork**: Short for mana dork. A creature that produces mana, typically a 1-mana 0/1 or 1/1. In Legacy, notable mana dorks include Noble Hierarch, Deathrite Shaman (now banned), and Birds of Paradise. *(Related cards: Noble Hierarch, Birds of Paradise, Deathrite Shaman, Elvish Mystic. See also: ramp, rock, bolt the bird.)*

**dredge mechanic**: A keyword ability that lets you skip your draw and instead mill N cards to return the dredge card from graveyard to hand. Dredge 6 (Golgari Grave-Troll) mills 6 cards. Enables graveyard strategies by filling the yard rapidly. *(Related cards: Golgari Grave-Troll, Stinkweed Imp. See also: Dredge, graveyard.)*

**durdle**: To play passively and inefficiently, doing things that don't meaningfully advance your game plan. 'Stop durdling and start attacking.' A durdly deck takes too long to win. *(See also: grind, tempo.)*

**edict**: An effect that forces the opponent to sacrifice a creature (named after Diabolic Edict). Gets around hexproof, shroud, and protection. Useful against True-Name Nemesis and Marit Lage in Legacy. *(Related cards: Diabolic Edict, Liliana's Triumph, Liliana of the Veil. See also: removal, sacrifice.)*

**equipment**: Artifact cards that attach to creatures for bonuses. In Legacy, equipment matters primarily through Stoneforge Mystic finding Batterskull or Kaldra Compleat. Umezawa's Jitte was historically dominant. *(Related cards: Batterskull, Kaldra Compleat, Umezawa's Jitte. See also: SFM, Stoneblade.)*

**ETB**: Enter the Battlefield. Refers to triggered abilities that happen when a permanent enters play. 'ETB effect' or 'ETB trigger.' Stoneforge Mystic has an ETB that tutors for equipment. Karakas can replay ETB legends. *(See also: trigger.)*

**exile**: A zone where cards are removed from the game. Exile-based removal (Swords to Plowshares) is stronger than destroy effects because exiled cards can't be recurred from the graveyard. 'Exile it' is preferred over 'remove it' in current terminology. *(See also: removal, swords.)*

**fateseal**: Looking at the top card(s) of an opponent's library and choosing to put them on top or bottom. Jace the Mind Sculptor's +2 ability fateseals. Repeatedly fatesealing an opponent with Jace usually wins the game. *(Related cards: Jace, the Mind Sculptor. See also: jace, lock.)*

**fizzle**: When a spell or ability fails to resolve because all its targets become illegal. Distinct from being countered. 'My Swords to Plowshares fizzled because they sacrificed the creature in response.' *(See also: resolve, whiff.)*

**flash**: A keyword that lets you cast a spell at instant speed. Creatures with flash (like Orcish Bowmasters, Endurance) are powerful in Legacy because they dodge sorcery-speed removal and can be deployed in response to opponent's plays. *(Related cards: Orcish Bowmasters, Endurance, Snapcaster Mage.)*

**flicker**: To exile a permanent and immediately return it to the battlefield, re-triggering ETB abilities. Ephemerate flickers a creature for W. Used with Grief for double discard, or with Stoneforge Mystic for double equipment search. *(Related cards: Ephemerate. See also: ETB, Scam.)*

**flip**: When a transform card changes to its other side. 'Flip the Delver' means revealing an instant/sorcery to transform Delver of Secrets into Insectile Aberration. Also used for miracle: 'flip the Terminus.' *(Related cards: Delver of Secrets. See also: delver, transform.)*

**flood**: Drawing too many lands and not enough spells. Mana flood is one of the two classic ways to lose in Magic (the other being mana screw). Legacy's cantrips help mitigate flood. *(See also: screw, brick, cantrip.)*

**gas**: High-quality spells that advance your game plan. The opposite of lands or dead draws. 'I need to draw gas to win this game.' A hand full of gas is a powerful hand of action spells. *(See also: brick, flood, action.)*

**go off**: To execute a combo, especially a long or complex one. 'The Storm player went off on turn 2 and killed me.' Implies a dramatic sequence of plays leading to a win. *(See also: combo, storm, goldfish.)*

**going off**: Executing a combo sequence. Synonym for 'go off.' 'They're going off with Storm' means the Storm player is casting their combo sequence of spells. *(See also: go off, combo.)*

**goldfish**: To play solitaire against a non-interactive opponent (a goldfish). Used to test how fast a deck can win without opposition. 'Storm can goldfish a turn 2 kill.' Goldfishing reveals a deck's best-case speed. *(See also: clock, go off, combo.)*

**graveyard**: The discard pile zone in Magic. In Legacy, the graveyard is essentially a second hand due to cards like Reanimate, Life from the Loam, Past in Flames, and dredge. Graveyard hate is essential in Legacy sideboards. *(See also: graveyard hate, Reanimator, Dredge.)*

**grind**: To play a long, attrition-based game where both players trade resources until one runs out. 'This matchup is a grind.' Grindy decks win by out-valuing the opponent over many turns. *(See also: value, attrition, midrange.)*

**hate**: Cards specifically included to beat a particular strategy. 'Graveyard hate' means cards like Leyline of the Void or Rest in Peace. 'I have hate for Reanimator in my sideboard.' *(See also: hose, silver bullet, sideboard, tech.)*

**hellbent**: Having no cards in hand. Also a keyword mechanic. In Legacy, Infernal Tutor becomes Demonic Tutor when hellbent, which is why Storm decks use Lion's Eye Diamond (discard hand) before resolving the tutor. *(Related cards: Infernal Tutor, Lion's Eye Diamond. See also: ANT, LED.)*

**hold up**: To leave mana untapped (usually for countermagic or instant-speed removal). 'Hold up Daze' means keeping an Island untapped to bounce. 'Holding up Force' means keeping a blue card in hand alongside Force of Will. *(See also: tap out, permission.)*

**hose**: To completely shut down a strategy with a specific card. 'Rest in Peace hoses Reanimator.' A hosing card makes a matchup nearly unwinnable for the opponent. *(See also: hate, silver bullet.)*

**infinite**: A combo loop that can be repeated unlimited times. In Legacy: Food Chain + Misthollow Griffin for infinite creature mana, Helm of Obedience + Rest in Peace for infinite mill. Technically 'arbitrarily large' rather than actually infinite. *(See also: combo, loop.)*

**jank**: A card or deck that is considered bad, unreliable, or overly cute. 'That combo is total jank.' Can be used affectionately for fun-but-uncompetitive strategies. *(See also: brew, spice.)*

**keep**: The decision to keep your opening hand rather than mulligan. 'I'll keep' or 'it's a keep on the play.' Knowing what constitutes a keepable hand varies by deck and matchup. *(See also: mull, nut draw.)*

**legend rule**: A rule that says if a player controls two legendary permanents with the same name, they must sacrifice one. In Legacy, relevant for Karakas (can bounce your own legend to replay ETB) and the Dark Depths + Thespian's Stage combo. *(Related cards: Karakas, Dark Depths, Thespian's Stage. See also: Karakas, Dark Depths combo.)*

**loop**: A repeatable sequence of plays. If a loop creates a net positive of resources or damage, it usually wins. Legacy combos often involve deterministic loops (can't be disrupted once started) or non-deterministic loops (require luck). *(See also: infinite, combo.)*

**lord**: A creature that gives +1/+1 or other bonuses to all creatures of a specific type. Lord of Atlantis gives all Merfolk +1/+1 and islandwalk. Lords are central to tribal strategies. *(Related cards: Lord of Atlantis, Elvish Archdruid. See also: tribal.)*

**mainboard**: The primary 60-card deck, as opposed to the sideboard. Also called 'maindeck.' 'Mainboard Pyroblast' means putting Pyroblast in the main 60 rather than sideboard, reflecting a blue-heavy metagame. *(See also: sideboard.)*

**metalcraft**: Having 3 or more artifacts on the battlefield. A keyword mechanic. Relevant in Legacy for 8-Cast decks where Mox Opal requires metalcraft and artifact synergies reward artifact count. *(Related cards: Mox Opal. See also: 8-Cast.)*

**mill**: To put cards from a library into the graveyard. Named after Millstone. In Legacy, Brain Freeze (storm) and Painter's Servant + Grindstone are the main mill win conditions. *(Related cards: Brain Freeze, Grindstone. See also: Storm, Painter.)*

**mise**: To get lucky, especially by topdecking the perfect card. 'I mised the Terminus on top.' Originally from 'might as well' (mise well). Can also be a noun: 'that was a mise.' *(See also: topdeck, nut draw, rip.)*

**misplay**: Making an incorrect play that reduces your chances of winning. Less harsh than 'punt.' 'I misplayed by fetching the wrong land' or 'not playing around Wasteland was a misplay.' *(See also: punt, play around.)*

**mull**: Short for mulligan. To send your opening hand back and draw one fewer card. 'I had to mull to five' means the player mulliganed twice, going from 7 to 6 to 5 cards. The London Mulligan rule lets you draw 7 each time but put cards on the bottom. *(See also: keep, nut draw, screw, flood.)*

**netdeck**: To copy a successful decklist from the internet rather than building your own. Sometimes used pejoratively, but in competitive Legacy, netdecking is often the smart choice given the format's high card quality bar. *(See also: brew, meta, staple.)*

**nut draw**: The absolute best possible opening hand and sequence of draws. 'They had the nut draw: turn 1 Entomb, turn 2 Reanimate Griselbrand.' Also called 'the nuts.' *(See also: mise, topdeck, curve out.)*

**on the draw**: Going second in a game (drawing an extra card but acting a turn behind). Being on the draw gives card advantage but loses tempo. Some Legacy decks (like control) prefer the draw for the extra card. *(See also: on the play, card advantage.)*

**on the play**: Going first in a game (taking the first turn but not drawing a card on turn 1). Being on the play is a significant advantage in Legacy because it means deploying threats and disruption before the opponent. *(See also: on the draw, tempo.)*

**peel**: To draw a card, especially off the top of the library. 'Peel the Brainstorm.' Implies dramatic tension about what you'll draw. *(See also: rip, topdeck.)*

**pitch**: To discard or exile a card from hand as an alternate cost to cast a spell. Legacy's pitch spells (Force of Will, Force of Negation, Grief, Solitude) are format-defining because they can be cast without mana. *(Related cards: Force of Will, Force of Negation, Grief, Solitude, Fury, Endurance, Subtlety. See also: free spell, pitch spell, FoW, force.)*

**planeswalker**: A permanent card type representing powerful allies. In Legacy, key planeswalkers include Jace the Mind Sculptor, Wrenn and Six, Teferi Time Raveler, Narset Parter of Veils, and Liliana of the Veil. Can be attacked by creatures. *(See also: jace, W6, Teferi, Narset.)*

**prowess**: A keyword ability that gives a creature +1/+1 until end of turn whenever you cast a noncreature spell. Monastery Mentor creates prowess tokens. Synergizes with Legacy's cantrip-dense game plans. *(Related cards: Monastery Mentor, Soul-Scar Mage. See also: cantrip.)*

**pump**: To increase a creature's power/toughness. Pump spells are central to Infect (Invigorate, Berserk) and can be temporary (instant/sorcery) or permanent (counters, equipment). *(Related cards: Invigorate, Berserk. See also: Infect, combat trick.)*

**punish**: To exploit a mistake or suboptimal play by the opponent. 'Punish them for tapping out by resolving Show and Tell.' Legacy rewards tight play and punishes errors harshly. *(See also: blow out, punt.)*

**punt**: To make a significant gameplay mistake that costs you the game. 'I punted by not playing around Daze.' Named after punting a football (giving up possession). Common in Legacy due to the format's complexity. *(See also: misplay.)*

**ramp**: To accelerate your mana production beyond one land per turn, using mana creatures, mana rocks, or extra land effects. In Legacy, Sol lands (Ancient Tomb, City of Traitors) and Mox Diamond provide ramp. *(Related cards: Ancient Tomb, City of Traitors, Mox Diamond. See also: dork, rock, sol land.)*

**resolve**: When a spell or ability successfully completes without being countered. 'Does it resolve?' is the question asked when putting a spell on the stack. A resolved threat is one that made it past countermagic. *(See also: the stack, counter, fizzle.)*

**rip**: To draw a card, especially one you needed. 'Rip the Force off the top.' Synonym for topdeck. Used colloquially when peeling cards dramatically. *(See also: topdeck, mise, peel.)*

**ritual**: A spell that generates more mana than it costs, enabling explosive turns. Named after Dark Ritual (B for BBB). Legacy rituals include Dark Ritual, Cabal Ritual, Rite of Flame, and Lion's Eye Diamond. Essential for Storm decks. *(Related cards: Dark Ritual, Cabal Ritual, Rite of Flame, Lion's Eye Diamond. See also: Storm, ramp, go off.)*

**rock**: A mana-producing artifact (mana rock). In Legacy, examples include Mox Diamond, Chrome Mox, Lotus Petal, and Lion's Eye Diamond. Can also refer to 'The Rock' archetype (BG midrange). *(Related cards: Mox Diamond, Chrome Mox, Lotus Petal, Lion's Eye Diamond, Sol Ring. See also: dork, ramp, artifact mana.)*

**scoop**: To concede the game, physically picking up your cards from the table. 'I scooped to the Marit Lage token.' Can also mean conceding a match. *(See also: concede.)*

**screw**: Short for mana screw. Not drawing enough lands or the right colors of mana to cast your spells. Legacy's low mana curves and free spells make this less common but still devastating. *(See also: flood, mull, color screw.)*

**spice**: Unusual or unexpected card choices that make a deck unique. 'That deck has some real spice in it.' Implies creativity but may also imply the choices are suboptimal. *(See also: tech, brew, jank.)*

**splash**: Adding a small number of cards in an additional color to a deck. 'Splash red for Pyroblast' means adding red mana sources and Pyroblast to a primarily non-red deck. Legacy manabases easily support splashes via dual lands. *(See also: manabase, greedy manabase.)*

**staple**: A card that is a core component of many decks in the format. Brainstorm, Force of Will, and Wasteland are Legacy staples. Staples define the format's identity. *(Related cards: Brainstorm, Force of Will, Wasteland, Swords to Plowshares, Lightning Bolt. See also: format, meta.)*

**stick**: A permanent (usually a creature or planeswalker) that successfully resolves and stays on the battlefield. 'If Jace sticks, I win.' Implies surviving potential removal or countermagic. *(See also: resolve, threat, sticks.)*

**stone cold**: Completely dead to something with no outs. 'I was stone cold dead to a resolved Emrakul.' Means there is absolutely nothing in your deck that can save you. *(See also: dead, no outs.)*

**suspend**: A mechanic that lets you exile a card and put time counters on it, casting it for free when the last counter is removed. In Legacy, Ancestral Vision (suspend 4) and Rift Bolt (suspend 1) are notable suspend cards. *(Related cards: Ancestral Vision, Rift Bolt.)*

**sweeper**: A spell that removes all or most creatures from the battlefield. Synonym for board wipe or wrath. Legacy sweepers include Terminus, Toxic Deluge, and Supreme Verdict. *(Related cards: Terminus, Toxic Deluge, Supreme Verdict. See also: wrath, board wipe.)*

**tap out**: To use all your mana on your turn, leaving nothing for instant-speed responses. 'Tapping out against Storm is dangerous.' In Legacy, knowing when to tap out vs hold up mana is a critical skill. *(See also: draw-go, permission.)*

**tech**: An innovative or unexpected card choice, usually in a sideboard, designed to beat a specific metagame threat. 'That one-of Pyroblast in the main is great tech against Murktide.' *(See also: spice, silver bullet, meta.)*

**threshold**: Having 7 or more cards in your graveyard. A keyword mechanic. Nimble Mongoose becomes a 3/3 with shroud at threshold, which is why the RUG Delver archetype was called 'Canadian Threshold.' Cabal Ritual makes BBBBB at threshold. *(Related cards: Nimble Mongoose, Cabal Ritual. See also: Canadian Threshold, RUG Delver.)*

**token**: A creature or permanent created by a spell or ability that doesn't correspond to a card. Monastery Mentor makes monk tokens, Young Pyromancer makes elemental tokens, Marit Lage is a token. Tokens cease to exist when they leave the battlefield. *(See also: Young Pyromancer, Monastery Mentor.)*

**top of library**: The top card(s) of your library. In Legacy, manipulating the top of library is essential: Brainstorm, Ponder, and Sensei's Divining Top all interact with it. Miracle cards reward knowing what's on top. *(See also: brainstorm, Ponder, miracle.)*

**topdeck**: Drawing exactly the card you need off the top of your library, especially when you're behind or out of other options. 'I topdecked the Force of Will to stop the combo.' Can be a noun or verb. *(See also: mise, nut draw, rip.)*

**transform**: When a double-faced card flips to its other side. Delver of Secrets transforms into Insectile Aberration. The Initiative creatures transform when taking the initiative. *(Related cards: Delver of Secrets. See also: flip, delver.)*

**tuck**: To put a card on the bottom of its owner's library. Terminus tucks all creatures. Jace the Mind Sculptor can tuck a creature with its -1 ability. Tucking avoids graveyard recursion and death triggers. *(Related cards: Terminus, Jace, the Mind Sculptor. See also: exile, Terminus.)*

**tutor**: A card that searches your library for a specific card. Named after the original Demonic Tutor. In Legacy, tutors include Entomb, Green Sun's Zenith, Natural Order, and Infernal Tutor. Tutors enable toolbox strategies and combo consistency. *(Related cards: Demonic Tutor, Entomb, Green Sun's Zenith, Natural Order, Infernal Tutor. See also: toolbox, silver bullet.)*

**wheel**: An effect that makes all players discard their hands and draw new cards, named after Wheel of Fortune. Also refers to a card coming back around the table in a draft. *(Related cards: Wheel of Fortune, Echo of Eons. See also: gas, refuel.)*

**whiff**: To fail to find what you need with a search or selection effect. 'I whiffed on the Collected Company' or 'My Goblin Charbelcher whiffed on a land.' In Legacy, whiffing on a Brainstorm means not finding useful cards. *(See also: brick, fizzle.)*

**wrath**: Any board-clearing spell that destroys all creatures, named after Wrath of God. Used as a verb: 'I wrathed the board.' In Legacy, Terminus is the most common wrath effect due to its miracle cost. *(Related cards: Wrath of God, Terminus, Supreme Verdict, Toxic Deluge. See also: sweeper, board wipe.)*

## Card Nicknames

**Abrupt Decay**: A 2-mana BG instant that destroys any nonland permanent with mana value 3 or less and cannot be countered. Premium removal in Legacy BG(x) decks because it gets around countermagic. *(Related cards: Abrupt Decay. See also: removal, uncounterable.)*

**Ad Nauseam**: A 5-mana instant that lets you reveal cards and put them in hand, losing life equal to each card's mana value. You choose when to stop. In ANT, you use this to draw your entire deck at low cost (most spells cost 0-2). *(Related cards: Ad Nauseam. See also: ANT, Storm.)*

**Archon**: Archon of Cruelty. A 7-mana creature with a devastating ETB: opponent discards, sacrifices, loses 3 life; you draw, gain 3 life. A Reanimator target that provides immediate value even if removed. *(Related cards: Archon of Cruelty. See also: Reanimator.)*

**Atraxa**: Atraxa, Grand Unifier. A 7-mana legendary creature that draws a large number of cards on ETB (revealing top 10, keeping one of each card type). A premier reanimation target in recent Legacy Reanimator builds alongside Griselbrand. *(Related cards: Atraxa, Grand Unifier. See also: Reanimator.)*

**Batterskull**: A 5-mana living weapon Equipment that creates a 4/4 vigilance lifelink token. Typically cheated into play with Stoneforge Mystic on turn 3. Can bounce itself to hand and be redeployed. *(Related cards: Batterskull, Stoneforge Mystic. See also: SFM, Stoneblade, equipment.)*

**Beans**: Beseech the Mirror. A 4-mana sorcery with bargain that can tutor any card and cast it for free if you sacrifice a permanent. Used as a combo enabler in Legacy Storm variants and other combo strategies. *(Related cards: Beseech the Mirror. See also: tutor, storm.)*

**Berserk**: A 1-mana green instant that doubles a creature's power but destroys it at end of turn. In Legacy Infect, this is lethal with even a small infect creature: a 1/1 with Berserk and any pump is often 10+ poison. *(Related cards: Berserk. See also: Infect, pump.)*

**bob**: Dark Confidant, nicknamed after Bob Maher who designed the card as an Invitational winner. Bob's ability reveals the top card of your library each upkeep, losing life equal to its mana cost but gaining card advantage. *(Related cards: Dark Confidant. See also: card advantage.)*

**Bowmasters**: Orcish Bowmasters. A 2-mana flash creature from Lord of the Rings that deals damage whenever an opponent draws a card beyond their first each turn. Devastates cantrip-heavy Legacy strategies and creates amass tokens. Format-warping card. *(Related cards: Orcish Bowmasters. See also: cantrip, flash.)*

**brainstorm**: The most-played card in Legacy. Draw 3, put 2 back. Used as a verb: 'brainstorm in response.' The power of Brainstorm comes from combining it with fetch lands to shuffle away the unwanted cards. 'Brainstorm lock' occurs when you're stuck with bad cards on top without a shuffle effect. *(Related cards: Brainstorm. See also: cantrip, fetch land, brainstorm lock.)*

**Carpet of Flowers**: A 1-mana green enchantment that adds mana during your main phase based on the number of Islands the opponent controls. A Legacy sideboard all-star against blue decks, which always control Islands (fetch for dual). *(Related cards: Carpet of Flowers. See also: sideboard, ramp.)*

**Cavern of Souls**: A land that names a creature type and produces mana that makes creatures of that type uncounterable. Used in tribal decks (Elves, Goblins, Merfolk) and Reanimator (naming Demon for Griselbrand). *(Related cards: Cavern of Souls. See also: uncounterable, tribal.)*

**Chalice**: Chalice of the Void. A XX-cost artifact that counters all spells with mana value equal to X. 'Chalice on 1' shuts down Brainstorm, Ponder, Swords to Plowshares, Delver, and most of Legacy's cantrips. *(Related cards: Chalice of the Void. See also: Chalice on 1, prison, stompy.)*

**Collector Ouphe**: A 2-mana green creature that prevents activated abilities of artifacts. Shuts down Aether Vial, Batterskull, Lion's Eye Diamond, and equipment. Searchable via Green Sun's Zenith. A key hatebear in Legacy. *(Related cards: Collector Ouphe. See also: hatebear, GSZ, silver bullet.)*

**Counterbalance**: A 2-mana enchantment that reveals the top card of your library to counter spells with the same mana value. Was a dominant Legacy strategy paired with Sensei's Divining Top (now banned). Still sometimes played with Brainstorm. *(Related cards: Counterbalance, Sensei's Divining Top. See also: Top, soft lock.)*

**Cradle**: Gaea's Cradle. A legendary land that taps for green mana equal to the number of creatures you control. The engine of Legacy Elves, producing explosive amounts of mana. On the Reserved List and extremely expensive. *(Related cards: Gaea's Cradle. See also: Elves, reserved list.)*

**Crop Rotation**: A 1-mana green instant that sacrifices a land to search for any land. At instant speed in Legacy, it finds Dark Depths, Thespian's Stage, Bojuka Bog, Karakas, or any utility land needed. A key card in Lands and Turbo Depths. *(Related cards: Crop Rotation. See also: Lands, Turbo Depths, toolbox.)*

**Dark Ritual**: A 1-mana black instant that adds BBB. The original and most iconic ritual spell. Enables turn-1 Hymn to Tourach, turn-1 Necropotence, or massive Storm turns. A Legacy black staple since 1993. *(Related cards: Dark Ritual. See also: ritual, Storm.)*

**Day's Undoing**: A 3-mana sorcery that shuffles all hands and graveyards into libraries and each player draws 7. Then the turn ends. Used in 8-Cast and Echo of Eons strategies to refuel while resetting the opponent's graveyard. *(Related cards: Day's Undoing. See also: wheel, 8-Cast.)*

**Daze**: A free counterspell that costs returning an Island to your hand. The quintessential Legacy tempo card. 'Daze math' refers to calculating whether your opponent can pay 1 extra mana. Best on the play when the opponent taps out. *(Related cards: Daze. See also: Daze math, tempo, free spell.)*

**delver**: Delver of Secrets, a 1-mana 1/1 that transforms into a 3/2 flyer when you reveal an instant or sorcery on top of your library. Also refers to the entire Delver archetype family (UR, RUG, Grixis, etc.). The most iconic Legacy creature. *(Related cards: Delver of Secrets. See also: Delver archetype, tempo, flip.)*

**Depths**: Dark Depths. A legendary land that enters with 10 ice counters. When the last counter is removed, it transforms into Marit Lage, a 20/20 flying indestructible token. Combined with Thespian's Stage to skip the counters entirely. *(Related cards: Dark Depths, Thespian's Stage, Marit Lage. See also: Turbo Depths, Dark Depths combo.)*

**DRS**: Deathrite Shaman. A 1-mana creature that could tap for mana, drain life, or gain life by exiling cards from graveyards. Was banned in Legacy in 2018 for being too versatile and ubiquitous. Still referenced frequently. *(Related cards: Deathrite Shaman. See also: dork, banned.)*

**Dryad Arbor**: A 0-mana green creature that is also a Forest land. Famously searchable by Green Sun's Zenith for X=0 as a turn-1 mana dork, or fetchable with a green fetch land. Has summoning sickness. *(Related cards: Dryad Arbor, Green Sun's Zenith. See also: GSZ, dork.)*

**EI**: Expressive Iteration. A 2-mana UR sorcery that lets you look at 3 cards, put one in hand and one into exile to play this turn. Was banned in Legacy for providing too-efficient card advantage to Delver decks. Now often referenced historically. *(Related cards: Expressive Iteration. See also: card advantage, banned.)*

**Elvish Reclaimer**: A 1-mana green creature that can sacrifice a land to search for any land. In Turbo Depths, finds Dark Depths or Thespian's Stage. In Lands, finds utility lands. Also grows to 3/4 with 3+ card types in graveyard. *(Related cards: Elvish Reclaimer. See also: Turbo Depths, Lands.)*

**Emmy**: Emrakul, the Aeons Torn. A 15-mana legendary Eldrazi with annihilator 6, flying, protection from colored spells, and an extra turn on cast. The ultimate cheat-into-play target for Show and Tell and Sneak Attack. *(Related cards: Emrakul, the Aeons Torn. See also: Sneak and Show, SnT.)*

**Endurance**: A 3-mana green Incarnation with flash and reach. Can be pitched (exile a green card) to put a graveyard back into its owner's library. Versatile graveyard hate that also serves as a decent body. *(Related cards: Endurance. See also: pitch, graveyard hate, Elemental Incarnation.)*

**Ensnaring Bridge**: A 3-mana artifact that prevents creatures with power greater than the number of cards in your hand from attacking. A prison piece that locks out creature-based strategies when you empty your hand. *(Related cards: Ensnaring Bridge. See also: prison.)*

**Entomb**: A 1-mana instant that searches your library for a card and puts it into your graveyard. The key enabler for Reanimator decks. 'Turn 1 Entomb for Griselbrand, turn 2 Reanimate' is the classic Legacy Reanimator line. *(Related cards: Entomb, Griselbrand, Reanimate. See also: Reanimator, graveyard.)*

**Ephemerate**: A 1-mana white instant that exiles a creature you control then returns it. Has rebound (free recast next upkeep). In Legacy, flickers Grief for 4 discards total, or Stoneforge Mystic for equipment value. *(Related cards: Ephemerate. See also: flicker, Scam, ETB.)*

**Exploration**: A 1-mana green enchantment that lets you play an additional land each turn. Key accelerant in the Lands archetype, enabling faster Wasteland locks and Dark Depths combos. *(Related cards: Exploration. See also: Lands, ramp.)*

**Flusterstorm**: A 1-mana counterspell with storm that targets instants or sorceries. Ideal for counter wars because each copy must be countered individually. The best anti-storm card and counter-war finisher in Legacy. *(Related cards: Flusterstorm. See also: counter war, storm, permission.)*

**FoN**: Abbreviation for Force of Negation. A pitch counterspell from Modern Horizons that counters noncreature spells for free on opponents' turns. Supplements Force of Will in Legacy as additional free interaction. *(Related cards: Force of Negation. See also: force, FoW, pitch.)*

**force**: Force of Will, the defining card of Legacy. A free counterspell that can be cast by exiling a blue card from hand and paying 1 life. Keeps combo decks in check and is the reason Legacy can support powerful strategies without being degenerate. *(Related cards: Force of Will. See also: FoW, pitch, free spell, counter war.)*

**FoW**: Abbreviation for Force of Will. The card that defines Legacy, allowing you to counter any spell for free by pitching a blue card. 'Do you have FoW?' is the eternal Legacy question. *(Related cards: Force of Will. See also: force, pitch, free spell.)*

**Fury**: A 5-mana red Incarnation with double strike. Can be pitched (exile a red card) to deal 4 damage divided among creatures/planeswalkers. Free board control that punishes creature strategies. *(Related cards: Fury. See also: pitch, removal.)*

**goyf**: Tarmogoyf, one of the most iconic creatures in Magic history. A 2-mana creature whose power and toughness equal the number of card types in all graveyards plus one. Was the dominant Legacy threat for years. *(Related cards: Tarmogoyf. See also: threat, beater.)*

**Grief**: A 4-mana black Incarnation with menace. Can be pitched (exile a black card) for a free Thoughtseize effect. Was combined with Reanimate/Ephemerate for oppressive turn-1 double-discard plays ('Scam'). *(Related cards: Grief. See also: pitch, hand attack, Scam.)*

**Griselbrand**: An 8-mana 7/7 flying lifelink demon that lets you draw 7 cards for 7 life. The premier reanimation target in Legacy because drawing 7-14 cards usually wins the game immediately. Also called 'Griseldaddy' or 'Grizzy B.' *(Related cards: Griselbrand. See also: Reanimator, Entomb.)*

**GSZ**: Green Sun's Zenith. A sorcery that searches for a green creature with mana value X or less and puts it onto the battlefield. A key toolbox card in Legacy that can find Dryad Arbor on turn 1 for acceleration or silver bullets later. *(Related cards: Green Sun's Zenith, Dryad Arbor. See also: toolbox, Maverick, Elves.)*

**Hoof**: Craterhoof Behemoth. The finisher of choice for Elves decks, typically found via Natural Order or Green Sun's Zenith. 'NO for Hoof' means cast Natural Order to find Craterhoof. *(Related cards: Craterhoof Behemoth. See also: Elves, NO.)*

**Hydroblast**: A 1-mana blue instant that can counter a red spell or destroy a red permanent. Also Blue Elemental Blast (functionally similar). Sideboard card against red decks. 'Hydro' for short. *(Related cards: Hydroblast, Blue Elemental Blast. See also: Pyroblast, sideboard.)*

**Hymn**: Hymn to Tourach. A 2-mana sorcery that forces the opponent to discard 2 cards at random. The randomness makes it devastating because the opponent can't choose what to keep. A Legacy staple in black decks. *(Related cards: Hymn to Tourach. See also: hand attack, disruption.)*

**Invigorate**: A pump spell that can be cast for free (opponent gains 3 life) by revealing a green card. Key free pump spell in Legacy Infect, often providing the critical +4/+4 needed for a lethal poison attack. *(Related cards: Invigorate. See also: Infect, pump, free spell.)*

**jace**: Almost always refers to Jace, the Mind Sculptor (JTMS), the most powerful planeswalker ever printed. A Legacy staple that provides card selection (Brainstorm ability), bounce, and an ultimate that exiles the opponent's library. *(Related cards: Jace, the Mind Sculptor. See also: planeswalker, brainstorm, finisher.)*

**Jitte**: Umezawa's Jitte. A 2-mana equipment that gains charge counters when equipped creature deals combat damage. Counters can be used for +2/+2, -1/-1 to a creature, or 2 life gain. Historically one of the best equipment in Legacy. *(Related cards: Umezawa's Jitte. See also: equipment, SFM.)*

**Kaldra**: Kaldra Compleat. A living weapon Equipment that creates an indestructible 4/4 token with trample, haste, and first strike. Replaced Batterskull in many Stoneforge Mystic strategies for its haste and indestructibility. *(Related cards: Kaldra Compleat, Stoneforge Mystic. See also: SFM, Batterskull, Stoneblade.)*

**Karakas**: A legendary land that taps for white and can return any legendary creature to its owner's hand. Used defensively to bounce attacking Emrakul or Marit Lage, and offensively to replay your own ETB legendary creatures. *(Related cards: Karakas. See also: Karakas bounce, Death and Taxes.)*

**Knight of the Reliquary**: A creature that grows by exiling lands from your graveyard and can sacrifice a land to search for any land. A powerful toolbox creature in Maverick that finds utility lands and grows to enormous size. *(Related cards: Knight of the Reliquary. See also: Maverick, toolbox.)*

**LED**: Lion's Eye Diamond. A 0-mana artifact that sacrifices to discard your hand and add 3 mana of any color. A key combo piece in ANT (Storm), Dredge, and other graveyard strategies. On the Reserved List. *(Related cards: Lion's Eye Diamond. See also: storm, ANT, reserved list.)*

**Leyline of the Void**: A 4-mana black enchantment that exiles cards going to the opponent's graveyard. Can start the game on the battlefield if in your opening hand. Premium graveyard hate in Legacy sideboards. *(Related cards: Leyline of the Void. See also: graveyard hate, sideboard.)*

**Liliana**: Usually refers to Liliana of the Veil in Legacy. A 3-mana planeswalker that forces both players to discard and forces opponents to sacrifice creatures. Can also refer to Liliana, the Last Hope. *(Related cards: Liliana of the Veil, Liliana, the Last Hope. See also: edict, planeswalker.)*

**Loam**: Life from the Loam. A 2-mana sorcery with dredge 3 that returns up to 3 lands from graveyard to hand. The engine of the Lands archetype and Loam strategies. Enables recurring Wasteland locks. *(Related cards: Life from the Loam. See also: Lands, dredge, Wasteland lock.)*

**Marit Lage**: A legendary 20/20 flying indestructible black Avatar token created by Dark Depths. Wins the game in 1 attack. Can be stopped by Karakas (bounces legendary), Swords to Plowshares (exiles), or edict effects. *(Related cards: Dark Depths, Thespian's Stage. See also: Depths, Karakas bounce.)*

**Maze**: Maze of Ith. A land that taps to untap an attacking creature and prevent all combat damage it deals. Used in Lands and various control strategies to neutralize attackers. Can also be used on your own creatures for untap tricks. *(Related cards: Maze of Ith. See also: Lands.)*

**Minsc and Boo**: Minsc & Boo, Timeless Heroes. A 4-mana RG planeswalker that creates Boo (a hamster token) and can fling it at opponents. Provides a threat, removal, and card advantage. Sees play in Legacy RUG and Temur builds. *(Related cards: Minsc & Boo, Timeless Heroes. See also: planeswalker.)*

**Mom**: Mother of Runes. A 1-mana white creature that taps to give another creature protection from a chosen color. A cornerstone of Death and Taxes, protecting threats from removal. Also called 'Mother.' *(Related cards: Mother of Runes, Giver of Runes. See also: Death and Taxes, protection.)*

**Monastery Mentor**: A 3-mana white creature that creates 1/1 prowess monk tokens whenever you cast noncreature spells. A powerful finisher in Legacy control decks that can quickly build an army using cheap cantrips. *(Related cards: Monastery Mentor. See also: finisher, token.)*

**Murktide**: Murktide Regent. A large flying threat that costs less for each instant/sorcery exiled from your graveyard. Often a 2-mana 6/6+ flyer in Legacy Delver decks. Also shorthand for UR Murktide Delver. *(Related cards: Murktide Regent. See also: Delver, threat, clock.)*

**Narset**: Narset, Parter of Veils. A 3-mana planeswalker that prevents opponents from drawing more than one card per turn and digs for noncreature, nonland cards. Shuts down Brainstorm's draw-3 and other card advantage. *(Related cards: Narset, Parter of Veils. See also: planeswalker, hate.)*

**NO**: Natural Order. A 4-mana sorcery that sacrifices a green creature to search for any green creature and put it onto the battlefield. Used in Elves and Maverick to find Craterhoof Behemoth or Progenitus. *(Related cards: Natural Order, Craterhoof Behemoth, Progenitus. See also: Elves, Maverick, tutor.)*

**Omniscience**: A 10-mana enchantment that lets you cast spells from hand for free. Cheated into play via Show and Tell in Legacy. Once resolved, cast Emrakul and any other threat for free. Also called 'Omni.' *(Related cards: Omniscience, Show and Tell. See also: Sneak and Show, SnT.)*

**Painter**: Painter's Servant, or the Painter archetype. The combo involves Painter's Servant (naming a color) + Grindstone to mill the opponent's entire library since every card becomes that color. A fast Legacy combo deck. *(Related cards: Painter's Servant, Grindstone. See also: Painter archetype, combo.)*

**Past in Flames**: A 4-mana sorcery that gives all instants and sorceries in your graveyard flashback. A key Storm card that lets you replay all your rituals and tutors for a second time to build storm count. *(Related cards: Past in Flames. See also: Storm, ANT.)*

**path**: Path to Exile. A 1-mana white instant that exiles a creature. Less common in Legacy than Swords to Plowshares because giving the opponent a land is a bigger downside than giving them life in a format with Wasteland. *(Related cards: Path to Exile. See also: swords, removal.)*

**Pithing Needle**: A 1-mana artifact that names a card and prevents its activated abilities from being used. Searchable by Urza's Saga. Names common targets include Wasteland, Thespian's Stage, Aether Vial, and planeswalkers. *(Related cards: Pithing Needle. See also: silver bullet, Urza's Saga.)*

**plow**: Swords to Plowshares. 'Plow your Murktide' means exile it with Swords to Plowshares. *(Related cards: Swords to Plowshares. See also: swords.)*

**Ponder**: A 1-mana sorcery that lets you look at the top 3 cards, rearrange or shuffle them, then draw. One of Legacy's premier cantrips alongside Brainstorm. Provides card selection and fuels Delver flips. *(Related cards: Ponder. See also: cantrip, brainstorm, card selection.)*

**Preordain**: A 1-mana sorcery that scries 2 then draws a card. The third-best cantrip in Legacy after Brainstorm and Ponder. Better at digging deep (can bottom both cards) but sees fewer total cards. *(Related cards: Preordain. See also: cantrip, brainstorm, Ponder.)*

**Price of Progress**: A 2-mana red instant that deals 2 damage per nonbasic land the opponent controls. Devastating in Legacy where most players control 3-4 nonbasics by mid-game. A Burn staple that can deal 6-10 damage. *(Related cards: Price of Progress. See also: Burn, greedy manabase.)*

**Prismatic Ending**: A sorcery that exiles a nonland permanent with mana value X or less, where X is the number of colors spent. Flexible removal in Legacy multicolor decks. Handles problematic permanents that dodge other removal. *(Related cards: Prismatic Ending. See also: removal.)*

**Pyroblast**: A 1-mana red instant that can counter a blue spell or destroy a blue permanent. One of the most important Legacy sideboard cards. Also Red Elemental Blast (functionally similar). 'Pyro' for short. *(Related cards: Pyroblast, Red Elemental Blast. See also: Hydroblast, sideboard.)*

**Rest in Peace**: A 2-mana white enchantment that exiles all cards in graveyards and exiles any card that would go to a graveyard. The most thorough graveyard hate in Legacy. Combines with Helm of Obedience for an instant kill. *(Related cards: Rest in Peace. See also: graveyard hate, Helm combo.)*

**Retrofitter Foundry**: A 1-mana artifact that creates tokens and upgrades them. Searchable by Urza's Saga. Provides a recursive threat that dodges most removal. A key card in 8-Cast and Death and Taxes with Saga. *(Related cards: Retrofitter Foundry, Urza's Saga. See also: Urza's Saga, 8-Cast.)*

**S&T**: Show and Tell. Alternative abbreviation. See SnT. *(Related cards: Show and Tell. See also: SnT, Sneak and Show.)*

**SFM**: Stoneforge Mystic. A 2-mana creature that searches for an Equipment card and can cheat it onto the battlefield. Typically fetches Batterskull or Kaldra Compleat. Defines the Stoneblade archetype in Legacy. *(Related cards: Stoneforge Mystic, Batterskull, Kaldra Compleat. See also: Stoneblade, equipment.)*

**Snapcaster**: Snapcaster Mage. A 2-mana flash creature that gives an instant or sorcery in your graveyard flashback. Provides value by reusing cantrips, removal, or countermagic. Less dominant in Legacy than Modern due to Daze antisynergy. *(Related cards: Snapcaster Mage. See also: value, flashback.)*

**Sneak Attack**: A 4-mana red enchantment that lets you put any creature from hand onto the battlefield with haste for R. The creature is sacrificed at end of turn. Paired with Emrakul (gets an extra turn trigger on attack) for devastating effect. *(Related cards: Sneak Attack, Emrakul, the Aeons Torn. See also: Sneak and Show.)*

**SnT**: Show and Tell. A 3-mana sorcery that lets each player put any permanent from hand onto the battlefield. Legacy combo decks use it to cheat in Emrakul, the Aeons Torn or Omniscience. Also abbreviated S&T. *(Related cards: Show and Tell, Emrakul, the Aeons Torn, Omniscience. See also: Sneak and Show, combo.)*

**Solitude**: A 5-mana white Incarnation with flash and lifelink. Can be pitched (exile a white card) for a free Swords to Plowshares effect. Premium free removal in Legacy white decks. *(Related cards: Solitude. See also: pitch, swords, removal.)*

**Spell Pierce**: A 1-mana counterspell that counters noncreature spells unless the opponent pays 2. Effective in the early game when opponents are tapped out. Loses effectiveness as the game goes long. *(Related cards: Spell Pierce. See also: permission, tempo.)*

**Stifle**: A 1-mana instant that counters a triggered or activated ability. In Legacy, used to counter fetch land activations (denying mana), Storm triggers, and various combo abilities. A tempo play that can be backbreaking. *(Related cards: Stifle. See also: tempo, mana denial.)*

**Subtlety**: A 4-mana blue Incarnation with flash and flying. Can be pitched (exile a blue card) to put a creature or planeswalker spell on top of its owner's library. A tempo-oriented free counterspell for creatures. *(Related cards: Subtlety. See also: pitch, permission.)*

**Surgical**: Surgical Extraction. A Phyrexian mana spell (free at the cost of 2 life) that exiles all copies of a card from the opponent's graveyard, hand, and library. Key Legacy sideboard card against combo and Reanimator. *(Related cards: Surgical Extraction. See also: graveyard hate, free spell.)*

**swords**: Swords to Plowshares. The best single-target creature removal in Magic. 1 white mana, exile target creature, its controller gains life. A Legacy staple since the format's inception. Also called 'StP' or 'plow.' *(Related cards: Swords to Plowshares. See also: path, removal, plow.)*

**Tabernacle**: The Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale. A legendary land that forces all creatures to be paid for (1 mana each upkeep) or be destroyed. One of the most expensive cards in Legacy, used in Lands. On the Reserved List. *(Related cards: The Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale. See also: Lands, reserved list.)*

**Teferi**: In Legacy, usually refers to Teferi, Time Raveler: a 3-mana planeswalker that prevents opponents from casting at instant speed and bounces permanents. Shuts down countermagic and flash. Also called 'T3feri' or 'Teferi3.' *(Related cards: Teferi, Time Raveler. See also: planeswalker, control.)*

**Tendrils**: Tendrils of Agony. The primary Storm win condition: drain 2 life per copy (1 base + storm copies). With storm count 9, that's 10 copies for 20 damage. 'Going to Tendrils' means executing the Storm kill. *(Related cards: Tendrils of Agony. See also: Storm, ANT, storm count.)*

**Terminus**: A 6-mana (or miracle cost W) sorcery that puts all creatures on the bottom of their owners' libraries. The premier board wipe in Legacy because it gets around indestructible and graveyard recursion. Miracling it at instant speed via Brainstorm is a defining Legacy play. *(Related cards: Terminus. See also: miracle, wrath, brainstorm.)*

**Thalia**: Thalia, Guardian of Thraben. A 2-mana 2/1 first strike creature that makes noncreature spells cost 1 more. The defining hatebear of Legacy. 'Thalia tax' refers to the additional mana cost she imposes on cantrips, Force of Will, etc. *(Related cards: Thalia, Guardian of Thraben. See also: Death and Taxes, hatebear, Thalia tax.)*

**Thassa's Oracle**: A 2-mana blue creature with an ETB that wins the game if your devotion to blue is >= cards in library. The modern win condition for Doomsday (stack a pile with 0-2 cards left in library). Also wins in Oops All Spells. *(Related cards: Thassa's Oracle. See also: Doomsday, Oops All Spells, win condition.)*

**Thoughtseize**: A 1-mana sorcery that lets you look at the opponent's hand and take any nonland card for 2 life. The premier targeted discard spell in Legacy. Also used as a verb: 'Thoughtseize them on turn 1.' *(Related cards: Thoughtseize. See also: hand attack, disruption.)*

**TNN**: True-Name Nemesis. A 3-mana 3/1 Merfolk with protection from a chosen player, making it nearly impossible to interact with in combat or with targeted removal. A Legacy staple in blue tempo and Stoneblade decks. *(Related cards: True-Name Nemesis. See also: evasion, Stoneblade.)*

**Top**: Sensei's Divining Top. A 1-mana artifact that lets you look at and rearrange the top 3 cards of your library. Was banned in Legacy due to slow play concerns but was once paired with Counterbalance for a powerful soft lock. *(Related cards: Sensei's Divining Top. See also: Counterbalance, banned.)*

**Toxic Deluge**: A 3-mana black sorcery that gives all creatures -X/-X where X is life you pay. Scales perfectly to the board state and gets around indestructible. A premier black sweeper in Legacy. *(Related cards: Toxic Deluge. See also: wrath, sweeper.)*

**Treasure Cruise**: A sorcery that costs 8 but with delve often costs just U. Draw 3 cards. Banned in Legacy for being an absurdly efficient Ancestral Recall analog. One of the most powerful delve spells ever printed. *(Related cards: Treasure Cruise. See also: delve, banned.)*

**Trinisphere**: A 3-mana artifact that makes all spells cost at least 3 mana. Devastating against Legacy's low-cost spells. Turns Brainstorm into a 3-mana cantrip and makes free spells (Daze, Force of Will's alternate cost) cost 3. *(Related cards: Trinisphere. See also: prison, Sphere effects.)*

**Tropical Island**: The blue-green dual land. Used in RUG Delver, BUG strategies, Infect, and other UG decks. Often abbreviated 'Trop.' *(Related cards: Tropical Island. See also: dual lands.)*

**Tundra**: The white-blue dual land. Used in Stoneblade, Death and Taxes splashes, Miracles, and UW control decks. *(Related cards: Tundra. See also: dual lands.)*

**Underground Sea**: The blue-black dual land. One of the most played and expensive duals in Legacy, used in Delver, Storm, Reanimator, Doomsday, and virtually every UB deck. Often abbreviated 'USea.' *(Related cards: Underground Sea. See also: dual lands.)*

**Uro**: Uro, Titan of Nature's Wrath. A 3-mana creature with escape that gains 3 life, draws a card, and puts a land into play. A value engine that defined Legacy for a period. Provides life gain, card advantage, and ramp in one card. *(Related cards: Uro, Titan of Nature's Wrath. See also: value, midrange.)*

**Urza's Saga**: An enchantment land from MH2 that creates artifact tokens and tutors for 0-1 mana artifacts. A Legacy powerhouse in 8-Cast, Death and Taxes, and other artifact strategies. Finds silver bullets like Pithing Needle or Retrofitter Foundry. *(Related cards: Urza's Saga. See also: 8-Cast, toolbox.)*

**Veil**: Short for Veil of Summer. 'I Veiled their Thoughtseize' means countering the discard spell and drawing a card with Veil of Summer. *(Related cards: Veil of Summer. See also: Veil of Summer.)*

**Veil of Summer**: A 1-mana green instant that gives your spells hexproof from blue/black and draws a card if the opponent cast a blue/black spell this turn. Called 'green Cryptic Command' for its efficiency. A Legacy sideboard staple. *(Related cards: Veil of Summer. See also: protection, sideboard.)*

**Vial**: Aether Vial. A 1-mana artifact that gains charge counters and can tap to put a creature with that mana value from hand onto the battlefield. The backbone of Death and Taxes, Goblins, and Merfolk. Uncounterable creature deployment. *(Related cards: Aether Vial. See also: Death and Taxes, Goblins.)*

**Volcanic Island**: The blue-red dual land. Essential for UR Delver, Sneak and Show, and any UR-based deck. One of the most played duals. Often abbreviated 'Volc.' *(Related cards: Volcanic Island. See also: dual lands.)*

**W6**: Wrenn and Six. A 2-mana RG planeswalker that returns lands from graveyard to hand each turn. Enables recursive Wasteland, fixes mana with fetch lands, and pings small creatures. A format-defining Legacy card. *(Related cards: Wrenn and Six. See also: Wasteland lock, planeswalker, mana denial.)*

**wasteland**: Wasteland the card, or used as a verb: 'wasteland their dual' means destroy their nonbasic land. A defining Legacy card that punishes greedy manabases and enables tempo strategies. Pairs with Stifle to deny fetch lands. *(Related cards: Wasteland. See also: mana denial, Wasteland lock, tempo.)*

**Young Pyromancer**: A 2-mana red creature that creates 1/1 Elemental tokens whenever you cast an instant or sorcery. A Legacy staple in Delver and midrange decks. Called 'Young Peezy' or 'YP.' *(Related cards: Young Pyromancer. See also: token, tempo.)*

## Archetype Terms

**4c Control**: A four-color control deck (often without red or without white) that leverages the best cards from multiple colors. Uses Uro, planeswalkers, and blue cantrips/countermagic. Also called 'Czech Pile' in some configurations. *(Related cards: Uro, Titan of Nature's Wrath, Force of Will, Swords to Plowshares. See also: Czech Pile, control, midrange.)*

**8-Cast**: A Legacy artifact-based deck that plays many 0 and 1-mana artifacts with draw engines like Thought Monitor, Thoughtcast, and artifact synergy payoffs. Named for playing 8 'cast trigger' draw effects. Also called Affinity. *(Related cards: Thought Monitor, Thoughtcast, Emry, Lurker of the Loch, Urza's Saga. See also: artifacts, combo.)*

**Aluren**: A Legacy combo deck built around the enchantment Aluren, which lets all players cast creatures with mana value 3 or less for free. Wins by looping creatures with enter-the-battlefield abilities for infinite value. *(Related cards: Aluren, Recruiter of the Guard, Cavern Harpy, Acererak the Archlich. See also: combo, infinite.)*

**ANT**: Ad Nauseam Tendrils. A Legacy Storm combo deck that uses Ad Nauseam to draw many cards, then casts rituals and Tendrils of Agony with a high storm count. More consistent but slower than TES. *(Related cards: Ad Nauseam, Tendrils of Agony, Dark Ritual, Cabal Ritual, Lion's Eye Diamond, Infernal Tutor, Past in Flames. See also: Storm, TES, LED.)*

**Belcher**: Goblin Charbelcher combo deck. Runs 0-1 lands and uses rituals to cast Charbelcher, which deals damage equal to the number of cards revealed until a land is hit. With few/no lands, this is usually lethal. A glass cannon Legacy deck. *(Related cards: Goblin Charbelcher, Land Grant, Elvish Spirit Guide, Simian Spirit Guide. See also: glass cannon, all-in.)*

**BUG**: The color combination of black, blue, and green (also called Sultai). In Legacy, BUG decks combine the best of Thoughtseize, cantrips, countermagic, Abrupt Decay, and Tarmogoyf/Uro. *(Related cards: Thoughtseize, Abrupt Decay, Force of Will. See also: Sultai, midrange.)*

**Burn**: An aggressive Legacy deck that aims to deal 20 damage entirely with direct damage spells. Lightning Bolt, Chain Lightning, Lava Spike, Rift Bolt, Price of Progress. Budget-friendly but competitive, punishes greedy manabases with Price of Progress. *(Related cards: Lightning Bolt, Chain Lightning, Lava Spike, Rift Bolt, Price of Progress, Eidolon of the Great Revel. See also: aggro, bolt.)*

**Canadian Threshold**: The original name for RUG Delver, predating Delver of Secrets. Named after the Canadian players who pioneered the Nimble Mongoose + Stifle tempo strategy. Still used by Legacy veterans. *(Related cards: Nimble Mongoose, Stifle, Wasteland. See also: RUG Delver.)*

**Cephalid Breakfast**: A Legacy combo deck using Cephalid Illusionist + Nomads en-Kor to mill your entire library, then win with Narcomoeba triggers and Dread Return. Named after the Cephalid creature type. A fringe but long-standing Legacy combo. *(Related cards: Cephalid Illusionist, Nomads en-Kor, Narcomoeba, Dread Return. See also: combo, graveyard.)*

**Czech Pile**: A 4-color (typically non-white or non-red) control deck named after the Czech players who popularized it. Plays Leovold, Emissary of Trest, Kolaghan's Command, and a pile of the best cards in every color. Also called '4c Control.' *(Related cards: Leovold, Emissary of Trest, Kolaghan's Command. See also: 4c Control, midrange, control.)*

**D&T**: Abbreviation for Death and Taxes. A Legacy white creature deck using tax effects and hatebears. *(Related cards: Thalia, Guardian of Thraben, Aether Vial. See also: Death and Taxes.)*

**Dark Depths combo**: The combo of Dark Depths + Thespian's Stage. Stage copies Depths (entering with 0 counters since it's not being 'played'), immediately creates Marit Lage. A 2-card combo that produces a 20/20 flying indestructible token. *(Related cards: Dark Depths, Thespian's Stage. See also: Turbo Depths, Lands, Marit Lage.)*

**Death and Taxes**: A mono-white or white-splash creature deck built around Aether Vial, Thalia, Stoneforge Mystic, and various hatebears. Abbreviated D&T. Disrupts opponents through taxing effects, Wasteland, Rishadan Port, and Karakas. *(Related cards: Aether Vial, Thalia, Guardian of Thraben, Stoneforge Mystic, Rishadan Port, Karakas. See also: D&T, hatebear, Vial, Thalia.)*

**Delver archetype**: Any tempo deck built around Delver of Secrets. The dominant macro-archetype of Legacy. Variants include UR Delver (Murktide), RUG Delver (Canadian Threshold), Grixis Delver, and Jeskai Delver. All share: cheap threats, cantrips, Daze, Wasteland, Force of Will. *(Related cards: Delver of Secrets, Daze, Wasteland, Force of Will. See also: delver, tempo, RUG Delver, Grixis Delver.)*

**Doomsday**: A combo deck built around the card Doomsday, which pays half your life to search your library for 5 cards and stack them as your new library. Then uses cantrips to draw through the pile and win, typically with Thassa's Oracle. *(Related cards: Doomsday, Thassa's Oracle. See also: combo, Doomsday pile.)*

**Dredge**: A Legacy graveyard combo deck that uses the dredge mechanic to fill its graveyard without drawing cards, then generates value with Bridge from Below, Narcomoeba, Cabal Therapy, and Ichorid. Extremely fast but fragile to graveyard hate. *(Related cards: Golgari Grave-Troll, Stinkweed Imp, Bridge from Below, Narcomoeba, Ichorid, Cabal Therapy. See also: dredge mechanic, graveyard, LED.)*

**Elves**: A Legacy combo/aggro deck that generates explosive mana through Gaea's Cradle and elf synergies, then wins with Natural Order into Craterhoof Behemoth or by generating massive card advantage with Glimpse of Nature. *(Related cards: Gaea's Cradle, Natural Order, Craterhoof Behemoth, Glimpse of Nature, Heritage Druid, Nettle Sentinel. See also: Cradle, NO, combo.)*

**Enchantress**: A Legacy combo/prison deck built around enchantments. Uses Argothian Enchantress and Enchantress's Presence to draw cards whenever enchantments are cast. Wins with Destiny Spinner or by locking opponents under Solitary Confinement. *(Related cards: Argothian Enchantress, Enchantress's Presence, Solitary Confinement, Sterling Grove. See also: prison, combo.)*

**Food Chain**: A Legacy combo deck using Food Chain (exile a creature to add mana equal to its CMC+1, only for creatures) with Misthollow Griffin or Eternal Scourge (castable from exile) for infinite creature mana. Wins with Walking Ballista or similar. *(Related cards: Food Chain, Misthollow Griffin, Eternal Scourge, Walking Ballista. See also: combo, infinite.)*

**Goblins**: A Legacy tribal deck using Goblin Lackey, Aether Vial, and Goblin Matron/Ringleader for card advantage. Can deploy threats without casting them (bypassing Chalice). Plays a grindy, tutor-heavy game with tribal synergies. *(Related cards: Goblin Lackey, Aether Vial, Goblin Matron, Goblin Ringleader, Muxus, Goblin Grandee. See also: Vial, tribal.)*

**Grixis Delver**: A blue-black-red Delver tempo deck that adds black for Thoughtseize and removal. Historically played Young Pyromancer and Gurmag Angler alongside the standard Delver shell. *(Related cards: Delver of Secrets, Thoughtseize, Young Pyromancer, Gurmag Angler. See also: Delver archetype, tempo.)*

**Helm combo**: The combo of Helm of Obedience + Rest in Peace (or Leyline of the Void). Activate Helm to mill until a card goes to graveyard, but since cards are exiled instead, the entire library gets exiled. Opponent loses on draw step. *(Related cards: Helm of Obedience, Rest in Peace, Leyline of the Void. See also: combo, Rest in Peace.)*

**Hogaak**: A Legacy graveyard deck built around Hogaak, Arisen Necropolis, which can be cast from the graveyard by convoking and delving. Combines with Vengevine and Altar of Dementia for a powerful graveyard aggro/combo strategy. *(Related cards: Hogaak, Arisen Necropolis, Vengevine, Altar of Dementia. See also: graveyard, Dredge.)*

**Infect**: An aggro/combo deck that wins by dealing 10 poison counters using creatures with infect (Glistener Elf, Blighted Agent) enhanced by pump spells (Invigorate, Berserk). Can win as early as turn 2 in Legacy. *(Related cards: Glistener Elf, Blighted Agent, Invigorate, Berserk, Inkmoth Nexus. See also: aggro, combo, pump.)*

**Jeskai Control**: A blue-white-red control deck that combines blue cantrips and countermagic, white removal (Swords to Plowshares), and red blasts (Pyroblast) plus Expressive Iteration or similar. Uses Stoneforge Mystic or Monastery Mentor as finishers. *(Related cards: Force of Will, Swords to Plowshares, Pyroblast, Monastery Mentor. See also: control, Stoneblade.)*

**Lands**: A Legacy archetype that wins using its mana base as its primary engine. Uses Life from the Loam to recur Wasteland (mana denial), Dark Depths + Thespian's Stage (20/20 Marit Lage), Maze of Ith, and The Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale. *(Related cards: Life from the Loam, Dark Depths, Thespian's Stage, Wasteland, Maze of Ith, The Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale, Exploration. See also: Loam, Depths, Wasteland lock, Tabernacle.)*

**Maverick**: A GW(x) creature-based midrange deck built around Green Sun's Zenith, Knight of the Reliquary, and various silver-bullet creatures. A toolbox strategy that attacks from multiple angles while disrupting with Thalia and Wasteland. *(Related cards: Green Sun's Zenith, Knight of the Reliquary, Thalia, Guardian of Thraben, Noble Hierarch. See also: GSZ, toolbox, hatebear.)*

**Merfolk**: A tribal aggro deck using Merfolk lords (Lord of Atlantis, Master of the Pearl Trident) to pump a team of islandwalking creatures. Runs Aether Vial, Force of Will, and can make opponents' lands into Islands for evasion. *(Related cards: Lord of Atlantis, Master of the Pearl Trident, Aether Vial, True-Name Nemesis. See also: tribal, Vial, TNN.)*

**Mono Red Prison**: A Legacy deck using Sol lands to power out Chalice of the Void, Blood Moon, Trinisphere, and other lock pieces, backed by threats like Goblin Rabblemaster and Chandra. Punishes greedy manabases and cantrip-heavy strategies. *(Related cards: Chalice of the Void, Blood Moon, Trinisphere, Ancient Tomb, City of Traitors, Chrome Mox, Goblin Rabblemaster. See also: prison, stompy, Moon Stompy, Chalice on 1.)*

**Moon Stompy**: A variant of Mono Red Prison. Uses Sol lands and Chrome Mox to deploy Blood Moon and Chalice of the Void early, then beats down with Goblin Rabblemaster, Legion Warboss, or similar aggressive creatures. 'Moon' refers to Blood Moon. *(Related cards: Blood Moon, Chalice of the Void, Chrome Mox, Ancient Tomb. See also: Mono Red Prison, prison, stompy.)*

**Nic Fit**: A Legacy midrange/ramp deck that uses Veteran Explorer and Cabal Therapy to ramp into large threats. Named somewhat obscurely. Sacrifices Veteran Explorer to give both players basic lands, but the Nic Fit player benefits more by casting expensive bombs. *(Related cards: Veteran Explorer, Cabal Therapy, Green Sun's Zenith. See also: midrange, ramp.)*

**Omni-Tell**: A variant of Sneak and Show that focuses on Show and Tell + Omniscience rather than Sneak Attack. Once Omniscience is in play, casts free spells to win immediately. *(Related cards: Omniscience, Show and Tell. See also: Sneak and Show.)*

**Oops All Spells**: A Legacy combo deck that runs zero lands, using Spirit Guides, Lotus Petal, and Chrome Mox for mana. Uses Balustrade Spy or Undercity Informer to mill the entire library (no lands to stop), then wins with Dread Return and Thassa's Oracle. *(Related cards: Balustrade Spy, Undercity Informer, Dread Return, Thassa's Oracle. See also: combo, all-in.)*

**Reanimator**: A combo deck that puts large creatures into the graveyard (via Entomb, Careful Study, or Faithless Looting) then returns them to the battlefield with Reanimate, Exhume, or Animate Dead. Primary targets: Griselbrand, Atraxa, Archon of Cruelty. *(Related cards: Entomb, Reanimate, Exhume, Animate Dead, Griselbrand. See also: Entomb, Griselbrand, graveyard.)*

**RUG Delver**: A red-blue-green Delver tempo deck, also called Canadian Threshold or 'Canlander Delver.' Historically played Nimble Mongoose and Tarmogoyf alongside Delver with Stifle and Wasteland for mana denial. One of the original Delver builds. *(Related cards: Delver of Secrets, Nimble Mongoose, Tarmogoyf, Stifle, Wasteland, Daze. See also: Delver archetype, Canadian Threshold, tempo.)*

**Scam**: A strategy combining Grief or other Elementals with Reanimate or Ephemerate to double their enter-the-battlefield triggers on turn 1. 'Scam' because it feels unfair: turn 1 Grief, reanimate it, strip 2 cards and have a 3/2 menace. *(Related cards: Grief, Reanimate, Ephemerate. See also: Grief, pitch.)*

**Sneak and Show**: A Legacy combo deck that uses Sneak Attack and Show and Tell to put Emrakul, the Aeons Torn or Griselbrand onto the battlefield. One of the format's premier combo decks, combining redundant cheat effects with blue cantrips and Force of Will. *(Related cards: Sneak Attack, Show and Tell, Emrakul, the Aeons Torn, Griselbrand. See also: SnT, Emmy, combo.)*

**Stoneblade**: A midrange/control deck centered on Stoneforge Mystic and Equipment (Batterskull, Kaldra Compleat). Usually in UW or Esper colors with cantrips, countermagic, and removal. Aims to grind value while threatening with equipped creatures. *(Related cards: Stoneforge Mystic, Batterskull, Kaldra Compleat, Force of Will. See also: SFM, equipment, control.)*

**Storm**: A combo archetype that wins by casting many spells in one turn, then finishing with a storm spell (Tendrils of Agony for lethal drain, Brain Freeze for mill). ANT and TES are the primary Legacy Storm variants. *(Related cards: Tendrils of Agony, Brain Freeze, Dark Ritual, Lion's Eye Diamond. See also: ANT, TES, go off, storm count.)*

**Sultai**: The Tarkir name for the BUG (black-blue-green) color combination. Used interchangeably with BUG in Legacy contexts. Sultai midrange plays value creatures and disruption. *(See also: BUG.)*

**TES**: The Epic Storm. A faster, more explosive Legacy Storm variant compared to ANT. Runs Burning Wish, Rite of Flame, and Chrome Mox for speed. Can win on turn 1 more frequently but is less consistent. *(Related cards: Burning Wish, Rite of Flame, Chrome Mox, Lion's Eye Diamond, Tendrils of Agony. See also: Storm, ANT.)*

**Turbo Depths**: A dedicated Dark Depths + Thespian's Stage combo deck that aims to create Marit Lage as fast as possible. Runs Crop Rotation, Elvish Reclaimer, and Sylvan Scrying to find combo pieces, plus disruption like Thoughtseize. *(Related cards: Dark Depths, Thespian's Stage, Crop Rotation, Elvish Reclaimer, Thoughtseize. See also: Dark Depths combo, Marit Lage, Depths.)*

## Strategy Concepts

**aggro**: An aggressive deck archetype that wins by deploying efficient threats early and attacking. Pure aggro is less common in Legacy; most aggressive decks are 'tempo' decks like Delver that combine threats with disruption. *(See also: tempo, burn, clock.)*

**all-in**: A strategy that commits fully to one game plan without backup. 'All-in combo' means a deck that either executes its combo or loses. Oops All Spells and Belcher are all-in Legacy decks. High risk, high reward. *(See also: combo, glass cannon.)*

**answer**: A card that can deal with an opponent's threat. 'I need an answer for that Marit Lage token.' Swords to Plowshares is the premier answer in Legacy white decks. *(Related cards: Swords to Plowshares, Force of Will, Abrupt Decay. See also: threat, removal, silver bullet.)*

**bait**: A spell cast specifically to draw out a counterspell so your more important spell can resolve. 'I cast Ponder as bait, then resolved Show and Tell when they used their Daze.' *(See also: force check, counter war.)*

**board presence**: The total strength and quality of permanents you control on the battlefield. 'They have a stronger board presence' means more/better creatures and other permanents in play. *(See also: board state, going wide, going tall.)*

**board state**: The current configuration of all permanents on the battlefield. Reading the board state correctly is essential in Legacy for deciding when to use removal, deploy threats, or go for a combo. *(See also: board presence.)*

**bolt the bird**: Strategic axiom meaning you should kill an opponent's mana dork (originally Birds of Paradise) immediately with a removal spell rather than saving it for a bigger threat. Prioritizes denying mana acceleration. *(Related cards: Lightning Bolt, Birds of Paradise. See also: bolt, dork, tempo.)*

**card advantage**: Having access to more cards than your opponent, either by drawing more or making them discard/lose cards. A fundamental concept in Magic theory. Legacy's cantrips (Brainstorm, Ponder) provide card selection rather than pure card advantage. *(Related cards: Brainstorm, Hymn to Tourach, Ancestral Recall. See also: value, two-for-one, tempo advantage.)*

**card selection**: The ability to choose which cards you draw or keep, as opposed to raw card draw. Brainstorm + fetch land provides exceptional card selection by seeing 3 new cards and putting 2 bad ones back then shuffling. *(Related cards: Brainstorm, Ponder, Preordain, Sensei's Divining Top. See also: cantrip, card advantage.)*

**color pie**: The distribution of abilities and effects among Magic's five colors. Each color has strengths and weaknesses. Legacy's deep card pool means each color's strengths are at their peak: blue has the best cantrips and counters, white the best removal, black the best discard, red the best direct damage, green the best creatures.

**combo**: A deck archetype or strategy built around a specific interaction between two or more cards that wins the game. Legacy is famous for powerful combos: Show and Tell + Emrakul, Dark Depths + Thespian's Stage, Painter's Servant + Grindstone. *(See also: go off, storm, infinite.)*

**control**: A deck archetype that wins by answering every opponent threat, drawing more cards, and eventually winning with a hard-to-beat finisher. Legacy control decks run Force of Will, Swords to Plowshares, Jace the Mind Sculptor, and various countermagic. *(Related cards: Force of Will, Jace, the Mind Sculptor, Swords to Plowshares. See also: midrange, permission, draw-go.)*

**counter war**: When both players fight over a spell on the stack with multiple counterspells. 'I Force of Willed their Show and Tell, they Force of Willed my Force, I had Flusterstorm for the win.' Counter wars are a defining feature of Legacy blue mirrors. *(Related cards: Force of Will, Flusterstorm, Pyroblast. See also: the stack, permission, force check.)*

**deterministic**: A combo that will succeed 100% of the time once started, requiring no luck. 'ANT's kill is deterministic once you have enough storm count.' Contrasted with non-deterministic combos that can fizzle. *(See also: non-deterministic, combo.)*

**disruption**: Cards that interfere with the opponent's game plan. In Legacy, disruption includes countermagic (Force of Will), hand attack (Thoughtseize), mana denial (Wasteland), and permanent-based hate (Chalice of the Void). *(Related cards: Force of Will, Thoughtseize, Wasteland, Chalice of the Void. See also: permission, hand attack, mana denial.)*

**draw-go**: A control playstyle that passes the turn while holding up mana for instant-speed interaction. Draw a card, say 'go.' The purest form of reactive control. Less common in modern Legacy but the philosophy persists. *(See also: control, permission, instant speed.)*

**evasion**: The ability of a creature to get past blockers. Flying, shadow, unblockable, and protection all provide evasion. True-Name Nemesis is Legacy's most notorious evasive creature (protection from a chosen player). *(Related cards: True-Name Nemesis, Delver of Secrets, Murktide Regent. See also: clock, threat.)*

**favorable matchup**: A pairing where your deck has a statistical advantage. 'Death and Taxes has a favorable matchup against Storm' because tax effects and Thalia disrupt combo. *(See also: matchup, unfavorable matchup.)*

**finisher**: A card designed to end the game once you have control. Control decks need finishers to close out games. In Legacy, common finishers include Jace the Mind Sculptor (fatesealing), Murktide Regent, and Monastery Mentor. *(Related cards: Jace, the Mind Sculptor, Murktide Regent, Monastery Mentor. See also: win condition, clock, threat.)*

**force check**: Playing a threatening spell specifically to see if the opponent has Force of Will. 'I cast Hymn to Tourach as a force check before comboing off.' If they Force it, you know the coast is clearer for your real threat. *(Related cards: Force of Will. See also: counter war, bait.)*

**format health**: The overall state of a format in terms of diversity, fun, and competitive balance. A healthy Legacy format has many viable archetypes. When one deck dominates, format health suffers and bans may follow. *(See also: meta, banned.)*

**game 1**: The first game of a match, played before sideboarding. In Legacy, game 1 often favors linear strategies (combo, aggro) because control/fair decks need sideboard cards to interact with diverse strategies. *(See also: sideboard, games 2 and 3.)*

**games 2 and 3**: Post-sideboard games where both players have access to targeted hate cards. Many Legacy matchups flip after sideboarding. 'We're favored games 2 and 3 because we bring in 6 cards against them.' *(See also: sideboard, game 1.)*

**glass cannon**: A deck that is extremely fast and powerful but very fragile. Can be disrupted easily by hate cards or countermagic. Belcher and Oops All Spells are Legacy glass cannons. *(See also: all-in, combo.)*

**going tall**: A strategy focused on making one or few creatures very large. Marit Lage (a 20/20) is the ultimate 'going tall' play. Murktide Regent grows larger as you exile instants/sorceries. *(Related cards: Marit Lage, Murktide Regent, Tarmogoyf. See also: going wide, voltron.)*

**going wide**: A strategy that wins by creating many small creatures rather than a few large ones. Elves in Legacy goes wide, producing massive quantities of creatures with Natural Order as a finisher. *(See also: going tall, aggro, swarm.)*

**hand attack**: Discard spells that strip cards from the opponent's hand. Legacy hand attack includes Thoughtseize, Hymn to Tourach, and Grief. Hand attack is proactive disruption that lets you see and dismantle the opponent's plan. *(Related cards: Thoughtseize, Hymn to Tourach, Grief, Unmask. See also: disruption.)*

**hard lock**: A board state where the opponent literally cannot play Magic anymore. Painter's Servant + Iona is a hard lock. Helm of Obedience + Rest in Peace is a hard lock combo that also wins immediately. *(Related cards: Painter's Servant, Iona, Shield of Emeria. See also: soft lock, prison, combo.)*

**hatebear**: A creature (typically 2-power, 2-toughness or similar) that disrupts the opponent's strategy. Thalia, Guardian of Thraben is the iconic Legacy hatebear, taxing noncreature spells. Others include Sanctum Prelate, Ethersworn Canonist. *(Related cards: Thalia, Guardian of Thraben, Sanctum Prelate, Ethersworn Canonist, Collector Ouphe, Spirit of the Labyrinth. See also: Death and Taxes, tax, stax.)*

**instant speed**: The ability to act during the opponent's turn or in response to their spells. Legacy is heavily defined by instant-speed interaction: countermagic, removal, and flash creatures. 'At instant speed' means using this timing advantage. *(See also: flash, the stack.)*

**line**: A sequence of plays or a strategic approach. 'What's the correct line here?' means 'what's the best series of plays?' Legacy's complexity means there are often multiple viable lines requiring deep analysis. *(See also: punt, play around.)*

**mana curve**: The distribution of mana costs in a deck. A good curve means having plays at each mana cost. Legacy decks have notoriously low curves; most competitive decks peak at 1-2 mana. A deck that 'curves out' plays optimally on each turn. *(See also: curve out, tempo.)*

**mana denial**: A strategy focused on preventing the opponent from having enough mana to execute their game plan. Legacy's mana denial tools include Wasteland, Rishadan Port, Stifle (on fetch lands), Daze, and Thalia. *(Related cards: Wasteland, Rishadan Port, Stifle, Daze. See also: tempo, Wasteland lock, Daze math.)*

**manabase**: The collection of lands (and sometimes mana rocks/dorks) that produce mana in a deck. Legacy manabases are uniquely constructed: dual lands + fetch lands give perfect mana but are vulnerable to Wasteland, Blood Moon, and Back to Basics. *(See also: dual lands, fetch land, Blood Moon.)*

**matchup**: How two specific decks perform against each other. 'The Delver vs Storm matchup favors Delver' because Daze and Force check Storm's combo. Understanding matchups is essential for sideboard decisions. *(See also: meta, sideboard.)*

**meta**: Short for metagame. The collective strategies, decks, and card choices prevalent in a given environment. 'The meta is full of Delver right now.' Understanding the meta is crucial for sideboard construction in Legacy. *(See also: metagame, tech, sideboard.)*

**metagame**: The ecosystem of decks and strategies being played in a given format or tournament. Also refers to strategic decisions made based on what opponents are likely playing. 'Metagaming for the event' means choosing cards/decks to beat the expected field. *(See also: meta, tech, sideboard.)*

**midrange**: A deck archetype positioned between aggro and control. Midrange decks play efficient threats and removal, adapting their role based on the matchup. In Legacy, BG(x) midrange decks use Thoughtseize and efficient creatures. *(Related cards: Thoughtseize, Tarmogoyf, Uro, Titan of Nature's Wrath. See also: aggro, control, value.)*

**mulligan decision**: The process of evaluating whether to keep or mulligan an opening hand. In Legacy, you evaluate: does this hand have a game plan? Can it interact with the opponent? Does it have mana? Key Legacy-specific factors include having Force of Will against combo. *(See also: mull, keep.)*

**non-deterministic**: A combo that has some chance of failure due to randomness. 'Dredge's combo is non-deterministic because you need to hit specific cards off dredges.' Charbelcher is non-deterministic if you run a land. *(See also: deterministic, whiff.)*

**permission**: Countermagic. A permission-based strategy relies on countering the opponent's key spells. Legacy has the deepest permission suite in Magic: Force of Will, Daze, Spell Pierce, Flusterstorm, Counterspell. *(Related cards: Force of Will, Daze, Spell Pierce, Flusterstorm, Counterspell. See also: counter, control, counter war.)*

**play around**: To make suboptimal plays to avoid being punished by a specific card the opponent might have. 'Play around Daze by casting your spell with extra mana open.' Reading the opponent's possible hands is essential in Legacy. *(See also: Daze math, punt.)*

**priority**: The right to take an action in the game. The active player gets priority first, then it passes. Understanding priority is essential in Legacy for timing plays correctly around instant-speed interaction. *(See also: the stack, resolve.)*

**prison**: A deck archetype that wins by preventing the opponent from playing Magic. Uses lock pieces like Chalice of the Void, Blood Moon, Trinisphere, and Ensnaring Bridge. Mono Red Prison is a tier Legacy deck. *(Related cards: Chalice of the Void, Blood Moon, Trinisphere, Ensnaring Bridge. See also: stax, lock, stompy.)*

**protection**: Ways to keep your threats alive. In Legacy, this includes countermagic to protect combo pieces, Veil of Summer against blue/black interaction, and Mother of Runes/Giver of Runes in creature decks. *(Related cards: Veil of Summer, Mother of Runes, Giver of Runes. See also: permission, counter.)*

**protection spell**: A spell used to protect your own permanent or combo from interaction. In Legacy, Veil of Summer, Flusterstorm (protecting your combo from counters), and Teferi, Time Raveler (preventing instant-speed responses) serve as protection. *(Related cards: Veil of Summer, Flusterstorm, Teferi, Time Raveler. See also: protection, permission.)*

**race**: When both players are trying to kill each other as fast as possible rather than interacting. 'It was a race: my Delver clock vs their Dark Depths combo.' Evaluating who wins the race is a key Legacy skill. *(See also: clock, goldfish.)*

**removal**: Spells that destroy, exile, or otherwise deal with opposing permanents. Premium Legacy removal includes Swords to Plowshares, Lightning Bolt, Prismatic Ending, and Abrupt Decay. *(Related cards: Swords to Plowshares, Lightning Bolt, Prismatic Ending, Abrupt Decay. See also: answer, bolt, path.)*

**rogue deck**: An unusual or unexpected deck that isn't part of the established metagame. Rogue decks can catch opponents off guard because they don't know what to expect. 'I lost to a rogue Stasis deck.' *(See also: brew, jank, spice.)*

**sideboard**: The 15-card pool of cards you can swap into your deck between games of a match. In Legacy, sideboard construction is critical because the format's diversity requires answers to combo, control, aggro, and prison strategies. *(See also: tech, hate, silver bullet, meta.)*

**silver bullet**: A specific card included (usually as a one-of) to answer a particular problem. Named for the weakness of werewolves. In Legacy, Green Sun's Zenith toolbox decks often run silver bullets like Collector Ouphe. *(Related cards: Collector Ouphe, Bojuka Bog, Karakas. See also: tech, hate, toolbox, tutor.)*

**soft lock**: A board state that makes it very difficult (but not impossible) for the opponent to play. Chalice of the Void on 1 is a soft lock against Delver decks; they can still draw 2+ mana spells but their 1-drops are shut off. *(Related cards: Chalice of the Void, Thalia, Guardian of Thraben. See also: hard lock, prison, lock.)*

**stabilize**: To survive an aggressive start and reach a board state where you're no longer in danger of losing. 'I stabilized at 3 life behind a Batterskull.' The key inflection point in aggro vs control matchups. *(See also: control, race.)*

**stax**: A strategy that uses permanents to tax or restrict the opponent's ability to play spells and use resources. Named after the card Smokestack. In Legacy, this overlaps with prison and tax-based strategies like Death and Taxes. *(Related cards: Smokestack, Trinisphere, Thorn of Amethyst, Rishadan Port. See also: prison, hatebear, tax.)*

**stompy**: A deck that plays fast mana (Sol lands, Chrome Mox, Simian Spirit Guide) into disruptive permanents like Chalice of the Void and Blood Moon. Moon Stompy and Eldrazi Stompy are Legacy examples. Named for the aggressive follow-up threats. *(Related cards: Chalice of the Void, Blood Moon, Chrome Mox, City of Traitors, Ancient Tomb. See also: prison, Mono Red Prison, Moon Stompy.)*

**tax**: Making spells or abilities cost more mana/resources than normal. A core Legacy strategy exemplified by Thalia, Sphere of Resistance, and Rishadan Port. 'Tax effects' slow down the opponent's game plan. *(Related cards: Thalia, Guardian of Thraben, Rishadan Port, Sphere of Resistance. See also: Thalia tax, Sphere effects, stax.)*

**tempo**: The advantage gained by using mana more efficiently than your opponent, effectively being 'ahead' on board and development. Tempo decks like Delver aim to deploy a cheap threat and protect it while disrupting the opponent's development with Daze and Wasteland. *(Related cards: Daze, Wasteland, Delver of Secrets. See also: tempo advantage, mana denial, Delver.)*

**tempo advantage**: Being ahead on board development relative to mana spent. Daze is a tempo card: it counters a spell but costs you a land drop. Wasteland + Daze is the classic Legacy tempo play pattern. *(Related cards: Daze, Wasteland, Delver of Secrets. See also: tempo, mana denial.)*

**the stack**: The zone where spells and abilities exist while waiting to resolve. Spells resolve in last-in-first-out order. Understanding the stack is crucial for Legacy's complex interactions. Players can respond to spells on the stack. *(See also: resolve, priority, counter war.)*

**threat**: A card that will win the game if not answered. Threats force the opponent to react. In Legacy, efficient threats like Delver of Secrets and Murktide Regent define entire archetypes. *(Related cards: Delver of Secrets, Murktide Regent, Marit Lage. See also: answer, clock, finisher.)*

**tier 1**: The best-performing decks in the format based on tournament results. In Legacy, tier 1 decks typically include Delver variants, Reanimator, and whatever combo/control deck is performing well in the current meta. *(See also: meta, tier 2.)*

**tier 2**: Competitive decks that are viable but not the most popular/successful. Many Legacy decks are perennial tier 2: Burn, Merfolk, Enchantress. Can spike tournaments with good metagame positioning. *(See also: meta, tier 1.)*

**toolbox**: A deck strategy that runs many one-of silver bullets accessed via tutors or search effects. In Legacy, Green Sun's Zenith decks are toolbox strategies, finding the right creature for any situation. *(Related cards: Green Sun's Zenith, Stoneforge Mystic, Recruiter of the Guard. See also: silver bullet, tutor.)*

**tribal**: A deck built around creature type synergies. Legacy tribal decks include Elves, Goblins, Merfolk, and Humans. Tribal strategies use lords (creatures that buff the tribe) and tribal-specific cards. *(See also: Elves, Goblins, Merfolk.)*

**two-for-one**: Getting two cards' worth of value from a single card, or trading one card for two of the opponent's. Hymn to Tourach is a classic 2-for-1. Also written as 2-for-1. *(Related cards: Hymn to Tourach, Kolaghan's Command. See also: card advantage, value.)*

**uncounterable**: A spell that cannot be countered. In Legacy, Abrupt Decay, Supreme Verdict, Veil of Summer (protects other spells), and Cavern of Souls (for named creature type) provide uncounterable effects. Bypasses Force of Will. *(Related cards: Abrupt Decay, Supreme Verdict, Cavern of Souls. See also: permission, counter war.)*

**unfavorable matchup**: A pairing where your deck is at a disadvantage. 'Reanimator has an unfavorable matchup against decks with maindeck graveyard hate.' Sideboard cards can improve unfavorable matchups. *(See also: matchup, favorable matchup.)*

**value**: Getting more out of your cards than their face value, typically through card advantage or synergy. 'Uro generates so much value.' Value-oriented strategies prioritize long-term resource accumulation over speed. *(See also: card advantage, grind, two-for-one.)*

**win condition**: The specific card(s) or combination that a deck uses to actually win the game. Storm's win condition is Tendrils of Agony. Doomsday's is assembling a lethal pile. Also called 'wincon.' *(Related cards: Tendrils of Agony, Thassa's Oracle. See also: finisher, combo.)*

## Legacy-Specific Terms

**8-Moon**: Running both Blood Moon and Magus of the Moon (a creature with Blood Moon's effect) in a deck, totaling 8 copies of the Moon effect. Maximizes the chances of shutting down nonbasic lands. Common in Mono Red Prison. *(Related cards: Blood Moon, Magus of the Moon. See also: Blood Moon, Mono Red Prison.)*

**Back to Basics**: A 3-mana blue enchantment that prevents nonbasic lands from untapping. The blue equivalent of Blood Moon. Used in mono-blue or light-splash control decks to punish greedy Legacy manabases. *(Related cards: Back to Basics. See also: Blood Moon, prison.)*

**banned**: A card that is not legal to play in a given format. Legacy has a significant banned list including Deathrite Shaman, Treasure Cruise, Dig Through Time, Sensei's Divining Top, and others. The DCI/Wizards manages the ban list. *(See also: restricted, the reserved list.)*

**Blood Moon**: A 3-mana red enchantment that turns all nonbasic lands into Mountains. Devastating in Legacy where most decks rely heavily on nonbasic lands (duals, fetches, utility lands). Can completely lock out multicolor decks. *(Related cards: Blood Moon. See also: Mono Red Prison, Moon Stompy, Back to Basics, 8-Moon.)*

**brainstorm lock**: When you cast Brainstorm without a way to shuffle away the two cards you put back, leaving you stuck drawing those cards on subsequent turns. A classic beginner mistake in Legacy. *(Related cards: Brainstorm. See also: brainstorm, fetch land, crack.)*

**Chalice check**: Evaluating whether your deck can function through a resolved Chalice of the Void on 1 (or 2). Decks that rely heavily on 1-mana spells fail the Chalice check. This affects deck construction and card choices. *(Related cards: Chalice of the Void. See also: Chalice on 1, Chalice.)*

**Chalice on 1**: Casting Chalice of the Void with X=1, countering all 1-mana spells. Devastates Legacy because the format's most important cards cost 1: Brainstorm, Ponder, Swords to Plowshares, Lightning Bolt, Delver of Secrets, Dark Ritual, Thoughtseize. *(Related cards: Chalice of the Void. See also: Chalice, prison, stompy.)*

**Cradle of the Hoof**: The combination of Gaea's Cradle powering out Craterhoof Behemoth in Elves. Craterhoof gives all your creatures +X/+X and trample where X is the number of creatures you control. Usually lethal with even a modest board. *(Related cards: Gaea's Cradle, Craterhoof Behemoth. See also: Elves, NO.)*

**Daze math**: The mental calculation of whether your opponent can pay the extra 1 mana from Daze. If they tapped all their lands, Daze is live. If they left a land up, it might not be. Also affects your own sequencing: do you play a land to play around Daze, or cast your spell into it? *(Related cards: Daze. See also: Daze, tempo, mana denial.)*

**Doomsday pile**: The specific 5-card stack created by Doomsday. Constructing the right pile for the game state (considering life total, cards in hand, mana available) is one of the most skill-intensive things in Legacy. Common piles involve cantrips leading to Thassa's Oracle. *(Related cards: Doomsday, Thassa's Oracle. See also: Doomsday.)*

**dual lands**: The original 10 dual lands from Alpha/Beta/Unlimited/Revised that have two basic land types and no drawback. (e.g., Underground Sea is Island Swamp). The backbone of Legacy manabases. All on the Reserved List. *(Related cards: Underground Sea, Volcanic Island, Tropical Island, Tundra, Bayou, Savannah, Scrubland, Badlands, Plateau, Taiga. See also: reserved list, fetch land, manabase.)*

**fetch land**: Lands that sacrifice to search for a land with a specific basic land type. In Legacy, they find dual lands (e.g., Polluted Delta finds Underground Sea). Enable Brainstorm shuffles, thin the deck, and fix mana perfectly. All 10 Zendikar/Onslaught fetches see play. *(Related cards: Polluted Delta, Flooded Strand, Scalding Tarn, Misty Rainforest, Verdant Catacombs, Windswept Heath, Wooded Foothills, Bloodstained Mire, Marsh Flats, Arid Mesa. See also: dual lands, brainstorm, crack.)*

**fetchable**: A land that can be found by fetch lands because it has a basic land type. In Legacy, dual lands are fetchable (Underground Sea is an Island Swamp), which is why they're so powerful: any fetch can find any color combination. *(See also: fetch land, dual lands.)*

**free spell**: A spell that can be cast without paying mana, usually with an alternate cost. Legacy's free spells include Force of Will (pitch blue card + 1 life), Daze (return Island), Force of Negation, and the Elemental Incarnation cycle. Free spells are what make Legacy unique. *(Related cards: Force of Will, Daze, Force of Negation, Grief, Solitude, Endurance. See also: pitch spell, FoW.)*

**graveyard hate**: Cards that disrupt graveyard strategies. Essential sideboard cards in Legacy. Includes Leyline of the Void, Rest in Peace, Surgical Extraction, Endurance, Tormod's Crypt, and Bojuka Bog. *(Related cards: Leyline of the Void, Rest in Peace, Surgical Extraction, Endurance, Tormod's Crypt, Bojuka Bog. See also: hate, Reanimator, Dredge.)*

**greedy manabase**: A manabase that runs too few basic lands, making it vulnerable to Wasteland, Blood Moon, and Back to Basics. 'That 4-color deck has a greedy manabase.' Legacy rewards punishing greed. *(See also: manabase, Blood Moon, Wasteland.)*

**Initiative**: A mechanic from Commander Legends: Battle for Baldur's Gate. Taking the initiative lets you venture through the Undercity dungeon. White Initiative creatures (White Plume Adventurer) formed a powerful Legacy deck that used fast mana and tax effects. *(Related cards: White Plume Adventurer, Seasoned Dungeoneer. See also: stompy, Death and Taxes.)*

**Karakas bounce**: Using Karakas to return a legendary creature to its owner's hand. Defensively bounces Emrakul, Griselbrand, or Marit Lage. Offensively re-triggers ETB abilities on your own legends. A key interaction in Legacy. *(Related cards: Karakas. See also: Karakas, Sneak and Show, Reanimator.)*

**miracle**: A keyword mechanic that allows a spell to be cast for a reduced cost when it's the first card drawn in a turn. Terminus (miracle W for a Wrath of God effect) is the primary miracle card in Legacy. Brainstorm enables setting up miracles. *(Related cards: Terminus, Entreat the Angels. See also: Terminus, brainstorm.)*

**pitch count**: The number of cards in hand that can be used to pay alternate costs for pitch spells. 'I have two blue cards for Force of Will pitch.' Managing pitch count is crucial in Legacy blue decks. *(Related cards: Force of Will. See also: pitch, force.)*

**pitch spell**: A spell cast by exiling a card from hand rather than paying mana. Force of Will (exile blue card), Force of Negation, Grief (exile black card), etc. 'Pitch' refers to the alternate cost of discarding/exiling. *(Related cards: Force of Will, Force of Negation, Grief, Solitude, Fury, Endurance, Subtlety. See also: free spell, pitch.)*

**Rishadan Port tax**: Using Rishadan Port's ability to tap an opponent's land during their upkeep, denying them a mana each turn. A key tempo play in Death and Taxes and other strategies. Combines with Wasteland for mana denial. *(Related cards: Rishadan Port. See also: Death and Taxes, mana denial, tax.)*

**shuffle effect**: Any effect that shuffles your library, most commonly cracking a fetch land. In Legacy, shuffle effects are critical for Brainstorm (put bad cards on top, then shuffle them away) and Top interactions. *(Related cards: Brainstorm. See also: brainstorm, fetch land, crack.)*

**sol land**: A land that produces 2 mana, named after Sol Ring. In Legacy, Ancient Tomb (2 colorless, 2 life) and City of Traitors (2 colorless, sacrificed when you play another land) enable turn-1 Chalice of the Void and other fast plays. *(Related cards: Ancient Tomb, City of Traitors. See also: ramp, stompy, prison.)*

**Sphere effects**: Permanents that tax spell casting (named after Sphere of Resistance). Includes Thorn of Amethyst, Trinisphere, and Thalia. Making spells cost more disrupts the mana-efficient strategies that Legacy is built on. *(Related cards: Sphere of Resistance, Thorn of Amethyst, Trinisphere, Thalia, Guardian of Thraben. See also: stax, prison, tax.)*

**storm count**: The number of spells cast before the storm spell in a given turn. Tendrils of Agony needs a storm count of 9 to deal 20 damage (10 copies x 2 damage each). Tracking storm count is essential for both the Storm player and opponent. *(Related cards: Tendrils of Agony. See also: Storm, go off.)*

**Surgical effect**: Exiling all copies of a card from the opponent's graveyard, hand, and library. Named after Surgical Extraction. Devastating against combo decks with limited win conditions. 'Surgical their Griselbrand.' *(Related cards: Surgical Extraction, Extirpate. See also: Surgical, hate.)*

**Thalia tax**: The additional 1 mana that Thalia, Guardian of Thraben adds to noncreature spells. Makes Brainstorm cost 2, Force of Will's alternate cost require 1 mana, and generally slows down spell-based decks enormously. *(Related cards: Thalia, Guardian of Thraben. See also: Thalia, Death and Taxes, hatebear, tax.)*

**the reserved list**: Wizards of the Coast's promise never to reprint certain cards, mostly from early Magic sets. Includes Legacy staples like dual lands, Gaea's Cradle, Lion's Eye Diamond, City of Traitors, and The Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale. The primary barrier to Legacy accessibility. *(Related cards: Underground Sea, Volcanic Island, Gaea's Cradle, Lion's Eye Diamond, The Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale, City of Traitors. See also: dual lands.)*

**turn 1 kill**: Winning the game on the very first turn. Possible in Legacy through Storm (ritual into ritual into Tendrils), Reanimator (Entomb + Reanimate Griselbrand), or Oops All Spells. Force of Will exists specifically to prevent this. *(See also: goldfish, go off, combo, Force of Will.)*

**Wasteland lock**: Recurring Wasteland every turn (via Life from the Loam, Crucible of Worlds, or Wrenn and Six) to destroy every land the opponent plays. Prevents them from ever having enough mana to cast spells. A primary win condition of the Lands deck. *(Related cards: Wasteland, Life from the Loam, Crucible of Worlds, Wrenn and Six. See also: Lands, mana denial, Wasteland, W6.)*

**wasteland-proof**: A manabase that can function even if Wasteland destroys key lands. Running basic lands or having redundant mana sources makes you wasteland-proof. Mono-colored decks are naturally wasteland-proof. *(Related cards: Wasteland. See also: Wasteland, basic, manabase.)*
