## What Is Legacy?

Legacy is a **non-rotating, eternal constructed format** in Magic: The Gathering that permits cards from **all Magic sets ever printed**, subject only to a curated ban list. It evolved from the format formerly known as **Type 1.5**. The modern Legacy format began in 2004-2006 when the DCI separated Legacy's banned list from Vintage's restricted list. The first Legacy Grand Prix was Grand Prix Philadelphia in 2005.

Unlike Standard or Pioneer, cards never "rotate out" — once legal, a card stays legal unless it is specifically banned. Unlike Vintage, which restricts powerful cards to 1 copy, Legacy outright bans them.

## Deck Construction Rules

- **Card Pool**: Every Magic: The Gathering set ever printed, minus banned cards
- **Minimum deck size**: 60 cards
- **Sideboard**: Up to 15 cards
- **Copy limit**: Maximum 4 copies of any individual card (except basic lands)
- **No rotation**: Cards remain legal indefinitely unless added to the ban list

## What Makes Legacy Unique

### Free Counterspells

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Force+of+Will&format=image&version=normal&set=all" alt="Force of Will" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Daze&format=image&version=normal&set=nem" alt="Daze" width="217">

[Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) and [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) allow players to interact on turn 0-1 without mana. This creates a unique strategic dynamic where tapping out for a threat doesn't mean you're defenseless. Force of Will is in ~45% of all Legacy decks.

### The Brainstorm Format

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Brainstorm&format=image&version=normal&set=ice" alt="Brainstorm" width="217">

[Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22) + fetchlands is the defining card selection engine. Brainstorm lets you draw 3 and put 2 back, then a fetchland shuffles away the bad cards. This gives blue decks extraordinary consistency and card quality. Brainstorm is in ~43% of decks.

The format supports the full spectrum:
- **Tempo**: Dimir Tempo, Izzet Delver — cheap threats + free countermagic + mana denial
- **Combo**: Oops! All Spells (turn-1 kills), Storm, Sneak and Show, [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22)
- **Control**: Beanstalk Control, Jeskai Control, UW Miracles
- **Prison/Stompy**: Eldrazi, Red Stompy, Affinity — [Chalice of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chalice+of+the+Void%22) + [Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22) locks
- **Midrange**: [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) Midrange, Jund — value-oriented and grindy
- **Land-based**: Lands — unique strategy using [Exploration](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Exploration%22), [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Life+from+the+Loam%22), and land-based win conditions
- **Tribal**: Goblins, Elves, Merfolk — synergy-driven creature strategies

### The Reserved List
Dual lands ([Underground Sea](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Underground+Sea%22), [Volcanic Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Volcanic+Island%22), [Tundra](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tundra%22), etc.) are on the Reserved List and can never be reprinted. These are format staples that provide two colors of mana with no drawback, and they drive much of Legacy's cost. An Underground Sea is typically $500-800+.

### Format Health
The March 2026 B&R announcement made **no changes**, citing balanced metagame health. No single deck exceeds 55% win rate. The format has ~54 distinct competitive archetypes. The most recent ban was [Entomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Entomb%22) (November 2025), which neutered Reanimator as a tier 1 strategy.

## Cost and Accessibility

Legacy is one of the most expensive paper formats due to Reserved List cards:

- **Budget option**: Oops! All Spells (~$1,100), Burn (~$1,500)
- **Mid-range**: Most competitive decks cost $3,000-$5,000
- **Expensive**: Lands (~$8,300), 4-Color Control (~$6,000+)
- **MTGO**: Significantly cheaper (~300-650 tix)
- **Proxies**: Many local game stores allow proxied Legacy events

### Play Platforms
- **Paper (LGS)**: Local game stores run Legacy events, some allow proxies
- **MTGO**: Magic Online has active Legacy leagues and challenges
- **SpellTable**: Webcam play for paper collections
- **Cockatrice / Moxfield Playtest**: Free online alternatives

## The Strategic Triangle

Legacy archetypes exist in a rough metagame triangle (from Reid Duke):

```
        Fair Blue (Delver, UWx Control)
       /                                \
      / beats                    beats   \
     /                                    \
  Combo  <————— beats ————————  Fair Nonblue
  (Storm, S&T, Oops)           (D&T, Stompy, Jund)
```

- **Fair Blue** beats **Combo** (free countermagic disrupts combo turns)
- **Fair Nonblue** beats **Fair Blue** (hatebears and mana denial punish cantrip-reliant strategies)
- **Combo** beats **Fair Nonblue** (no free countermagic means combo goes off unimpeded)

This is a simplification — individual matchups vary greatly — but it captures the macro-level strategic dynamics that drive Legacy metagame shifts.

## How Legacy Games Play Out

### The Typical Turn Sequence

**Turn 0 (Before the game starts)**:
- Decide who goes first (play vs. draw)
- Draw opening hand of 7 cards
- London Mulligan: put hand back, draw 7 again, put 1 card on bottom. Repeat if needed.
- Legacy mulligan decisions are critical — knowing whether a hand is keepable requires deep format knowledge
- Some decks ([Leyline of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Leyline+of+the+Void%22), [Chancellor of the Annex](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chancellor+of+the+Annex%22)) have "turn 0" effects

**Turn 1 — The Most Important Turn**:
Legacy's power level means turn 1 often defines the game:
- **Combo**: Oops! All Spells can win. Storm can set up. Reanimator can cheat in a creature.
- **Tempo**: [Delver of Secrets](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Delver+of+Secrets%22), [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) backup, or [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) an opponent's land.
- **Prison**: [Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22) → [Chalice of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chalice+of+the+Void%22) on 1 locks out cantrips.
- **Fair**: Play a fetchland, pass. Represent [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22) or [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22).

**Turns 2-3 — The Critical Window**:

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Blood+Moon&format=image&version=normal&set=drk" alt="Blood Moon" width="217">

- Combo decks that didn't win turn 1 are trying to go off now
- Tempo decks deploy their main threats ([Murktide Regent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Murktide+Regent%22), [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22))
- Control decks establish card advantage (Jace, [Up the Beanstalk](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Up+the+Beanstalk%22))
- Prison decks add more locks ([Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22), [Trinisphere](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Trinisphere%22))

**Turns 4+ — The Grind**:
- If the game goes this long, it usually favors control and midrange strategies
- Combo decks that haven't won yet are likely struggling
- Resource management becomes critical — who has more cards? More mana? Board presence?

### Key Strategic Concepts

**Tempo**: The concept of "time" as a resource. A tempo play gains you a turn while costing your opponent one. [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) is the purest tempo card — it counters a spell for free, but the opponent can just pay 1 more mana next turn. You win by converting that time advantage into damage.

**Card Advantage**: Having access to more cards than your opponent. [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22) + fetchland effectively draws 1 extra card (you keep the best 1, shuffle away 2 bad ones). Ancestral Vision, [Sylvan Library](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sylvan+Library%22), and Uro all generate raw card advantage.

**Virtual Card Advantage**: Making a card in the opponent's hand useless. [Chalice of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chalice+of+the+Void%22) on 1 blanks every 1-mana spell in their hand. [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22) turns off every nonbasic land. These effects can be worth multiple cards without actually drawing you anything.

**Mana Denial**: Preventing the opponent from casting spells by destroying or taxing their mana. [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) is the classic combination. Lands takes this to the extreme with Wasteland recursion via [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Life+from+the+Loam%22). [Rishadan Port](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rishadan+Port%22) taps a land during upkeep so they can never use it.

**Free Spells**: Cards that cost 0 mana to cast (by paying an alternative cost). [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) (exile a blue card + 1 life), [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) (return an [Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Island%22)), [Force of Negation](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Negation%22), [Solitude](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Solitude%22), [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22), [Grief](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grief%22), [Fury](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Fury%22). These break the fundamental rule of MTG that spells require mana, enabling interaction even when tapped out.

<img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Solitude&format=image&version=normal&set=mh2" alt="Solitude" width="217"> <img src="https://api.scryfall.com/cards/named?exact=Endurance&format=image&version=normal&set=mh2" alt="Endurance" width="217">

**Storm Count**: The number of spells cast in a turn. [Tendrils of Agony](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tendrils+of+Agony%22) and [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Empty+the+Warrens%22) copy themselves for each prior spell. Storm decks spend their turn casting rituals ([Dark Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Ritual%22), [Rite of Flame](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rite+of+Flame%22), [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond%22)) to build a lethal count before casting the finisher.

### Categorical Bans
- All **Conspiracy** cards (designed for draft, not constructed)
- All **ante** cards (gambling, not legal in tournaments)
- All cards that bring **stickers** or **Attractions** into the game (Un-set mechanics)
- Cards with **culturally offensive** art, text, or names

### The Dual Lands

**Why they matter**: Dual lands enter untapped and produce two colors with zero drawback. No other land in Magic does this. Fetchlands ([Polluted Delta](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Polluted+Delta%22), [Scalding Tarn](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Scalding+Tarn%22), etc.) can search for them, providing perfect mana fixing.

### Fetchland-Dual Interactions
The 10 fetchlands each find two basic land types. Since duals have basic land types ([Underground Sea](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Underground+Sea%22) is an "[Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Island%22) [Swamp](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swamp%22)"), fetchlands find duals too:

- **Polluted Delta** ([Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Island%22)/[Swamp](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swamp%22)): Finds [Underground Sea](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Underground+Sea%22), [Volcanic Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Volcanic+Island%22), [Tropical Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tropical+Island%22), [Tundra](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tundra%22), [Bayou](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Bayou%22), [Badlands](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Badlands%22) + basic Island/Swamp
- **Scalding Tarn** ([Mountain](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mountain%22)/[Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Island%22)): Finds [Volcanic Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Volcanic+Island%22), [Underground Sea](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Underground+Sea%22), [Tropical Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tropical+Island%22), [Tundra](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tundra%22), [Badlands](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Badlands%22), [Plateau](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Plateau%22) + basics
- **Misty Rainforest** ([Forest](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Forest%22)/[Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Island%22)): Finds [Tropical Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tropical+Island%22), [Underground Sea](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Underground+Sea%22), [Volcanic Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Volcanic+Island%22), [Tundra](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tundra%22), [Bayou](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Bayou%22), [Savannah](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Savannah%22) + basics

This is why fetchlands + duals create such flexible mana bases — one fetchland might access 6+ different dual lands.

## Common Play Patterns

### The Brainstorm Lock

[Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22) without a fetchland can "lock" you — you put 2 bad cards on top and now have to draw them. Always have a shuffle effect (fetchland, [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22)) available before casting Brainstorm if possible. The best Brainstorm patterns:
1. Cast [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22), see 3 new cards
2. Put 2 unwanted cards on top
3. Crack a fetchland to shuffle them away
4. You effectively drew the best card of 4 and shuffled 2 bad ones into your deck

### The Daze Check
When an opponent plays a spell and leaves 1 mana open, they might have [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22). When they tap out? They definitely don't. Experienced players "Daze check" by leading with the less important spell first. If it resolves, the opponent spent their Daze on the wrong thing. If it gets Dazed, your important spell resolves next turn.

### The Force of Will Decision
[Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) costs you 2 cards (Force + a blue card). This means you should only Force spells that would otherwise cost you the game. Don't Force a [Tarmogoyf](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tarmogoyf%22) if you can just block it. Force the [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) that would put Emrakul into play. Learning when to Force and when to hold is one of the most important Legacy skills.

### Wasteland Sequencing
With [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) in your deck, the question is: when do you use it?
- **Against greedy mana bases** (3+ colors): [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) early to cut them off a color
- **Against 1-2 color decks**: Save it for their important utility land ([Urza's Saga](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Urza%27s+Saga%22), [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Depths%22), [Karakas](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Karakas%22))
- **With [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22)**: [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) their land, then Daze their spell — they can't pay the 1 mana because you just destroyed their land

## Glossary of Legacy-Specific Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Bolt the Bird** | Kill the mana creature immediately (don't let them accelerate) |
| **Cantrip** | A spell that draws a card as part of its effect ([Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22), [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22)) |
| **Going off** | When a combo deck executes its winning combo turn |
| **Goldfish** | Test a deck by playing solitaire (no opponent) |
| **The Jace test** | "Does this creature die to Jace bouncing it?" (4+ toughness matters) |
| **Pitch spell** | A spell with an alternative cost of exiling a card ([Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22), [Solitude](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Solitude%22)) |
| **Surgical** | Target and remove all copies of a card from the game |
| **Chalice on 1** | [Chalice of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chalice+of+the+Void%22) with 1 counter — counters all 1-mana spells |
| **Fetchland** | Land that sacrifices to search for a basic land type |
| **Dual** | Original dual lands from Alpha/Beta/Unlimited/Revised |
| **EOT** | "End of turn" — when instant-speed plays typically happen |
| **FoW** | [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) |
| **Mise** | To get lucky, or to luckily win |
| **Punt** | To make a mistake that costs you the game |
| **Scoop** | Concede the game |
| **SB** | Sideboard |
| **Wasteland lock** | Recurring [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) to destroy all opponent's lands |
