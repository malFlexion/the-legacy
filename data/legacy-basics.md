# Legacy Format — Complete Guide

## What Is Legacy?

Legacy is a **non-rotating, eternal constructed format** in Magic: The Gathering that permits cards from **all Magic sets ever printed**, subject only to a curated ban list. It evolved from the format formerly known as **Type 1.5**. The modern Legacy format began in 2004-2006 when the DCI separated Legacy's banned list from Vintage's restricted list. The first Legacy Grand Prix was Grand Prix Philadelphia in 2005.

Unlike Standard or Pioneer, cards never "rotate out" — once legal, a card stays legal unless it is specifically banned. Unlike Vintage, which restricts powerful cards to 1 copy, Legacy outright bans them.

## Deck Construction Rules

- **Card Pool**: Every Magic: The Gathering set ever printed, minus banned cards
- **Minimum deck size**: 60 cards
- **Sideboard**: Up to 15 cards
- **Copy limit**: Maximum 4 copies of any individual card (except basic lands)
- **No rotation**: Cards remain legal indefinitely unless added to the ban list

## What Makes Legacy Unique

### Free Counterspells
Force of Will and Daze allow players to interact on turn 0-1 without mana. This creates a unique strategic dynamic where tapping out for a threat doesn't mean you're defenseless. Force of Will is in ~45% of all Legacy decks.

### The Brainstorm Format
Brainstorm + fetchlands is the defining card selection engine. Brainstorm lets you draw 3 and put 2 back, then a fetchland shuffles away the bad cards. This gives blue decks extraordinary consistency and card quality. Brainstorm is in ~43% of decks.

### Efficient Threats and Answers
- One-mana threats: Delver of Secrets, Goblin Lackey, Mother of Runes
- Zero-mana interaction: Force of Will, Force of Negation, Daze, Solitude
- Mana denial: Wasteland (48% of decks), Daze, Rishadan Port, Blood Moon

### All Strategies Are Viable
The format supports the full spectrum:
- **Tempo**: Dimir Tempo, Izzet Delver — cheap threats + free countermagic + mana denial
- **Combo**: Oops! All Spells (turn-1 kills), Storm, Sneak and Show, Doomsday
- **Control**: Beanstalk Control, Jeskai Control, UW Miracles
- **Prison/Stompy**: Eldrazi, Red Stompy, Affinity — Chalice of the Void + Ancient Tomb locks
- **Midrange**: Ocelot Pride Midrange, Jund — value-oriented and grindy
- **Land-based**: Lands — unique strategy using Exploration, Life from the Loam, and land-based win conditions
- **Tribal**: Goblins, Elves, Merfolk — synergy-driven creature strategies

### The Reserved List
Dual lands (Underground Sea, Volcanic Island, Tundra, etc.) are on the Reserved List and can never be reprinted. These are format staples that provide two colors of mana with no drawback, and they drive much of Legacy's cost. An Underground Sea is typically $500-800+.

### Format Health
The March 2026 B&R announcement made **no changes**, citing balanced metagame health. No single deck exceeds 55% win rate. The format has ~54 distinct competitive archetypes. The most recent ban was Entomb (November 2025), which neutered Reanimator as a tier 1 strategy.

## Cost and Accessibility

Legacy is one of the most expensive paper formats due to Reserved List cards:
- **Budget option**: Oops! All Spells (~$1,100), Burn (~$1,500)
- **Mid-range**: Most competitive decks cost $3,000-$5,000
- **Expensive**: Lands (~$8,300), 4-Color Control (~$6,000+)
- **MTGO**: Significantly cheaper (~300-650 tix)
- **Proxies**: Many local game stores allow proxied Legacy events

### Play Platforms
- **Paper (LGS)**: Local game stores run Legacy events, some allow proxies
- **MTGO**: Magic Online has active Legacy leagues and challenges
- **SpellTable**: Webcam play for paper collections
- **Cockatrice / Moxfield Playtest**: Free online alternatives

## The Strategic Triangle

Legacy archetypes exist in a rough metagame triangle (from Reid Duke):

```
        Fair Blue (Delver, UWx Control)
       /                                \
      / beats                    beats   \
     /                                    \
  Combo  ←————— beats ————————  Fair Nonblue
  (Storm, S&T, Oops)           (D&T, Stompy, Jund)
```

- **Fair Blue** beats **Combo** (free countermagic disrupts combo turns)
- **Fair Nonblue** beats **Fair Blue** (hatebears and mana denial punish cantrip-reliant strategies)
- **Combo** beats **Fair Nonblue** (no free countermagic means combo goes off unimpeded)

This is a simplification — individual matchups vary greatly — but it captures the macro-level strategic dynamics that drive Legacy metagame shifts.
