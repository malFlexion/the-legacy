## 1. Delver / Tempo

**Strategy**: Deploy cheap, efficient threats and protect them with free countermagic and efficient removal while denying resources with [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22).
**Colors**: Blue + 1-3 splash colors (most commonly Black)
**Tier**: 1
**Meta Share**: ~23% (combined family)

### Overview

The Delver family has defined Legacy for over a decade. The core game plan is deceptively simple: land a cheap, evasive threat on turn one or two, then leverage free counterspells like [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) and [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) alongside [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) to strand the opponent on resources while your threat closes the game. This "protect the queen" strategy is the most proven and refined approach to Legacy, and the archetype consistently represents the largest collective metagame share.

The printing of [Murktide Regent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Murktide+Regent%22) in Modern Horizons 2 and the subsequent additions from Modern Horizons 3 (particularly [Psychic Frog](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Psychic+Frog%22)) reshaped the family dramatically. Psychic Frog's ability to grow, gain evasion, and protect itself via the graveyard pushed Dimir colorings to the forefront. The loss of [Expressive Iteration](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Expressive+Iteration%22) to banning further cemented UB as the premier tempo shell, though Izzet variants remain competitive through different card advantage engines.

Today, Dimir Tempo (sometimes called "Shadow" or "Dimir Scam" depending on the threat suite) is the clear frontrunner at 14.6% metagame share, making it the single most played deck in Legacy. The archetype rewards tight play, metagame knowledge, and precise sequencing. Mastering the Delver family is often considered the best way to learn Legacy fundamentals.

### Variants

#### Dimir Tempo (UB, Tier 1 -- 14.6%)

The dominant tempo shell in Legacy. Dimir Tempo pairs [Psychic Frog](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Psychic+Frog%22) and [Murktide Regent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Murktide+Regent%22) as its primary threats alongside the full suite of black disruption: [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22), [Fatal Push](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Fatal+Push%22), and often [Snuff Out](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Snuff+Out%22) for free removal. The discard-based disruption gives the deck a proactive angle that red removal-based builds lack, letting it surgically remove combo pieces or opposing answers before deploying threats.

The deck's mana base is clean and punishing -- running a high [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) count is easy without a third color. Some builds include [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22) as a versatile two-drop that punishes cantrips and provides board presence. The addition of [Psychic Frog](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Psychic+Frog%22) in MH3 was transformative: it gives the deck a threat that grows, dodges removal via its activated ability, gains flying when needed, and fuels Murktide by filling the graveyard. Typical lists run 4 Psychic Frog, 3-4 [Murktide Regent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Murktide+Regent%22), 4 [Delver of Secrets](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Delver+of+Secrets%22), and sometimes 2-3 Orcish Bowmasters.

Choose Dimir when: You want the most consistent, proven tempo shell with the best disruption-to-threat ratio. Best in open metagames and when combo is prevalent.

#### Izzet Delver (UR, Tier 2 -- 4.3%)

The classic red splash gives access to [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22) and occasionally [Chain Lightning](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chain+Lightning%22) or [Unholy Heat](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Unholy+Heat%22) for removal that doubles as reach. Izzet trades the proactive disruption of [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) for a faster clock and the ability to burn opponents out from moderate life totals. [Dragon's Rage Channeler](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dragon%27s+Rage+Channeler%22) often appears as the one-drop of choice alongside Delver, providing card selection via surveil and a reasonable threat once delirium is achieved.

Post-[Expressive Iteration](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Expressive+Iteration%22) ban, Izzet relies on cantrips and raw card quality rather than a dedicated card advantage engine. Some builds have adopted Of One Mind or other draw spells to fill the gap. The mana base is slightly less painful than Dimir (no [Watery Grave](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Watery+Grave%22) / [Underground Sea](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Underground+Sea%22) demands), and the red splash enables powerful sideboard options like [Pyroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyroblast%22), [Meltdown](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Meltdown%22), and [Smash to Smithereens](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Smash+to+Smithereens%22).

Choose Izzet when: Your metagame is creature-heavy and you want reach. [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22) is at its best against other Delver decks and small-creature strategies.

#### Sultai Tempo (BUG, Tier 2.5)

Sultai Tempo combines black disruption with green for [Tarmogoyf](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tarmogoyf%22) (still a reasonable beater in Legacy) and occasionally [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22) or [Sylvan Library](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sylvan+Library%22). This was the dominant Delver variant in the early 2020s but has fallen off significantly. The mana base is greedier than Dimir, and the green threats don't offer the same rate as [Psychic Frog](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Psychic+Frog%22) and Murktide.

Some pilots still favor the shell for [Abrupt Decay](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Abrupt+Decay%22) (uncounterable removal for [Counterbalance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Counterbalance%22), [Sylvan Library](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sylvan+Library%22), and other problematic permanents) and [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22) as maindeck graveyard hate. [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Life+from+the+Loam%22) packages occasionally appear in more midrange-leaning builds.

Choose Sultai when: You value [Abrupt Decay](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Abrupt+Decay%22) and natural graveyard interaction. Best in metagames with many permanent-based strategies.

#### Grixis Tempo (UBR, Tier 2.5)

Grixis combines the best of Dimir and Izzet: [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) plus [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22) alongside [Psychic Frog](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Psychic+Frog%22) and Murktide. The cost is a significantly greedier mana base that is more vulnerable to [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) and [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22). When it works, Grixis has the most versatile answers in the format. When it doesn't, it takes 4+ damage from its own lands and gets Wastelanded out of games.

Key cards unique to Grixis include Kolaghan's Command (card advantage and artifact destruction), and the sideboard gains access to both [Pyroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyroblast%22) and [Surgical Extraction](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Surgical+Extraction%22) / [Leyline of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Leyline+of+the+Void%22). Some builds lean into a [Young Pyromancer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Young+Pyromancer%22) plan for go-wide capabilities.

Choose Grixis when: You expect a diverse metagame and want access to every answer. Best when [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) decks are less prevalent.

#### Temur Tempo / Canadian Threshold (RUG, Tier 3)

The original "good stuff" Delver variant and one of Legacy's most storied archetypes. Canadian Threshold (named for its origin in the Canadian Magic community) pairs Nimble Mongoose and [Tarmogoyf](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tarmogoyf%22) with [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22) and the full cantrip/free counter suite. While outclassed by modern threats, dedicated pilots continue to put up results.

The deck has adapted by incorporating [Murktide Regent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Murktide+Regent%22) (somewhat awkwardly alongside Nimble Mongoose's threshold requirement) and sometimes [Dragon's Rage Channeler](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dragon%27s+Rage+Channeler%22). [Stifle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stifle%22) occasionally appears as additional mana denial alongside [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22). The green splash also provides [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22) and occasionally Klothys, God of Destiny from the sideboard.

Choose Temur when: You're a nostalgic pilot who values Nimble Mongoose's shroud and a proactive threat suite. Best against removal-heavy metagames.

#### Jeskai Delver / Murktide (UWR, Tier 3)

White offers [Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22) (the best single-target removal spell in Legacy) and sideboard access to cards like [Prismatic Ending](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Prismatic+Ending%22), Lavinia, Azorius Renegade, and [Meddling Mage](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Meddling+Mage%22). Jeskai Delver aims to combine Izzet's reach with white's unconditional answers.

The mana base is the biggest challenge: supporting WW, RR, and UU costs is nearly impossible alongside [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22). Most builds accept that they can't aggressively Wasteland and instead play a slightly more midrange game. [Monastery Mentor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Monastery+Mentor%22) sometimes appears as a top-end threat that generates tokens alongside the cheap spell suite.

Choose Jeskai when: You want [Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22) and don't mind a less aggressive mana denial plan.

#### Esper Tempo (UWB, Tier 3)

Esper Tempo pairs white's premium removal ([Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22), [Prismatic Ending](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Prismatic+Ending%22)) with black's disruption ([Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22)) in a tempo shell. This variant sometimes leans midrange, running [Stoneforge Mystic](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stoneforge+Mystic%22) alongside [Delver of Secrets](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Delver+of+Secrets%22) for a dual-axis threat profile.

The tension between "be aggressive with Delver/[Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22)" and "grind with Stoneforge/[Batterskull](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Batterskull%22)" makes Esper the most challenging tempo variant to build correctly. Successful lists tend to commit to one axis more than the other.

Choose Esper when: You want the most versatile individual card quality and don't mind sacrificing some consistency.

#### Mono Blue Tempo (U, Tier 3)

Mono Blue Tempo strips the archetype to its purest form: Delver, Murktide, cantrips, and free counters with a mana base entirely of Islands and [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22). This makes the deck essentially immune to Wasteland and [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22) while maximizing [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) and [Back to Basics](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Back+to+Basics%22) from the sideboard.

Without colored removal, the deck relies on [Dismember](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dismember%22) (or no removal at all) and [Subtlety](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Subtlety%22) as its answers to resolved threats. The lack of [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) means combo matchups can be rougher game one. Some builds include Brazen Borrower or Flicker of Fate as additional interaction.

Choose Mono Blue when: Your metagame is full of [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) and [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22) decks.

#### UW Delver (UW, Tier 3)

A streamlined two-color build that gains [Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22) over Mono Blue while keeping a clean mana base. Occasionally runs [Stoneforge Mystic](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stoneforge+Mystic%22), blurring the line with Stoneblade strategies. [Teferi, Time Raveler](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Teferi%2C+Time+Raveler%22) out of the sideboard is a potent tool against other blue decks.

Choose UW when: You want [Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22) with minimal mana base damage.

#### Stryfo Pile (4C, Tier 3)

Named after MTGO player Stryfo, this is the ultimate "good cards" deck -- a four-color tempo/midrange hybrid that plays the best card from every color. Typically UBrg or UBRw, it leverages [Deathrite Shaman](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Deathrite+Shaman%22)'s fixing (in pre-ban eras) or careful mana base construction to access [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22), [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22), [Abrupt Decay](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Abrupt+Decay%22), and sometimes [Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22) alongside the blue tempo core.

In the current era, Stryfo Pile variants tend to lean into three colors with a light splash for the fourth. The deck rewards extensive format knowledge and is popular among veteran players who want maximum flexibility.

Choose Stryfo Pile when: You know the metagame intimately and want a surgical answer for every matchup.

#### 4 Color Tempo (UBRG/WUBR, Tier 3)

A catch-all category for tempo decks that splash a fourth color for specific cards. Commonly splashing green for [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22) and [Uro, Titan of Nature's Wrath](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Uro%2C+Titan+of+Nature%27s+Wrath%22), or white for [Prismatic Ending](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Prismatic+Ending%22) and [Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22). These builds sacrifice mana base consistency for raw card quality.

Choose 4C Tempo when: You want a specific hate card that requires a fourth color and can accept the mana base risk.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Lands ([Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) + pressure is hard for them; [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) taxes their slower plays), Elves (counterspells disrupt their combo; pressure closes before they rebuild), Painter (fragile combo that folds to [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) + Daze), Oops/Belcher (free counters are devastating against all-in combo)
- **Unfavorable**: 4C Control / Beanstalk (grinds harder; card advantage eventually overwhelms your threats), Mono Red Prison ([Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22) + [Chalice of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chalice+of+the+Void%22) shuts down your entire strategy), Death and Taxes (Thalia taxes your spells; [Aether Vial](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Aether+Vial%22) dodges counters; equipment outclasses your creatures), Eldrazi (Chalice on 1 is game over; [Thought-Knot Seer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thought-Knot+Seer%22) strips key cards)
- **Even**: Other Delver variants (tempo mirrors are highly play/draw dependent), Reanimator (depends on disruption drawn; strong but not dominant), [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) (Force + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) is good but they can power through), [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) Midrange (close matchup that depends on builds and sideboard plans)

## 2. Ocelot Pride / Energy

**Strategy**: Deploy efficient creatures that generate token value and snowball board presence through energy and triggered abilities.
**Colors**: White + Red/Black (primarily Mardu, Boros, or Naya)
**Tier**: 1
**Meta Share**: ~15% (combined family)

### Overview

[Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22)'s arrival in Modern Horizons 3 introduced one of the most efficient creature engines Legacy has ever seen. The one-mana 1/1 with lifelink that creates 1/1 Cat tokens whenever you gain life with another permanent became the centerpiece of an entirely new archetype family. Combined with the energy package -- [Amped Raptor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Amped+Raptor%22), [Galvanic Discharge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Galvanic+Discharge%22), [Guide of Souls](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Guide+of+Souls%22) -- the Ocelot Pride decks represent a paradigm shift: fair creature decks that can match blue tempo strategies in efficiency while going wider.

The energy mechanic provides both card selection (via [Amped Raptor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Amped+Raptor%22)'s cascade-like ability) and removal ([Galvanic Discharge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Galvanic+Discharge%22) scales with accumulated energy) without requiring blue cards. This gives white-based creature decks a form of card advantage and interaction that previously required splashing blue. The result is a creature strategy that doesn't fold to a single removal spell because every card generates value.

The archetype rose rapidly through 2025, with [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) Midrange establishing itself as the second-most-played archetype behind Dimir Tempo. Mardu builds incorporating Bowmasters and black disruption compete with leaner Boros and Naya configurations. The deck's ability to pivot between aggressive starts (turn 1 Ocelot Pride into turn 2 [Guide of Souls](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Guide+of+Souls%22)) and grindy midrange games makes it versatile across metagames.

| Volatile Stormdrake | Evasive energy threat; steals opposing creatures |
| [Phlage, Titan of Fire's Fury](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Phlage%2C+Titan+of+Fire%27s+Fury%22) | (Red/White builds) Recursive threat; gains life to trigger Ocelot |
| Phelia, Exuberant Shepherd | Flicker engine; resets ETB creatures, generates additional value |

### Variants

#### Ocelot Pride Midrange (WRx, Tier 1 -- 8.5%)

The default configuration, typically Naya or Boros with a light splash. This build maximizes the energy package alongside [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) and [Guide of Souls](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Guide+of+Souls%22). The midrange designation comes from its ability to play a longer game than pure aggro builds -- [Amped Raptor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Amped+Raptor%22) provides card advantage, [Galvanic Discharge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Galvanic+Discharge%22) scales into late-game removal, and the token generation creates inevitability.

Typical lists run the full 4 [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22), 4 [Guide of Souls](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Guide+of+Souls%22), 4 [Amped Raptor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Amped+Raptor%22), and 3-4 [Galvanic Discharge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Galvanic+Discharge%22) alongside efficient creatures like [Ajani, Nacatl Pariah](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ajani%2C+Nacatl+Pariah%22) and sometimes Phelia, Exuberant Shepherd. The mana base leverages Prismatic Vista and basic [Plains](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Plains%22)/[Mountain](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mountain%22) to play through [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) and [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22). [Solitude](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Solitude%22) occasionally appears as a free removal spell that triggers Ocelot Pride's lifelink clause.

Choose this when: You want the most streamlined, proven Ocelot build with the highest floor.

#### Mardu Energy / Aggro (WBR, Tier 2 -- 3%)

Adding black gives access to [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22), [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22), and sometimes [Dark Confidant](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Confidant%22) or [Grief](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grief%22). The Mardu build trades some energy consistency for proactive disruption and the format's best two-drop creature (Bowmasters). The interaction between Bowmasters' token generation and [Guide of Souls](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Guide+of+Souls%22) is particularly strong.

Some Mardu lists lean aggressive with Goblin Bombardment as a sacrifice outlet that converts tokens into direct damage, while others play a more controlling game with [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) and Vindicate. The mana base is greedier than Boros, making the deck more vulnerable to [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22).

Choose Mardu when: You want hand disruption and Bowmasters, especially in blue-heavy metagames where Bowmasters punishes cantrips.

#### Boros Energy / Aggro / Ocelot (WR, Tier 2)

The lean two-color version that maximizes mana consistency and [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) usage. Without a third color, Boros can run 3-4 Wasteland and punish greedy mana bases while curving out with efficient creatures. [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22) and sometimes [Chain Lightning](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chain+Lightning%22) provide reach to close games.

Boros builds often include Forth Eorlingas! as a card advantage source that also generates tokens for the Ocelot/Guide engine. The lower curve and cleaner mana make this the most aggressive variant.

Choose Boros when: You want maximum aggression and mana consistency.

#### Naya Energy (WRG, Tier 2)

Green adds [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22) (graveyard hate that's also a 3/4 creature), [Sythis, Harvest's Hand](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sythis%2C+Harvest%27s+Hand%22) (in enchantment-heavy builds), and occasionally [Knight of the Reliquary](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Knight+of+the+Reliquary%22) or Questing Beast. Some Naya builds incorporate a light [Green Sun's Zenith](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Green+Sun%27s+Zenith%22) toolbox for creature tutoring.

The most notable green inclusion is often simply [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22) in the sideboard, making some "Naya" decks effectively Boros with a green splash. Deeper green builds sometimes use Collected Company to generate explosive board states.

Choose Naya when: You value [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22)'s maindeckable graveyard hate and want a slightly bigger creature profile.

#### Jeskai Ocelot / Kitten / Pride (WUR, Tier 2.5)

The blue splash is controversial but powerful: it adds [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22), [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22), and sometimes [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) to the creature shell. This creates tension -- the deck wants to play creatures on curve but also wants to hold up free counters. Successful Jeskai builds tend to be more midrange, using cantrips to find the right half of the deck for each matchup.

[Teferi, Time Raveler](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Teferi%2C+Time+Raveler%22) from the sideboard is a powerful addition, and some builds run Volatile Stormdrake as an evasive energy threat that can steal opposing creatures. [Narset, Parter of Veils](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Narset%2C+Parter+of+Veils%22) occasionally appears as a card advantage hoser.

Choose Jeskai when: You can't imagine playing Legacy without [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22) and want to hedge against combo.

#### Azorius Ocelot (WU, Tier 3)

A cleaner blue-white build that pairs the Ocelot engine with [Stoneforge Mystic](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stoneforge+Mystic%22) and occasionally Spell Queller. This variant blurs the line between [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) strategies and traditional Stoneblade. The equipment package ([Kaldra Compleat](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Kaldra+Compleat%22), [Batterskull](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Batterskull%22)) provides an alternative threat axis that doesn't rely on the token engine.

Choose Azorius when: You want a Stoneblade-adjacent strategy with [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) as an additional angle.

#### Esper Ocelot (WUB, Tier 3)

The greediest blue-based Ocelot build, adding [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22) and [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) to the Azorius shell. Powerful when the mana works but significantly more vulnerable to [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22). Occasionally seen with Raffine, Scheming Seer as a threat that provides card selection and grows the team.

Choose Esper when: You want every powerful card regardless of mana base cost.

#### Orzhov Ocelot (WB, Tier 3)

A two-color build focused on the lifegain/token synergies with black's disruption. [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) and [Dark Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Ritual%22) occasionally enable explosive starts, and Bitter Triumph provides another removal option. [Scrubland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Scrubland%22) is underplayed in Legacy, making this a budget-friendly entry point.

Choose Orzhov when: You want [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) without the mana base strain of a third color.

#### Mardu Channeler / Inti / Legends / Tokens / Sacrifice (WBR variants, Tier 2.5-3)

A family of Mardu builds that de-emphasize the energy package in favor of other synergies. [Fable of the Mirror-Breaker](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Fable+of+the+Mirror-Breaker%22) (creating treasure tokens and copying creatures), Inti, Seneschal of the Sun (rewarding discard with impulse draw and +1/+1 counters), and various legendary creatures define these builds.

Sacrifice-oriented versions run Goblin Bombardment and Blood Artist effects to convert tokens into damage. Legends-matter builds use Mox Amber with a high legendary count for mana acceleration. Token builds focus on [Bitterblossom](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Bitterblossom%22) and Lingering Souls for persistent board presence. These are meta-call variants that exploit specific weaknesses.

Choose these when: You have a specific metagame read that rewards a less conventional approach.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Storm / ANT (fast clock + Thalia/[Mindbreak Trap](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mindbreak+Trap%22) from sideboard; they can't easily combo through a wide board), Lands (faster clock; [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Depths%22) combo is too slow), Dredge (mainboard lifegain races; sideboard hate seals it), Painter (wider board than they can handle; [Galvanic Discharge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Galvanic+Discharge%22) answers key pieces)

## 3. Show and Tell

**Strategy**: Cheat a game-ending permanent into play using [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) or [Sneak Attack](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sneak+Attack%22), bypassing mana costs entirely.
**Colors**: Blue + Red (primarily UR)
**Tier**: 1
**Meta Share**: ~10% (combined family)

### Overview

[Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) is Legacy's premier "big mana" combo strategy. The plan is elegant: cast Show and Tell (a three-mana sorcery that lets each player put a permanent from their hand into play), revealing either [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn%22) or [Omniscience](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Omniscience%22) while the opponent puts in something dramatically less impactful. The deck uses the blue cantrip suite ([Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22), [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22), [Preordain](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Preordain%22)) to assemble its combo while protecting it with [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) and other countermagic.

The archetype splits into two major branches: Sneak and Show (which also runs [Sneak Attack](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sneak+Attack%22) as a redundant cheat mechanism) and Omni-Tell (which focuses exclusively on [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) into [Omniscience](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Omniscience%22), then casts spells for free). Sneak and Show has historically been the more popular and consistent version because Sneak Attack provides a backup plan that plays differently enough to dodge some hate cards.

[Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) has been a Legacy staple since the early 2010s and remains one of the most effective combo strategies. Its resilience comes from running the same free countermagic suite as blue tempo decks -- [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22), [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) (in some builds), and [Flusterstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Flusterstorm%22) protect the combo. The deck's biggest vulnerability is that Show and Tell is symmetrical: opponents can put in their own threats ([Archon of Cruelty](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Archon+of+Cruelty%22), [Atraxa, Grand Unifier](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Atraxa%2C+Grand+Unifier%22)) or lock pieces ([Containment Priest](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Containment+Priest%22), [Dress Down](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dress+Down%22)). Experienced Show and Tell pilots learn to play around these possibilities through careful cantripping and timing.

### Variants

#### Sneak and Show (UR, Tier 1 -- 6.7%)

The flagship variant runs both [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) and [Sneak Attack](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sneak+Attack%22) as cheat mechanisms, with [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn%22) and [Griselbrand](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Griselbrand%22) as the primary payoffs. The redundancy is the deck's greatest strength: even if the opponent has an answer for Show and Tell (like [Containment Priest](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Containment+Priest%22)), Sneak Attack operates on a completely different axis. Conversely, Sneak Attack blanks hate like putting your own creature into play off their Show and Tell.

The typical Sneak and Show list runs 4 [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22), 4 [Sneak Attack](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sneak+Attack%22), 4 Emrakul, 3-4 [Griselbrand](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Griselbrand%22), fast mana ([Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22), [City of Traitors](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22City+of+Traitors%22), [Lotus Petal](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lotus+Petal%22), [Simian Spirit Guide](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Simian+Spirit+Guide%22)), and the full blue cantrip/counter suite. Some builds include [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22) in the sideboard as a devastating plan B that locks non-basic-heavy opponents out of the game entirely. The Sol land mana base means the deck can cast Blood Moon off its own lands without issue.

The deck typically kills on turns 2-4. Turn 1 kills are possible ([Lotus Petal](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lotus+Petal%22) + [Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22) + [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22)) but rare. The play pattern is often: cantrip on turn 1, cantrip on turn 2 while holding [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22), resolve Show and Tell on turn 3 with protection.

#### Omni-Tell (U, Tier 2 -- 1.7%)

Omni-Tell focuses exclusively on [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) into [Omniscience](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Omniscience%22), then wins deterministically by casting spells for free. The kill typically involves casting [Cunning Wish](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Cunning+Wish%22) for a win condition (often [Release the Ants](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Release+the+Ants%22) via repeated clash wins, Petals of Insight looping, or Enter the Infinite into Jace, Wielder of Mysteries) or simply casting Emrakul for free.

The mono-blue-ish mana base (sometimes splashing red for sideboard cards) makes the deck more resilient to [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) and [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22) than Sneak and Show. However, losing [Sneak Attack](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sneak+Attack%22) means the deck is more vulnerable to permanent-based hate like [Containment Priest](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Containment+Priest%22) and to opponents putting in problematic permanents off the symmetrical [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22).

Omni-Tell compensates with a more controlling game plan: [Defense Grid](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Defense+Grid%22), [Flusterstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Flusterstorm%22), and sometimes [Teferi, Time Raveler](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Teferi%2C+Time+Raveler%22) protect the combo from both counters and flash-based interaction. The deck is more consistent than Sneak and Show (it only needs two types of cards: [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) + [Omniscience](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Omniscience%22)) but less resilient to disruption.

Choose Omni-Tell when: You want a more deterministic combo with fewer dead draws (no Sneak Attacks that need red mana).

#### Show and Copy (UR, Tier 3)

A niche variant that uses [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) to put Emrakul into play, then copies it with cards like Spark Double or Clone effects. Some versions use [Dress Down](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dress+Down%22) to neutralize opposing threats put into play off Show and Tell's symmetry before flickering or cloning their own Emrakul.

Choose Show and Copy when: You want to hedge against opponents exploiting [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22)'s symmetry.

#### Show and Karn (UR + artifacts, Tier 3)

A hybrid build that combines [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) with [Karn, the Great Creator](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Karn%2C+the+Great+Creator%22)'s artifact toolbox. Karn can be put into play off Show and Tell to immediately fetch a [Mycosynth Lattice](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mycosynth+Lattice%22) from the sideboard, locking the opponent out of activated abilities. Alternatively, the deck can Show in [Omniscience](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Omniscience%22) or Emrakul as a traditional win.

Choose Show and Karn when: You want a lock-piece plan B that doesn't require attacking.

#### Sneak Attack (Mono Red / Rx, Tier 3)

Pure [Sneak Attack](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sneak+Attack%22) builds without [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22), typically in mono-red or red-heavy shells. These decks use fast mana ([Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22), [City of Traitors](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22City+of+Traitors%22), [Simian Spirit Guide](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Simian+Spirit+Guide%22), [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Seething+Song%22)) to rush out Sneak Attack and put Emrakul or Worldspine Wurm into play. Without blue cantrips or counters, the deck is less consistent but faster and immune to blue-based hate.

Some builds include [Through the Breach](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Through+the+Breach%22) as an additional one-shot cheat spell. Mono Red [Sneak Attack](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sneak+Attack%22) is largely outclassed by Sneak and Show but occasionally appears as a meta call when blue hate is high.

Choose [Sneak Attack](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sneak+Attack%22) when: You want to dodge all blue hate cards and don't mind lower consistency.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Lands (they can't interact with the combo meaningfully; too slow), Death and Taxes (Thalia is manageable with Sol lands; they lack hard counters), Elves (faster and doesn't care about their board), [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) (faster goldfish; they lack counterspells in most builds), Stompy / Chalice decks ([Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) costs 3, dodging Chalice on 1 and 2)
- **Unfavorable**: Dimir Tempo ([Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) + [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) + fast clock is the nightmare), Delver variants (free counters + pressure is the best way to beat combo), Beanstalk Control (Force of Will + [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22) + their own [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) targets like Atraxa), Reanimator (faster, and they can reanimate your own [Griselbrand](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Griselbrand%22) if it dies)
- **Even**: Storm (both are fast combo; comes down to interaction and who's on the play), Other combo (Oops, [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) -- racing matchups), 8-Cast (Chalice is annoying but not backbreaking; [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) fights work)

## 4. Oops! All Spells / Belcher

**Strategy**: Win the game on turn one or two by exploiting a deckbuilding restriction -- playing zero or near-zero lands to enable explosive combo kills.
**Colors**: Varies (WUBRG / RG / U)
**Tier**: 1 (Oops), 2 (Belcher)
**Meta Share**: ~10% (combined family)

### Overview

"Oops! All Spells" and [Goblin Charbelcher](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Goblin+Charbelcher%22) represent Legacy's all-in combo extreme. These decks aim to win on turn one with startling regularity, leveraging the most explosive mana acceleration in the format ([Dark Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Ritual%22), Cabal Ritual, [Elvish Spirit Guide](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Elvish+Spirit+Guide%22), [Simian Spirit Guide](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Simian+Spirit+Guide%22), [Lotus Petal](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lotus+Petal%22), [Chrome Mox](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chrome+Mox%22), [Mox Opal](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mox+Opal%22), [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond%22)) alongside zero-land or near-zero-land deck construction.

The "Oops" archetype runs literally zero lands, instead using the modal double-faced cards from Zendikar Rising ([Agadeem's Awakening](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Agadeem%27s+Awakening%22), [Sea Gate Restoration](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sea+Gate+Restoration%22), [Turntimber Symbiosis](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Turntimber+Symbiosis%22), etc.) and Baldur's Gate (various) as both spells and "lands." This allows [Balustrade Spy](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Balustrade+Spy%22) or [Undercity Informer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Undercity+Informer%22) to mill the entire library when they enter the battlefield (since there are no lands to stop the mill), enabling a win through [Thassa's Oracle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thassa%27s+Oracle%22) or reanimation of multiple creatures. The deck's turn-one win rate is remarkably high -- over 60% on the play in many configurations.

[Goblin Charbelcher](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Goblin+Charbelcher%22) takes a different approach: it runs zero or one land (often a single [Taiga](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Taiga%22) to enable [Land Grant](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Land+Grant%22)) and activates Goblin Charbelcher to deal lethal damage. With no lands in the deck (or with the single land on the bottom from a careful setup), Charbelcher reveals the entire library, dealing damage equal to the number of cards revealed. This is typically 40+ damage -- more than enough. Belcher decks can also win via [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Empty+the+Warrens%22), creating 14+ Goblin tokens on turn one.

These decks are the ultimate glass cannons: devastatingly fast but highly vulnerable to disruption. A single [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) on the key spell often ends the game, and mulligan decisions are agonizing. Despite this fragility, the archetype's pure speed and the difficulty of consistently having Force of Will on turn one make it a perpetual metagame force.

### Variants

#### Oops! All Spells (WUBRG, Tier 1 -- 8.2%)

The premier glass cannon of Legacy. Oops plays zero true lands, instead using MDFCs ([Agadeem's Awakening](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Agadeem%27s+Awakening%22), [Sea Gate Restoration](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sea+Gate+Restoration%22), [Turntimber Symbiosis](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Turntimber+Symbiosis%22), Shatterskull Smashing, Emeria's Call, etc.) that function as enters-tapped lands when needed but count as spells for [Balustrade Spy](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Balustrade+Spy%22) and [Undercity Informer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Undercity+Informer%22)'s mill ability.

The turn-one line is typically: fast mana (Ritual, Petal, Spirit Guide) into [Balustrade Spy](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Balustrade+Spy%22) or [Undercity Informer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Undercity+Informer%22), mill entire library, then win via one of several paths. The most common is [Thassa's Oracle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thassa%27s+Oracle%22) (devotion check with empty library). Backup lines include reanimating multiple creatures ([Dread Return](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dread+Return%22) sacrificing [Narcomoeba](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Narcomoeba%22) tokens that entered from the mill) to flashback Dread Return targeting Thassa's Oracle.

The deck has evolved significantly with printings like [Grief](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grief%22) (free hand disruption to clear [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) before going off) and [Pact of Negation](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pact+of+Negation%22) for protection. Some builds include Grief + Salvage in the sideboard for a more disruptive post-board plan. The "Spy" variant focuses on [Balustrade Spy](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Balustrade+Spy%22), while the "Informer" variant emphasizes [Undercity Informer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Undercity+Informer%22) -- most lists run both for redundancy.

Recent innovations include using [Grief](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grief%22) to strip opposing [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) before comboing, and [Cabal Therapy](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Cabal+Therapy%22) (flashed back off [Narcomoeba](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Narcomoeba%22) tokens) as additional disruption in the graveyard-based kill chain.

#### Goblin Charbelcher (RG, Tier 2)

The original land-light combo deck. Belcher runs zero or one land (a single [Taiga](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Taiga%22) found by [Land Grant](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Land+Grant%22)) and aims to activate [Goblin Charbelcher](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Goblin+Charbelcher%22) to reveal the entire deck and deal 40+ damage. The deck wins either by resolving Charbelcher and activating it or by generating a massive storm count and casting [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Empty+the+Warrens%22).

The Belcher game plan is more straightforward than Oops but also more fragile: Charbelcher costs 4 mana and then requires another activation, making it slower than [Balustrade Spy](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Balustrade+Spy%22). However, Belcher compensates with [Burning Wish](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Burning+Wish%22), which provides access to a versatile sideboard toolbox including [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Empty+the+Warrens%22), [Shattering Spree](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Shattering+Spree%22), and [Tendrils of Agony](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tendrils+of+Agony%22).

The mana production is similar to Oops: [Dark Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Ritual%22), Cabal Ritual, [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond%22), [Lotus Petal](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lotus+Petal%22), [Chrome Mox](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chrome+Mox%22), Elvish/[Simian Spirit Guide](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Simian+Spirit+Guide%22), [Rite of Flame](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rite+of+Flame%22), [Desperate Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Desperate+Ritual%22), and sometimes [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Seething+Song%22). [Land Grant](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Land+Grant%22) and Gitaxian Probe (where legal) provide free spells that increase storm count while finding the single [Taiga](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Taiga%22).

Choose Belcher when: You want more sideboard flexibility ([Burning Wish](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Burning+Wish%22) toolbox) and don't mind being slightly slower than Oops.

#### Mono Blue Belcher (U, Tier 3)

A fundamentally different take on the Charbelcher concept. Instead of fast mana rituals, Mono Blue Belcher uses [Goblin Charbelcher](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Goblin+Charbelcher%22) alongside Sevinne's Reclamation and Mana Severance effects to remove all lands from the library. Alternatively, some builds use Paradigm Shift to literally replace their library with their graveyard (which contains no lands after setup).

The blue shell provides [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22), [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22), [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22), and [Cunning Wish](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Cunning+Wish%22) -- making the deck dramatically more interactive than traditional Belcher. The tradeoff is speed: Mono Blue Belcher is a turn 3-4 combo deck rather than a turn 1-2 deck. However, it can protect its combo with countermagic and is much more resilient to disruption.

Some Mono Blue Belcher builds use Thought Lash or Paradigm Shift to exile the library, then win with [Thassa's Oracle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thassa%27s+Oracle%22) -- essentially an Oops-style kill through a blue cantrip shell. The deck occasionally overlaps with "[Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22)" strategies in card choices.

Choose Mono Blue Belcher when: You want a Charbelcher deck that can interact with the opponent and play through disruption.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Lands (turn-one kill is way too fast; they have zero relevant interaction game 1), Death and Taxes (Thalia is too slow on the draw; Vial doesn't help against combo), Eldrazi (Chalice on 1 is too slow; they can't interact turn 0), Stompy / Prison (must have the lock piece turn 1 on the play, otherwise dead), [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) (no blue interaction; too slow to race)
- **Unfavorable**: Dimir Tempo ([Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) + [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) is 8-12 pieces of interaction), Izzet Delver (Force + Daze; they're fast enough to punish a stumble), 4C Control (Force + [Flusterstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Flusterstorm%22) + multiple layers of interaction), Any blue deck with a reasonable clock (the fundamental weakness of all-in combo)
- **Even**: [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) (racing combo matchup; whoever has Force wins), Storm (similar speed; interaction quality determines the winner), Reanimator (both are fast combo; [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) checks decide games)

## 5. Storm

**Strategy**: Generate a high storm count through ritual-based mana acceleration and card draw, then win with a lethal storm spell like [Tendrils of Agony](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tendrils+of+Agony%22) or a massive [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Empty+the+Warrens%22).
**Colors**: Black + Red/Blue (primarily BRu, Br, UR)
**Tier**: 1.5
**Meta Share**: ~5% (combined family)

### Overview

Storm is Legacy's iconic deterministic combo archetype. Unlike [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) or Oops, which cheat permanents into play, Storm wins by casting a critical mass of spells in a single turn and finishing with [Tendrils of Agony](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tendrils+of+Agony%22) (draining 2 life per spell cast that turn) or [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Empty+the+Warrens%22) (creating 2 Goblin tokens per spell cast). The archetype demands mastery of sequencing, resource management, and risk assessment -- a single misstep in the combo turn can strand you with an unfinished kill and no resources.

The Storm family has branched into multiple distinct engines. The EPIC Storm (TES) and [Ad Nauseam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ad+Nauseam%22) Tendrils (ANT) represent the black ritual-based approach, using [Dark Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Ritual%22), Cabal Ritual, and [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond%22) for explosive mana generation. Ruby Storm takes a fundamentally different route, using red rituals ([Rite of Flame](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rite+of+Flame%22), [Desperate Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Desperate+Ritual%22), [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Seething+Song%22)) and [Past in Flames](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Past+in+Flames%22) or [Birgi, God of Storytelling](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Birgi%2C+God+of+Storytelling%22) to chain spells. Newer variants like Beseech Storm leverage [Beseech the Mirror](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Beseech+the+Mirror%22) as a powerful tutor that can also exploit Lion's Eye Diamond's mana. Necro Storm uses Necropotence for massive card draw to fuel the combo turn.

Storm rewards deep format knowledge -- knowing when to "go for it" versus when to wait is the defining skill. The archetype consistently punishes unprepared metagames and remains one of Legacy's most feared game-one strategies.

| [Birgi, God of Storytelling](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Birgi%2C+God+of+Storytelling%22) | (Ruby Storm) Adds R whenever you cast a spell; enables chaining |
| [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Empty+the+Warrens%22) | Backup win; creates a goblin army via storm |
| [Lotus Petal](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lotus+Petal%22) | Free mana acceleration and storm count |
| [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22) | Card selection; sets up combo turns and hides key cards from discard |
| [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22) | Finds combo pieces; smooths draws |
| Necropotence | (Necro Storm) Massive card draw engine; pay life to draw your combo |

### Variants

#### The EPIC Storm / TES (BRu, Tier 2 -- 3.5%)

TES is the faster, more aggressive storm variant. It prioritizes speed over resilience, aiming to combo on turns 1-2 with the help of [Burning Wish](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Burning+Wish%22), [Rite of Flame](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rite+of+Flame%22), and [Chrome Mox](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chrome+Mox%22) alongside the core black rituals. TES typically runs a light blue splash for [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22) and [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22), plus [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) or [Veil of Summer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Veil+of+Summer%22) for protection.

The defining card is [Burning Wish](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Burning+Wish%22), which provides access to a sideboard toolbox: [Tendrils of Agony](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tendrils+of+Agony%22), [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Empty+the+Warrens%22), [Echo of Eons](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Echo+of+Eons%22), [Peer into the Abyss](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Peer+into+the+Abyss%22), and various utility sorceries. This makes TES remarkably flexible post-board while maintaining a fast game-one kill. The deck also runs [Galvanic Relay](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Galvanic+Relay%22) as a value engine that stores spells in exile for the following turn, providing resilience against discard.

TES is more "all-in" than ANT but compensates with raw speed. A skilled TES pilot can navigate through disruption by recognizing when to pivot between Tendrils, [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Empty+the+Warrens%22), and the [Burning Wish](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Burning+Wish%22) toolbox.

Choose TES when: You want the fastest storm variant with the most flexibility in win conditions.

#### Ad Nauseam Tendrils / ANT (UBr, Tier 2)

ANT is the classic, methodical storm deck. It leans harder on the blue cantrip suite and uses [Ad Nauseam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ad+Nauseam%22) as its primary card draw engine, paying life to draw a massive hand of rituals and win conditions. ANT typically combos on turns 2-4, taking an extra turn to set up compared to TES but compensating with more consistency and interaction ([Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22), [Flusterstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Flusterstorm%22) from the sideboard).

The core combo line involves casting rituals to reach enough mana, resolving Infernal Tutor (with an empty hand via [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond%22)) to find [Ad Nauseam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ad+Nauseam%22) or [Tendrils of Agony](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tendrils+of+Agony%22), and finishing the opponent. [Past in Flames](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Past+in+Flames%22) provides a secondary engine that recasts rituals from the graveyard for a second wave of mana and storm count.

ANT's lower curve (no [Rite of Flame](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rite+of+Flame%22) or [Chrome Mox](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chrome+Mox%22)) means it takes less damage from [Ad Nauseam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ad+Nauseam%22), allowing deeper digs. The deck is more resilient to single pieces of disruption but slower against decks that can present a fast clock alongside interaction.

Choose ANT when: You prefer a more methodical, resilient storm deck that can play through disruption.

#### Ruby Storm (R/UR, Tier 2.5 -- 0.4%)

Ruby Storm takes a fundamentally different approach, eschewing black rituals entirely in favor of red mana acceleration and [Past in Flames](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Past+in+Flames%22) or [Birgi, God of Storytelling](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Birgi%2C+God+of+Storytelling%22) as the engine. The deck chains red rituals ([Rite of Flame](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rite+of+Flame%22), [Desperate Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Desperate+Ritual%22), [Seething Song](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Seething+Song%22), [Pyretic Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyretic+Ritual%22)) alongside cheap cantrips ([Manamorphose](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Manamorphose%22), [Faithless Looting](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Faithless+Looting%22)) to build storm count, then wins with [Grapeshot](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grapeshot%22) or [Empty the Warrens](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Empty+the+Warrens%22).

Birgi is the key innovation: with Birgi in play, every spell effectively costs 1 less (since it refunds R), turning cheap cantrips into free spells and rituals into even more explosive mana. [Past in Flames](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Past+in+Flames%22) gives the deck a second wave, recasting the entire graveyard. [Bonus Round](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Bonus+Round%22) is sometimes included as a spell-doubling effect that makes the combo turn nearly impossible to stop.

Ruby Storm's biggest advantage is that it dodges much of the hate aimed at traditional storm: it doesn't need [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond%22), doesn't care about graveyard hate until [Past in Flames](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Past+in+Flames%22), and doesn't use black mana (so opposing Pyroblasts are dead). The deck is, however, slower and more vulnerable to creature removal (Birgi dying is often game over).

Choose Ruby Storm when: You want a storm deck that attacks from a different angle and dodges traditional storm hate.

#### Beseech Storm (B/UB, Tier 2.5)

Beseech Storm leverages [Beseech the Mirror](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Beseech+the+Mirror%22) as a four-mana tutor with bargain -- sacrificing an artifact, enchantment, or token to cast the found card for free. Combined with [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond%22) (crack LED for mana, sacrifice it to bargain Beseech, find and cast Tendrils or [Ad Nauseam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ad+Nauseam%22) for free), this creates a compact and powerful combo. The deck often runs [Grim Monolith](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grim+Monolith%22) or other artifact mana to enable the bargain cost.

Choose Beseech Storm when: You want a compact combo engine built around a powerful tutor.

#### Izzet / UR Storm (UR, Tier 3)

UR Storm blends red rituals with blue cantrips and sometimes Gifts Ungiven or Fact or Fiction for card advantage. This variant plays more like a control-combo hybrid, using countermagic to survive the early game before assembling a combo turn. Some builds use Underworld Breach as a value engine alongside [Brain Freeze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brain+Freeze%22) for a mill-based win.

Choose UR Storm when: You want more interaction and a cantrip-heavy storm shell.

#### Necro Storm (UB, Tier 3)

Necro Storm uses Necropotence as a one-card combo engine: resolve Necropotence, draw 20+ cards, untap, and win with a massive hand full of rituals and Tendrils. The deck typically uses [Dark Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Ritual%22) to power out an early Necropotence and protects it with [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) and [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22). The challenge is surviving the turn you tap out for Necropotence.

Choose Necro Storm when: You want a storm deck built around a single powerful engine card.

#### Cascade Storm (URx, Tier 3)

Cascade Storm uses cascade spells ([Shardless Agent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Shardless+Agent%22), Violent Outburst, Demonic Dread) to chain into key combo pieces. By manipulating the bottom of the mana curve, cascade always finds a specific spell -- often [Hypergenesis](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Hypergenesis%22), Glimpse of Tomorrow, or a ritual that enables the combo turn. This variant overlaps somewhat with other cascade strategies in the format.

Choose Cascade Storm when: You want a combo deck that's harder to disrupt with traditional countermagic.

#### Saga Storm (URx, Tier 3)

Saga Storm incorporates [Urza's Saga](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Urza%27s+Saga%22) as a threat and artifact tutor alongside the storm combo. Saga finds [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond%22), [Lotus Petal](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lotus+Petal%22), or other key artifacts while also creating Construct tokens as a backup plan. The artifact synergy package gives the deck a fair game plan that can win without storming off.

Choose Saga Storm when: You want a storm deck with a credible plan B through artifact beatdown.

#### Mind's Desire (UBR, Tier 3)

Mind's Desire storm focuses on resolving the six-mana spell that exiles cards equal to the storm count, letting you cast them for free. Mind's Desire is one of the most explosive storm payoffs -- with a storm count of 5+, it typically finds enough rituals and action to chain into lethal Tendrils. The deck is slower but more explosive once it gets going.

Choose Mind's Desire when: You enjoy the spectacle of the biggest possible combo turns.

#### High Tide (U, Tier 3)

[High Tide](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22High+Tide%22) is Legacy's oldest combo deck, using the enchantment High Tide to double the mana produced by Islands. With multiple High Tides and [Candelabra of Tawnos](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Candelabra+of+Tawnos%22) (or [Time Spiral](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Time+Spiral%22) to untap all lands), the deck generates absurd mana and wins via [Brain Freeze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brain+Freeze%22) or [Blue Sun's Zenith](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blue+Sun%27s+Zenith%22). Mono blue with [Cunning Wish](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Cunning+Wish%22) for a toolbox makes this a uniquely interactive combo deck.

[High Tide](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22High+Tide%22) rewards deep knowledge of its own sequencing and is beloved by experienced combo players. The deck is exceptionally difficult to play optimally but very powerful in skilled hands.

Choose [High Tide](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22High+Tide%22) when: You want the purest "big blue" combo experience and don't mind one of Legacy's steepest learning curves.

#### TEG / The Eternal Garden (UGx, Tier 3)

TEG uses [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Life+from+the+Loam%22), [Exploration](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Exploration%22), and cycling lands to generate card advantage while eventually assembling a storm kill or grinding the opponent out with value. This hybrid approach gives the deck inevitability that traditional storm lacks.

Choose TEG when: You want a storm-adjacent strategy with a strong grindy backup plan.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Lands (minimal interaction; too slow to race storm), Death and Taxes (Thalia slows but doesn't stop the combo; no hard counters), Eldrazi (no interaction game 1; Chalice on 1 is manageable with rituals costing B), [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) (no countermagic in most builds; too slow to race)

- **Even**: [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) (both are combo; whoever has Force wins), Oops / Belcher (racing matchup; interaction quality decides), Reanimator (similar speed; disruption quality matters)

## 6. Control

**Strategy**: Answer every threat, generate incremental card advantage, and win the long game with superior resources, often through planeswalkers, resilient threats, or inevitability engines.
**Colors**: Blue + White + 1-3 splash colors (primarily Jeskai, UW, 4/5C)
**Tier**: 1.5
**Meta Share**: ~5% (combined family)

### Overview

Control in Legacy occupies a unique space: it must answer the fastest combo decks in Magic (turn-one kills), the most efficient tempo decks (Delver with free counters), and the grindiest midrange strategies -- all while establishing its own win condition. This has historically made pure control difficult in Legacy, as the format's speed and diversity punish reactive strategies that draw the wrong answers.

The printing of Beanstalk Giant // Fertile Footsteps (and its companion [Up the Beanstalk](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Up+the+Beanstalk%22)) changed the calculus dramatically. Up the Beanstalk draws a card whenever you cast a spell with mana value 5 or greater, turning [Leyline Binding](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Leyline+Binding%22), [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) (when hard-cast), and [Solitude](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Solitude%22) into cantripping interaction. This card advantage engine gave control decks the ability to keep pace with Legacy's most efficient strategies. Beanstalk Control quickly rose to become the premier control archetype.

Beyond Beanstalk, Legacy control takes many forms: UW Miracles uses [Counterbalance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Counterbalance%22) and [Terminus](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Terminus%22) for a lock-based approach, Jeskai Control leverages [Monastery Mentor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Monastery+Mentor%22) or planeswalkers as win conditions, and various multicolor piles use the best answers from each color. The common thread is prioritizing answers over threats, card advantage over tempo, and late-game inevitability over early-game pressure.

### Variants

#### Beanstalk Control (4/5C, Tier 1.5 -- 2.3%)

The premier control deck in Legacy. Beanstalk Control uses a 4-5 color mana base (enabled by Triomes and fetch lands) to power domain for [Leyline Binding](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Leyline+Binding%22) while drawing cards off [Up the Beanstalk](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Up+the+Beanstalk%22). The deck plays a critical mass of 5+ MV spells that are normally overcosted -- [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) (hard-cast), [Solitude](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Solitude%22), [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22), [Fury](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Fury%22), [Subtlety](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Subtlety%22) -- all of which become cantrips with Beanstalk in play.

The typical game plan: deploy [Up the Beanstalk](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Up+the+Beanstalk%22) early, answer everything with [Leyline Binding](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Leyline+Binding%22) and [Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22), and bury the opponent in card advantage. Win conditions vary -- Minsc & Boo, Teferi, or simply attacking with Elemental Incarnations. The mana base is greedy but functional, relying on Triomes (Raugrin, Spara's, Ziatora's, Jetmir's) to enable domain.

The deck's weakness is its mana base: [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22), [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22), and [Back to Basics](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Back+to+Basics%22) can be devastating. [Price of Progress](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Price+of+Progress%22) out of Burn sideboards is often lethal. Fast combo can also punish the deck's slower setup turns.

Choose Beanstalk when: You want the highest raw card quality in Legacy and are confident your metagame won't punish a greedy mana base.

#### Domain / Temur / Sultai / Simic / Cascade Beanstalk Variants (3-5C, Tier 2-3)

Various Beanstalk builds that trim colors or adjust the shell. Domain Beanstalk is the full 5-color build. Temur Beanstalk drops white for a leaner mana base. Sultai Beanstalk uses black and green for [Abrupt Decay](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Abrupt+Decay%22) and Uro. Simic Beanstalk is the leanest, focusing on UG card advantage. Cascade Beanstalk adds [Shardless Agent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Shardless+Agent%22) or other cascade spells for additional Beanstalk triggers.

Choose these when: You want the Beanstalk engine but with a more focused or resilient mana base.

#### Jeskai Control (WUR, Tier 2 -- 0.9%)

Jeskai Control pairs the blue cantrip/counter suite with white removal ([Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22), [Terminus](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Terminus%22), [Prismatic Ending](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Prismatic+Ending%22)) and red for [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22), [Pyroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyroblast%22), and [Monastery Mentor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Monastery+Mentor%22). The deck plays a classic draw-go style, holding up countermagic and removal until it can deploy a Mentor or planeswalker to close the game.

[Monastery Mentor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Monastery+Mentor%22) is the signature threat: with the deck's high density of cheap instants and sorceries, Mentor can create a lethal board in 1-2 turns while the control player maintains interaction. Some builds prefer [Shark Typhoon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Shark+Typhoon%22) as an uncounterable threat that can also be cycled for value.

Choose Jeskai when: You want a traditional three-color control deck with [Monastery Mentor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Monastery+Mentor%22) as a finisher.

#### UW Miracles (WU, Tier 2 -- 0.5%)

The classic Legacy control deck. Miracles uses [Counterbalance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Counterbalance%22) (reveal a card with matching mana value to counter a spell) alongside [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22), [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22), and [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor%22) to manipulate the top of the library. When Counterbalance is active and you can control your top card, opposing spells are countered for free. [Terminus](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Terminus%22) provides a one-mana miracle sweeper that Brainstorm can set up at instant speed.

The deck has declined from its peak dominance (the [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sensei%27s+Divining+Top%22) era) but remains a viable strategy. Modern builds supplement [Counterbalance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Counterbalance%22) with [Teferi, Time Raveler](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Teferi%2C+Time+Raveler%22), [Back to Basics](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Back+to+Basics%22), and [Monastery Mentor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Monastery+Mentor%22). The clean two-color mana base is a significant advantage in a [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) format.

Choose UW Miracles when: You want a classic control deck with a clean mana base and the [Counterbalance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Counterbalance%22) lock.

#### UWx Control (WU + splash, Tier 2.5)

A catch-all for UW-based control decks that splash a third color for specific cards. Splashing black adds [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) and [Fatal Push](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Fatal+Push%22). Splashing red adds [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22) and [Pyroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyroblast%22). These builds sacrifice some mana base consistency compared to straight UW but gain access to powerful additional interaction.

Choose UWx when: You want the UW Miracles core but need a specific card from a third color.

#### UWx Mentor (WUx, Tier 2.5)

Mentor-focused control builds that prioritize [Monastery Mentor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Monastery+Mentor%22) as the primary win condition, building the entire deck around protecting and enabling it. These run more cheap spells (Gitaxian Probe where legal, [Mishra's Bauble](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mishra%27s+Bauble%22)) to maximize Mentor triggers.

Choose UWx Mentor when: You want to build entirely around the [Monastery Mentor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Monastery+Mentor%22) win condition.

#### Bant Control (WUG, Tier 3)

Bant adds green for [Uro, Titan of Nature's Wrath](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Uro%2C+Titan+of+Nature%27s+Wrath%22), [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22), and sometimes [Sylvan Library](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sylvan+Library%22) or [Ice-Fang Coatl](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ice-Fang+Coatl%22). Uro provides a recursive threat that gains life, draws cards, and ramps -- a perfect fit for a control strategy. Endurance gives mainboard graveyard interaction that doubles as a reasonable body.

Choose Bant when: You want Uro and [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22) alongside UW control fundamentals.

#### BUG Control (UBG, Tier 3)

BUG (Sultai) Control pairs blue cantrips and counters with black removal ([Fatal Push](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Fatal+Push%22), [Abrupt Decay](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Abrupt+Decay%22), [Toxic Deluge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Toxic+Deluge%22)) and green value (Uro, [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22), [Leovold, Emissary of Trest](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Leovold%2C+Emissary+of+Trest%22)). Leovold's ability to shut down opposing card draw while replacing itself makes it a powerful control finisher.

Choose BUG when: You want [Abrupt Decay](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Abrupt+Decay%22), Leovold, and the most versatile removal suite.

#### Sultai Leovold (UBG, Tier 3)

A BUG Control variant specifically built to maximize [Leovold, Emissary of Trest](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Leovold%2C+Emissary+of+Trest%22). Leovold's "opponents can't draw more than one card per turn" clause shuts down [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22), [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22), and most card advantage in the format. Paired with [Hullbreacher](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Hullbreacher%22) (where legal) or Narset, the deck can lock opponents out of cards entirely.

Choose Sultai Leovold when: You want to build around Leovold's powerful card draw restriction.

#### Grixis Control (UBR, Tier 3)

Grixis Control uses Kolaghan's Command, [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22), and [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) alongside the blue control package. The deck lacks white's premium removal ([Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22), [Terminus](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Terminus%22)) but gains proactive disruption and recursive value through K-Command. Some builds use [Young Pyromancer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Young+Pyromancer%22) or Dack Fayden as additional threats.

Choose Grixis when: You want proactive disruption ([Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22)) without green or white.

#### 4/5C Control (WUBRG, Tier 3)

Maximally greedy control builds that play the best card from every color. Czech Pile (named after Czech Magic players who popularized the archetype) is the most famous example, combining Leovold, Kolaghan's Command, [Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22), and the full blue suite. These decks have the highest individual card quality but the most vulnerable mana bases.

Choose 4/5C when: You want maximum card quality and accept mana base fragility.

#### Czech Pile (4C, Tier 3)

The original 4-color control deck, typically UBRG or WUBR. Czech Pile was the premier "good stuff" control deck before Beanstalk, using [Deathrite Shaman](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Deathrite+Shaman%22) (pre-ban) and later Uro for mana fixing and value. Modern versions use various configurations of the best cards from 4 colors.

Choose Czech Pile when: You're a veteran who knows the format intimately and wants surgical answers for everything.

#### Standstill / Landstill (UWx, Tier 3)

Standstill decks use the enchantment Standstill (draw 3 cards when either player casts a spell) alongside man-lands like [Mishra's Factory](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mishra%27s+Factory%22) or Celestial Colonnade. The plan is to deploy Standstill and a man-land, then attack for 2-3 per turn while the opponent must either let you draw 3 or lose to the slow clock. [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) provides mana denial while you attack with lands.

Choose Standstill when: You enjoy a patient, land-based control strategy.

#### Sharkstill (UWx, Tier 3)

A Standstill variant that uses [Shark Typhoon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Shark+Typhoon%22) alongside the enchantment. Cycling Shark Typhoon doesn't cast a spell (it's an activated ability), so it doesn't break your own Standstill while creating a flying Shark token. This gives the deck a much more meaningful clock than traditional Standstill builds.

Choose Sharkstill when: You want Standstill with a faster, more reliable clock.

#### UB Control / Midrange (UB, Tier 3)

UB Control pairs efficient black removal with blue countermagic in a lean two-color shell. The deck often blurs the line between control and midrange, using threats like [Murktide Regent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Murktide+Regent%22) or [Psychic Frog](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Psychic+Frog%22) alongside a more controlling card selection suite. Some builds lean into [The One Ring](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22The+One+Ring%22) for card advantage.

Choose UB when: You want a clean two-color control shell that dodges [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) issues.

#### UR Control (UR, Tier 3)

UR Control uses [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22), [Pyroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyroblast%22), and sometimes [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22) alongside the blue control suite. [Murktide Regent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Murktide+Regent%22) often serves as the finisher. The deck plays a tempo-control hybrid game, answering threats while landing a big flier.

Choose UR when: You want cheap red removal and [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22) access in a control shell.

#### Mono Blue Control (U, Tier 3)

Pure mono-blue control using [Back to Basics](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Back+to+Basics%22), [Counterbalance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Counterbalance%22), and a suite of counterspells. The mana base is immune to [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) (all basic Islands), and Back to Basics locks out greedy opponents. Win conditions include [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor%22) and [Murktide Regent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Murktide+Regent%22).

Choose Mono Blue when: You want the most [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22)-proof control deck possible.

#### Brought Back Control (WUx, Tier 3)

A control variant that uses Brought Back to recur fetchlands, [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22), or other permanents for incremental value. Combined with Wasteland, Brought Back provides a mana denial plan within a control shell.

Choose Brought Back when: You want a unique value engine that also attacks the opponent's mana.

#### Rosheen Control (RGx, Tier 3)

A niche control strategy built around Rosheen Meanderer or X-spell synergies, using big mana payoffs alongside controlling elements. This is a fringe archetype that occasionally appears in metagames where opponents don't expect it.

Choose Rosheen when: You want to surprise opponents with an unconventional control approach.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Delver / Tempo (card advantage grinds through their threats; [Terminus](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Terminus%22) handles boards cheaply), Death and Taxes (removal answers their creatures; card advantage buries them), Stompy / Chalice decks (answers for lock pieces plus card advantage), Painter (removal for key pieces plus countermagic for combo)
- **Unfavorable**: Fast combo (Storm, Oops, Belcher -- too fast before control stabilizes unless you have Force), Lands (hard to interact with their combo; [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) + [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Life+from+the+Loam%22) grinds control out), [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) (putting [Omniscience](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Omniscience%22) or Emrakul into play through counters is their specialty)
- **Even**: [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) (depends on sweeper timing; can go either way), Other control (mirror matches determined by card advantage engines), Reanimator ([Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) is good but they can grind with [Reanimate](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Reanimate%22) effects)

## 7. Stoneblade

**Strategy**: Combine efficient blue interaction with [Stoneforge Mystic](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stoneforge+Mystic%22)'s equipment tutoring to deploy a threat that outclasses opposing creatures while maintaining countermagic backup.
**Colors**: White + Blue + optional splash (primarily UW, UWR, UWB)
**Tier**: 2
**Meta Share**: ~2% (combined family)

### Overview

Stoneblade is Legacy's quintessential fair midrange-control hybrid. The deck's centerpiece is [Stoneforge Mystic](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stoneforge+Mystic%22), a two-mana creature that tutors an equipment card on ETB and can cheat it into play for 1W at instant speed. The equipment package -- typically [Kaldra Compleat](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Kaldra+Compleat%22) and [Batterskull](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Batterskull%22) -- provides threats that are difficult to remove and outclass nearly every creature in the format. Kaldra Compleat creates an indestructible 5/5 with first strike, trample, and haste, while Batterskull provides a 4/4 vigilance lifelinker that can bounce and be replayed.

The "Blade" in Stoneblade refers to the equipment strategy, and the deck pairs it with the blue cantrip/counter suite ([Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22), [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22), [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22)) and efficient removal ([Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22)). The result is a deck that can play at instant speed, hold up countermagic, and deploy threats that dominate the board.

Stoneblade's strength is its flexibility: the deck can play a controlling game against aggro, a tempo game against combo, and a midrange game against control. Its weakness is that it's a "fair" deck in an "unfair" format -- the equipment plan can be too slow against dedicated combo, and the individual cards, while excellent, don't generate the same explosiveness as dedicated synergy decks.

### Variants

#### Stoneblade (UW, Tier 2 -- 2.0%)

The default configuration: UW with the full cantrip/counter suite, [Stoneforge Mystic](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stoneforge+Mystic%22), and efficient white removal. The clean two-color mana base supports [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) and is resilient to opposing mana denial. [True-Name Nemesis](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22True-Name+Nemesis%22) often serves as the secondary threat, carrying equipment as an unblockable, protection-from-everything attacker.

Modern UW Stoneblade builds sometimes incorporate Phelia, Exuberant Shepherd for additional value, flickering [Stoneforge Mystic](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stoneforge+Mystic%22) for extra equipment tutors. The deck plays a patient game: deploy Stoneforge, hold up counters, and grind out incremental advantages.

Choose UW when: You want the most consistent, clean mana base with the core Stoneblade game plan.

#### Jeskai Stoneblade (UWR, Tier 2.5)

Adding red gives access to [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22) (removal and reach), [Pyroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyroblast%22) (devastating in blue mirrors), and sometimes [Monastery Mentor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Monastery+Mentor%22) as an additional threat axis. The red splash also enables [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22) from the sideboard, which can steal games against greedy mana bases.

Choose Jeskai when: You want [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22) and [Pyroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyroblast%22) in your Stoneblade shell.

#### Esper Stoneblade (UWB, Tier 2.5)

Black adds [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) for proactive disruption, Vindicate or Damn for flexible removal, and sometimes Baleful Strix for a value creature that draws a card. Esper Stoneblade can play a more disruptive game than UW, stripping combo pieces with Thoughtseize while developing the equipment plan.

Choose Esper when: You want [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) and a more proactive disruption package.

#### Bant Stoneblade (UWG, Tier 3)

Green adds [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22), Uro, and sometimes [Sylvan Library](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sylvan+Library%22) or [Green Sun's Zenith](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Green+Sun%27s+Zenith%22). The Bant build leans midrange, using green value cards to extend the game while Stoneforge provides the threat. [Ice-Fang Coatl](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ice-Fang+Coatl%22) (a 1/1 flash flier that draws a card and gains deathtouch with snow lands) is a popular inclusion.

Choose Bant when: You want Uro and [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22) as additional value engines.

#### 4 Color Stoneblade (3-4C, Tier 3)

Greedy multicolor builds that splash for the best cards from additional colors. Common configurations include UWBR (Esper + red for [Pyroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyroblast%22)/Bolt) or UWBG (Esper + green for [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22)/Uro). The mana base suffers but the card quality is maximized.

Choose 4C when: You want every powerful card and don't mind mana base fragility.

#### Abzan Stoneblade (WBG, Tier 3)

A non-blue Stoneblade variant that trades countermagic for hand disruption ([Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22), [Hymn to Tourach](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Hymn+to+Tourach%22)) and green value ([Knight of the Reliquary](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Knight+of+the+Reliquary%22), [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22)). Without [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22), the deck is worse against combo but stronger in fair creature matchups with cards like [Abrupt Decay](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Abrupt+Decay%22) and [Liliana of the Veil](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Liliana+of+the+Veil%22).

Choose Abzan when: You want to play Stoneblade without blue and maximize hand disruption.

#### Boros Stoneblade (WR, Tier 3)

An aggressive non-blue variant pairing Stoneforge with red's efficient threats and removal. [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22), [Magus of the Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Magus+of+the+Moon%22), and sometimes [Broadside Bombardiers](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Broadside+Bombardiers%22) provide reach. The deck is fast and punishing but lacks card selection without cantrips.

Choose Boros when: You want aggressive Stoneblade without blue's card selection.

#### Orzhov Stoneblade (WB, Tier 3)

WB pairs Stoneforge with [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22), [Dark Confidant](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Confidant%22), and efficient black removal. Tidehollow Sculler or Kitesail Freebooter provide additional hand disruption on a body that can carry equipment.

Choose Orzhov when: You want a disruptive, grindy Stoneblade build without blue.

#### Mono White Stoneblade (W, Tier 3)

The leanest possible Stoneblade build, running all [Plains](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Plains%22) and [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22). Without another color, the deck relies on [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben%22), [Recruiter of the Guard](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Recruiter+of+the+Guard%22), and [Flickerwisp](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Flickerwisp%22) alongside the Stoneforge package. This blurs the line with Death and Taxes but maintains a Stoneblade identity through heavier equipment focus.

Choose Mono White when: You want maximum [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) usage and immunity to [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22).

#### UWx Stoneblade (UW + splash, Tier 2.5)

A catch-all for UW Stoneblade decks with a light third-color splash for specific cards -- not deep enough to be Jeskai or Esper but gaining access to one or two key off-color spells. Often a single [Volcanic Island](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Volcanic+Island%22) for [Pyroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyroblast%22) or an [Underground Sea](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Underground+Sea%22) for [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22).

Choose UWx when: You want mostly UW but need a specific splash card for your metagame.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Delver / Tempo (equipment outclasses their threats; STP handles their creatures; True-Name is unanswerable), Burn ([Batterskull](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Batterskull%22)'s lifelink is game-ending), Death and Taxes (better equipment, countermagic trumps Vial)
- **Unfavorable**: Combo (Storm, Oops, [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) -- too slow to pressure, not enough disruption), Lands (Marit Lage flies over equipment; [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) + Loam grinds you out), 4C Control / Beanstalk (out-grinds you with superior card advantage)
- **Even**: [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) (equipment dominates combat but their go-wide plan can overwhelm), Other midrange (depends on specific card matchups and builds), Eldrazi (Chalice is annoying but equipment matches their big creatures)

## 8. Death and Taxes / Vial

**Strategy**: Deploy hatebears and disruptive creatures via [Aether Vial](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Aether+Vial%22) to tax the opponent's mana and spells, denying resources while attacking with efficiently-statted white creatures.
**Colors**: White (mono or with splash colors via [Aether Vial](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Aether+Vial%22))
**Tier**: 2
**Meta Share**: ~2% (combined family)

### Overview

Death and Taxes is Legacy's premier creature-based disruption deck. Named after the certainty of "death and taxes," the archetype uses [Aether Vial](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Aether+Vial%22) to deploy creatures at instant speed without paying mana, freeing up lands for [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) activations and [Rishadan Port](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rishadan+Port%22) mana denial. Every creature in the deck serves double duty: it attacks for damage while simultaneously disrupting the opponent's game plan.

The deck's core engine is [Aether Vial](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Aether+Vial%22) on one. Once Vial is active, D&T operates at instant speed, flashing in creatures like [Flickerwisp](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Flickerwisp%22) (exile a permanent temporarily), [Skyclave Apparition](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Skyclave+Apparition%22) (exile a nonland, nontoken permanent), or [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben%22) (tax noncreature spells by 1) at end of turn or in response to key plays. This creates a uniquely interactive creature deck that plays more like a tempo-control strategy than a traditional aggro deck.

[Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) and [Rishadan Port](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rishadan+Port%22) provide mana denial that pairs devastatingly with Thalia's tax effect. An opponent facing Thalia + Wasteland + Port often can't cast their spells at all, let alone at a competitive rate. The deck's creatures are individually modest (2/1s and 3/1s) but their collective disruption creates a prison-like effect.

D&T has adapted to every metagame shift in Legacy's history, absorbing new hatebears and white creatures as they're printed. Recent additions like Phelia, Exuberant Shepherd (flicker engine), [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) (token generation), and various initiative creatures have kept the archetype competitive.

### Variants

#### Death and Taxes (W, Tier 2 -- 2.0%)

The classic mono-white build. D&T runs 4 [Aether Vial](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Aether+Vial%22), 4 Thalia, 4 [Stoneforge Mystic](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stoneforge+Mystic%22), and a curated suite of hatebears that shifts with the metagame. The mono-white mana base supports 4 [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) and 4 [Rishadan Port](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rishadan+Port%22) while still casting all its spells, making it one of the most mana-denial-heavy fair decks in Legacy.

Modern D&T has evolved with Phelia, Exuberant Shepherd as a powerful new two-drop that flickers permanents on attack. The flicker synergies with [Skyclave Apparition](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Skyclave+Apparition%22) (permanent exile), [Stoneforge Mystic](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stoneforge+Mystic%22) (extra equipment tutors), and [Recruiter of the Guard](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Recruiter+of+the+Guard%22) (repeated tutoring) give the deck a value engine it previously lacked.

The equipment package (typically [Kaldra Compleat](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Kaldra+Compleat%22) + [Batterskull](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Batterskull%22)) provides the deck's primary win condition, with Stoneforge cheating them into play at instant speed through Vial or its activated ability.

#### Eldrazi and Taxes (WC, Tier 2.5)

Adding colorless Eldrazi gives D&T access to [Thought-Knot Seer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thought-Knot+Seer%22) (hand disruption on a 4/4), [Reality Smasher](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Reality+Smasher%22) (hasty 5/5 with discard protection), and Eldrazi Displacer (repeatable flicker). The colorless mana comes from Caves of Koilos, [Eldrazi Temple](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Eldrazi+Temple%22), and [Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22), providing explosive mana that pure D&T lacks.

The Eldrazi creatures are individually more powerful than traditional D&T creatures, but the mana base is less consistent and supports fewer colorless utility lands ([Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22), Port). Some builds lean more Eldrazi than Taxes, playing closer to a midrange Eldrazi deck with white splash.

Choose Eldrazi and Taxes when: You want bigger individual threats and don't mind a less aggressive mana denial plan.

#### Steel and Taxes (W + artifacts, Tier 3)

Steel and Taxes incorporates artifact synergies: [Nettlecyst](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Nettlecyst%22), [Thought Monitor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thought+Monitor%22), and sometimes Kappa Cannoneer alongside the D&T shell. [Urza's Saga](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Urza%27s+Saga%22) provides a land that creates Construct tokens and tutors for key one-mana artifacts ([Aether Vial](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Aether+Vial%22), [Retrofitter Foundry](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Retrofitter+Foundry%22)).

Choose Steel and Taxes when: You want artifact synergies and [Urza's Saga](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Urza%27s+Saga%22) alongside hatebears.

#### Esper Vial (WUB, Tier 2.5)

Esper Vial adds blue and black for powerful creatures like Baleful Strix (deathtouch flier that draws a card), [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22), and sometimes Fallen Shinobi or Tidehollow Sculler. The blue splash enables [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22) in some builds, though many maintain the Vial-only mana plan with just splash creatures.

Choose Esper Vial when: You want D&T's disruption with card advantage creatures from blue and black.

#### Azorius Vial / Phelia (WU, Tier 2.5)

A UW build focused on maximizing Phelia, Exuberant Shepherd alongside blue value creatures. Reflector Mage, Spell Queller, and sometimes Deputy of Detention provide a disruptive blue creature suite that Vial can deploy at instant speed. The blue splash is light -- often just a few [Tundra](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tundra%22) or [Hallowed Fountain](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Hallowed+Fountain%22).

Choose Azorius Vial when: You want to maximize Phelia's flicker synergies with blue creatures.

#### Jeskai Vial (WUR, Tier 3)

Red adds [Imperial Recruiter](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Imperial+Recruiter%22) (tutors any creature with power 2 or less, complementing [Recruiter of the Guard](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Recruiter+of+the+Guard%22)), [Magus of the Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Magus+of+the+Moon%22) ([Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22) on a creature), and sometimes [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22). The double-Recruiter toolbox gives the deck unprecedented creature tutoring consistency.

Choose Jeskai Vial when: You want the full Recruiter toolbox and [Magus of the Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Magus+of+the+Moon%22).

#### Orzhov Vial (WB, Tier 3)

Black adds [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22), [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22), and [Plague Engineer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Plague+Engineer%22) (names a creature type to give -1/-1). The WB shell provides hand disruption alongside D&T's creature disruption, creating a more proactive plan against combo.

Choose Orzhov Vial when: You want hand disruption without blue.

#### Mono White Vial / Blink / Initiative / Prison / Rabbits (W, Tier 2.5-3)

Various mono-white builds that emphasize different aspects of the D&T shell. Blink builds maximize flicker effects ([Charming Prince](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Charming+Prince%22), Restoration Angel, Yorion). Initiative builds focus on [Seasoned Dungeoneer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Seasoned+Dungeoneer%22) and White Plume Adventurer for the dungeon mechanic. Prison builds run [Chalice of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chalice+of+the+Void%22), Suppression Field, and Spirit of the Labyrinth for maximum lock pieces. Rabbits builds incorporate the rabbit token theme from recent sets.

Choose these when: You want a mono-white Vial deck with a specific synergy focus.

#### Weenie White (W, Tier 3)

Aggressive mono-white builds that skip the Vial/tax plan for maximum creature pressure. Champion of the Parish, Thalia's Lieutenant, and other Human synergies provide a go-wide aggressive plan. This is closer to a Legacy White Weenie deck than traditional D&T.

Choose Weenie White when: You want pure aggression over disruption.

#### Soldiers (W/WU, Tier 3)

A tribal variant that leverages Soldier creature type synergies. Preeminent Captain cheats Soldiers into play, and various lords pump the team. The deck overlaps with D&T in card choices (Thalia is a Soldier) but has a distinct tribal identity.

Choose Soldiers when: You want a tribal synergy deck with D&T's disruption backbone.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Storm / Combo (Thalia + [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) + Port creates a soft lock; they can't combo through the tax effects), Delver / Tempo (equipment outclasses their threats; Vial dodges [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22); [Flickerwisp](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Flickerwisp%22) disrupts their game plan), Lands (Port + Wasteland attacks their mana; Flickerwisp exiles Marit Lage; flying creatures go over)
- **Unfavorable**: [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) / Energy (they go wider; [Galvanic Discharge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Galvanic+Discharge%22) handles your creatures; token generation overwhelms), Elves (too many creatures to handle; they combo faster than you can disrupt), 4C Control / Beanstalk (card advantage grinds through your disruption; sweepers handle your board)
- **Even**: [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) (no hard counters but Thalia + fast clock can race; [Containment Priest](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Containment+Priest%22) from sideboard), Reanimator ([Karakas](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Karakas%22) handles legends; Containment Priest is devastating; but they can be too fast), Stoneblade (similar creature quality; comes down to Vial vs countermagic advantage)

## 9. Painter

**Strategy**: Assemble the two-card combo of [Painter's Servant](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Painter%27s+Servant%22) (naming a color) + [Grindstone](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grindstone%22) to mill the opponent's entire library in one activation.
**Colors**: Red (primarily mono-red or Rx)
**Tier**: 2
**Meta Share**: ~4.1% (combined family)

### Overview

Painter is a compact artifact combo deck that wins by combining [Painter's Servant](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Painter%27s+Servant%22) (which makes all cards in all zones the chosen color) with [Grindstone](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grindstone%22) (which mills two cards and repeats if they share a color). Since Painter's Servant makes every card the same color, Grindstone never stops, milling the opponent's entire library. The deck operates out of a red shell with fast mana ([Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22), [City of Traitors](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22City+of+Traitors%22), [Simian Spirit Guide](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Simian+Spirit+Guide%22)) and uses [Imperial Recruiter](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Imperial+Recruiter%22) to tutor for Painter's Servant.

The red shell also provides [Goblin Welder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Goblin+Welder%22) for artifact recursion, [Pyroblast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyroblast%22) as mainboard interaction (devastating against blue decks), and [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22) / [Magus of the Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Magus+of+the+Moon%22) as a prison backup plan. The deck can pivot between combo and prison depending on the matchup, making it more resilient than a pure combo strategy.

### Variants

#### Painter (R, Tier 2 -- 4.1%)

[Goblin Engineer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Goblin+Engineer%22) provides additional artifact tutoring and recursion.

## 10. Lands

**Strategy**: Abuse powerful lands with [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Life+from+the+Loam%22) recursion, [Exploration](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Exploration%22) for extra land drops, and the [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Depths%22) + [Thespian's Stage](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thespian%27s+Stage%22) combo to create Marit Lage, a 20/20 flying indestructible token.
**Colors**: Primarily RG, BG, GW, or multi-color
**Tier**: 1.5
**Meta Share**: ~3.8% (combined family)

### Overview

Lands is one of Legacy's most unique archetypes, operating almost entirely through its mana base rather than spells. The core engine is [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Life+from+the+Loam%22) recurring [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) (for repeated land destruction), cycling lands (for card draw via dredge), and the [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Depths%22) + [Thespian's Stage](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thespian%27s+Stage%22) combo (copy Dark Depths with Stage; the copy has no ice counters, immediately sacrifices to create Marit Lage). [Exploration](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Exploration%22) and [Manabond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Manabond%22) enable multiple land drops per turn, accelerating this engine dramatically.

The deck is exceptionally difficult to interact with using traditional means -- countermagic doesn't stop land abilities, creature removal doesn't hit lands, and [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) can be recurred infinitely with Loam. The deck's weakness is its speed: assembling the engine takes time, and fast combo or aggressive tempo decks can close the game before Lands establishes its lock.

### Variants

#### Lands (RG, Tier 1.5 -- 3.8%)

The classic build using [Punishing Fire](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Punishing+Fire%22) + [Grove of the Burnwillows](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grove+of+the+Burnwillows%22) as a recurring removal engine alongside the Depths combo and Loam lock. Valakut [Exploration](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Exploration%22) provides additional card advantage in some builds. Saga variants incorporate [Urza's Saga](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Urza%27s+Saga%22) for Construct tokens and artifact tutoring.

#### Selesnya Depths / GWx Depths (GW, Tier 2.5 -- 0.8%)

White-green builds focused on the [Dark Depths](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Depths%22) combo with [Knight of the Reliquary](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Knight+of+the+Reliquary%22) as both a land tutor and a large beater. [Swords to Plowshares](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Swords+to+Plowshares%22) provides premium removal. Some builds add [Elvish Reclaimer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Elvish+Reclaimer%22) for additional land-tutoring redundancy.

#### Sultai Depths / Mono Green Depths (BG/G, Tier 3)

Black-green builds use Sylvan Scrying and Vampire Hexmage (instantly removes all ice counters from Depths) for a faster, more focused combo. Mono Green versions maximize [Crop Rotation](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Crop+Rotation%22) and [Elvish Reclaimer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Elvish+Reclaimer%22) with the simplest mana base.

#### Aggro Loam / Loam Pox / 4 Color Loam / Bant Loam (various, Tier 3)

Midrange-control decks built around [Life from the Loam](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Life+from+the+Loam%22)'s engine with various creature suites. Aggro Loam pairs Loam with aggressive creatures and Seismic Assault. Loam Pox combines Loam with [Smallpox](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Smallpox%22) and [Liliana of the Veil](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Liliana+of+the+Veil%22) for a resource denial strategy. 4 Color and Bant Loam maximize card quality across multiple colors.

#### Dryad Valakut (RG, Tier 3)

Uses Dryad of the Ilysian Grove to make all lands count as Mountains, enabling Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle to deal 3 damage per land drop. Combined with [Exploration](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Exploration%22) and Loam, this creates a machine-gun effect.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Fair creature decks without flying (Marit Lage dominates), control without [Dress Down](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dress+Down%22) (hard to interact with lands), midrange ([Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) lock is devastating)
- **Unfavorable**: Delver/Tempo (fast clock + [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) before engine assembles), fast combo (too slow to race), [Karakas](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Karakas%22) decks (bounces Marit Lage)
- **Even**: [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) (depends on speed and interaction), other combo (racing matchup)

## 11. Prison / Stompy

**Strategy**: Deploy lock pieces ([Chalice of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chalice+of+the+Void%22), [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22), [Trinisphere](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Trinisphere%22)) using fast mana to prevent the opponent from playing Magic, then win with undercosted threats.
**Colors**: Red, Colorless, White, or multi-color
**Tier**: 1.5-2
**Meta Share**: ~15% (combined sub-archetypes)

### Overview

Prison / Stompy decks exploit Legacy's reliance on cheap spells and nonbasic lands by deploying lock pieces before opponents can establish their game plan. The shared DNA is [Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22) + [City of Traitors](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22City+of+Traitors%22) for fast mana, [Chalice of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chalice+of+the+Void%22) on 1 (locks out cantrips, Swords, rituals), and threats that don't require the locked mana values. These decks are the metagame's police force -- they keep blue cantrip decks and combo honest.

### Variants

#### Red Stompy / Dragon Stompy (R, Tier 2 -- 2.7%)

Mono-red prison with Chalice, [Blood Moon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Blood+Moon%22), [Trinisphere](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Trinisphere%22), and efficient red threats like [Goblin Rabblemaster](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Goblin+Rabblemaster%22), [Fury](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Fury%22), and sometimes Chandra, Torch of Defiance. The original "Stompy" deck and still one of the most effective prison strategies. [Fable of the Mirror-Breaker](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Fable+of+the+Mirror-Breaker%22) provides card advantage and token generation.

#### Eldrazi (C/Cx, Tier 2 -- 3.1%)

Colorless Eldrazi using [Eldrazi Temple](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Eldrazi+Temple%22) and [Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22) to power out [Thought-Knot Seer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thought-Knot+Seer%22), [Reality Smasher](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Reality+Smasher%22), and [Endbringer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endbringer%22) ahead of curve. [Chalice of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chalice+of+the+Void%22) on 1 is devastating alongside these large threats. Some builds splash white for [Solitude](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Solitude%22) or Displacer.

#### Affinity Stompy (various, Tier 2 -- 2.4%)

Artifact-heavy builds using [Urza's Saga](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Urza%27s+Saga%22), [Nettlecyst](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Nettlecyst%22), [Thought Monitor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thought+Monitor%22), and Kappa Cannoneer. The artifact synergies provide both threats and card advantage while Chalice locks out opponents. Echo Affinity and Emry Affinity sub-variants use [Emry, Lurker of the Loch](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Emry%2C+Lurker+of+the+Loch%22) for artifact recursion.

#### Artifacts Prison / Blue Artifacts / 8-Cast (U/C, Tier 2 -- 2.0%/1.1%)

8-Cast uses Thoughtcast and [Thought Monitor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thought+Monitor%22) alongside cheap artifacts for a card-advantage-based prison deck. The "8-Cast" name refers to running 8 cheap draw spells. MUD (0.5%) is the classic all-colorless artifact prison deck using [Metalworker](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Metalworker%22) and [Staff of Domination](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Staff+of+Domination%22).

#### Initiative Stompy / Boros Initiative (W/WR, Tier 2 -- 2.0%)

White-based prison using White Plume Adventurer and [Seasoned Dungeoneer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Seasoned+Dungeoneer%22) to take the initiative, gaining dungeon rewards each turn. Chalice, Thalia, and [Chrome Mox](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Chrome+Mox%22) provide the lock, while initiative creatures generate card advantage. Naya Initiative splashes green for additional creatures.

#### Steel Stompy / Mono Red variants / Mono Blue variants (various, Tier 2.5-3)

Steel Stompy focuses on artifact creatures and equipment. Mono Red variants include Initiative, Artifacts, Cauldron (Agatha's Soul Cauldron combo), Midrange, and Wizards builds. Mono Blue variants include Karn-based prison, artifact-focused builds, and pure Prison/Stasis shells. UW Artifacts, Boros/Bant/Mono Green Prison, Stax, As Foretold, and 8 Mulch round out the category.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Delver/Tempo (Chalice on 1 is game-ending), Storm (Chalice + [Trinisphere](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Trinisphere%22) shuts down rituals), cantrip-heavy decks (Chalice on 1 blanks [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22)/[Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22))
- **Unfavorable**: Lands (doesn't care about Chalice; grinds through prison), [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) (costs 3, dodges Chalice on 1-2), [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) (goes wide around lock pieces)
- **Even**: Other prison decks (mirror depends on who locks first), fair midrange (depends on draw quality)

## 12. Mystic Forge / Artifact Combo

**Strategy**: Use artifact-based engines to generate infinite mana or card advantage, then win with a large payoff spell or loop.
**Colors**: Colorless, Blue, or White
**Tier**: 2
**Meta Share**: ~3.7% (combined family)

### Overview

[Mystic Forge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mystic+Forge%22) Combo uses Mystic Forge (cast artifacts and colorless spells from the top of your library) alongside [Sensei's Divining Top](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sensei%27s+Divining+Top%22) (draw a card and put Top on top of the library) and cost reducers (Foundry Inspector, Ugin, the Ineffable, Cloud Key) to draw the entire deck. With Forge + Top + cost reducer, you tap Top to draw and put it on top, then cast it for free off Forge, repeating infinitely. The kill is typically [Walking Ballista](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Walking+Ballista%22) or Aetherflux Reservoir.

Related artifact combo decks include Bomberman ([Auriok Salvagers](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Auriok+Salvagers%22) + [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond%22) for infinite mana), [Coveted Jewel](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Coveted+Jewel%22) loops, Power Artifact + [Grim Monolith](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grim+Monolith%22)/Basalt Monolith for infinite mana, Tezzerator (Tezzeret, Agent of Bolas as both tutor and win condition), and various Paradox Engine / Paradoxical Outcome chains.

### Variants

#### Mystic Forge Combo (C, Tier 2 -- 3.0%)

The primary build, typically colorless with [Ancient Tomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ancient+Tomb%22), [City of Traitors](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22City+of+Traitors%22), and [Grim Monolith](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grim+Monolith%22). [Karn, the Great Creator](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Karn%2C+the+Great+Creator%22) provides a wishboard of silver bullets. Consistent and resilient due to the artifact redundancy and tutoring available.

#### Coveted Jewel (Cx, Tier 3 -- 0.7%)

Builds focused on looping [Coveted Jewel](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Coveted+Jewel%22) with flicker or sacrifice effects to draw 3 cards repeatedly while generating mana. Often paired with [Goblin Welder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Goblin+Welder%22) or other artifact recursion.

#### Bomberman / Salvager Combo (W, Tier 3)

[Auriok Salvagers](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Auriok+Salvagers%22) + [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond%22) generates infinite mana by repeatedly sacrificing and recurring LED. Wins with [Walking Ballista](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Walking+Ballista%22) or [Pyrite Spellbomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Pyrite+Spellbomb%22). Compact combo that fits into hatebear shells.

#### Tezzerator / Ironworks / Paradox Engine / Riddlesmith (various, Tier 3)

Niche artifact combo decks using different engines. Tezzerator uses Tezzeret to turn artifacts into 5/5s. Ironworks (Krark-Clan Ironworks) sacrifices artifacts for mana. Paradox Engine untaps all artifacts when you cast a spell. Riddlesmith loots through the deck with artifact casts.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Creature decks without artifact hate (combo ignores their board), Lands (fast combo before their engine)
- **Unfavorable**: [Null Rod](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Null+Rod%22) / [Collector Ouphe](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Collector+Ouphe%22) decks (shuts off entire strategy), blue tempo (Force + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22)), Karn mirrors
- **Even**: Other combo (racing), prison decks (depends on who establishes first)

## 13. Reanimator

**Strategy**: Put a massive creature into the graveyard early, then reanimate it with a one or two-mana reanimation spell for a fast, game-ending threat.
**Colors**: Black + Blue or White
**Tier**: 1.5
**Meta Share**: ~2.0% (combined family)

### Overview

Reanimator aims to put a game-ending creature ([Griselbrand](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Griselbrand%22), [Archon of Cruelty](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Archon+of+Cruelty%22), [Atraxa, Grand Unifier](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Atraxa%2C+Grand+Unifier%22)) into the graveyard via [Entomb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Entomb%22), [Faithless Looting](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Faithless+Looting%22), or [Careful Study](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Careful+Study%22), then bring it back with [Reanimate](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Reanimate%22), [Animate Dead](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Animate+Dead%22), or [Exhume](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Exhume%22) on turns 1-2. The deck combines explosive speed with some of the most powerful individual threats in Magic. Griselbrand in particular is often an immediate win -- drawing 7-14 cards finds enough interaction to protect the 7/7 flying lifelinker.

### Variants

#### Dimir Reanimator (UB, Tier 1.5 -- 2.0%)

The standard build using [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22) and [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) for card selection and protection alongside the reanimation package. [Careful Study](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Careful+Study%22) and sometimes [Faithless Looting](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Faithless+Looting%22) provide additional discard outlets. The blue splash makes the deck more resilient to disruption than mono-black versions.

#### Orzhov Reanimator / Rescaminator (WB, Tier 2.5)

White provides Teferi's Protection, [Archon of Cruelty](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Archon+of+Cruelty%22) synergies, and sometimes [Ephemerate](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ephemerate%22) for repeated ETB triggers. "Rescaminator" builds use [Grief](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grief%22) + Ephemerate (the "Scam" package) to strip the opponent's hand while building toward reanimation.

#### Buried Phoenix (BR, Tier 3)

Uses Buried Alive to put three creatures into the graveyard at once, often including Arclight Phoenix for immediate value alongside a reanimation target. Red provides [Faithless Looting](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Faithless+Looting%22) as a discard outlet.

#### Worldgorger Dragon (BRx, Tier 3)

Combo variant that reanimates Worldgorger Dragon with [Animate Dead](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Animate+Dead%22), creating an infinite loop (Dragon exiles all permanents including Animate Dead, Dragon leaves, permanents return, Animate Dead re-targets Dragon). Each iteration untaps all lands for infinite mana, winning with a mana sink like Shivan Hellkite or [Walking Ballista](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Walking+Ballista%22).

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Lands (too slow; no graveyard hate game 1), creature decks without blue (no [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22)), fair decks without hate
- **Unfavorable**: [Surgical Extraction](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Surgical+Extraction%22) decks (removes reanimation targets permanently), [Rest in Peace](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Rest+in+Peace%22) / [Leyline of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Leyline+of+the+Void%22) (shuts off graveyard), Dimir Tempo (Force + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) + [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) + fast clock)
- **Even**: [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) (racing combo), other fast combo (speed dependent), control (depends on countermagic drawn)

## 14. Doomsday / Oracle Combo

**Strategy**: Cast [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) to build a five-card library that deterministically wins the game, typically through [Thassa's Oracle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thassa%27s+Oracle%22).
**Colors**: Blue + Black
**Tier**: 2
**Meta Share**: ~2.7% (combined family)

### Overview

[Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) is Legacy's most cerebral combo deck. The namesake spell (BBB sorcery) lets you search your library and construct a new five-card library in any order, exiling the rest and paying half your life. A skilled Doomsday pilot builds a pile that wins through any anticipated disruption -- the "puzzle" aspect of constructing the perfect pile for each game state is the deck's defining feature. The most common kill is [Thassa's Oracle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thassa%27s+Oracle%22) with an empty library, but piles can be built to play around [Stifle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stifle%22), [Surgical Extraction](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Surgical+Extraction%22), graveyard hate, and more.

[Necrodominance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Necrodominance%22) variants use the MH3 printing as an alternative card-advantage engine, often alongside Oracle as the win condition but without necessarily casting [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22).

### Key Cards

|------|------|
| [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) | Build a 5-card library to win deterministically |
| [Thassa's Oracle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thassa%27s+Oracle%22) | Win condition; wins with 0-2 cards in library |
| [Necrodominance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Necrodominance%22) | Card advantage engine; alternative to [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) |
| [Dark Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Ritual%22) | Enables turn-1 [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) |
| [Brainstorm](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Brainstorm%22) / [Ponder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ponder%22) | Card selection; draws into the [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) pile |

| Tainted Pact | Alternative win; exiles library one-by-one with Oracle trigger on stack |

### Variants

#### Doomsday (UB, Tier 2 -- 2.7%)

The classic build with cantrips, disruption, and the [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) + Oracle kill. Runs [Dark Ritual](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dark+Ritual%22) for speed and [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) for protection. The skill ceiling is extremely high -- pile construction varies for every game state.

#### Dimir Necrodominance (UB, Tier 2.5)

Uses [Necrodominance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Necrodominance%22) to draw massive hands, then wins with Oracle or assembles another combo kill. Less fragile than [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) but requires Necrodominance to stick for a turn cycle. Necro Showdown variants add Showdown of the Skalds or similar effects.

#### Tainted Pact (UB, Tier 3)

Runs a singleton mana base and card suite to enable Tainted Pact as a one-card combo with [Thassa's Oracle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thassa%27s+Oracle%22). Cast Oracle, hold priority, cast Tainted Pact exiling your entire library, Oracle trigger resolves with devotion > library size. Requires strict singleton deck construction.

#### Thassa's Lash / Paradigm (UB, Tier 3)

Alternative Oracle combo builds using Thought Lash (exile library by paying 0 repeatedly) or Paradigm Shift (swap library and graveyard when graveyard is empty) to empty the library for Oracle. These provide different angles of attack that dodge some hate cards.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Fair creature decks (combo goes over the top), Lands (no relevant interaction), Stompy (Chalice on 1 doesn't stop [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) at BBB)
- **Unfavorable**: Tempo/Delver ([Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22) + Force + clock), [Stifle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stifle%22) effects (counter Oracle trigger), surgical-heavy decks
- **Even**: Other combo (speed and interaction dependent), control ([Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) fights)

## 15. Pox / Mono Black

**Strategy**: Deny the opponent all resources -- lands, hand, creatures -- through symmetrical discard, sacrifice effects, and removal while winning with resilient threats or inevitability.
**Colors**: Black (mono or with splashes)
**Tier**: 2.5
**Meta Share**: ~3.3% (combined family)

### Overview

Pox strategies weaponize resource denial through cards like [Smallpox](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Smallpox%22) (each player discards, sacrifices a creature, sacrifices a land, loses 1 life), [Hymn to Tourach](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Hymn+to+Tourach%22) (random discard 2), [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22), and [Liliana of the Veil](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Liliana+of+the+Veil%22). The deck leverages symmetry-breaking through cards that benefit from an empty board and empty hands -- [Bloodghast](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Bloodghast%22), [Nether Spirit](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Nether+Spirit%22), and Cursed Scroll don't care about resource parity. Mono Black Midrange variants play a more threat-dense game with [Sheoldred, the Apocalypse](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Sheoldred%2C+the+Apocalypse%22) and Dauthi Voidwalker.

#### Pox (B, Tier 2.5 -- 2.0%)

Classic mono-black resource denial with [Smallpox](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Smallpox%22), Hymn, [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22), and Liliana. Wins through attrition with [Nether Spirit](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Nether+Spirit%22), [Mishra's Factory](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mishra%27s+Factory%22), or planeswalker ultimates. Incredibly punishing against greedy mana bases and midrange.

#### Mono Black Midrange (B, Tier 2.5 -- 1.3%)

Threat-dense mono-black with Sheoldred, Dauthi Voidwalker, Tourach Dread Cantor, and [Hymn to Tourach](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Hymn+to+Tourach%22). Less focused on [Smallpox](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Smallpox%22) symmetry and more on deploying powerful individual threats backed by disruption.

#### Mono Black variants (various, Tier 3)

Includes Mono Black Aggro, Dauthi builds, Necro (Necropotence-based), Combo, Helm ([Helm of Obedience](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Helm+of+Obedience%22) + [Leyline of the Void](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Leyline+of+the+Void%22)), [Death's Shadow](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Death%27s+Shadow%22), Prison, Demons, Initiative, and Slivers. Each exploits different aspects of black's card pool. 8-Rack focuses on [The Rack](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22The+Rack%22) and Shrieking Affliction to punish empty hands.

#### Death's Shadow (UBx/BRx, Tier 2.5)

Uses [Death's Shadow](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Death%27s+Shadow%22) alongside [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22), [Reanimate](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Reanimate%22) (on opponents' creatures), and aggressive life payment to deploy a massive undercosted threat. Dimir and Rakdos variants exist with different supporting cards. Eva Green and Deadguy Ale are historical predecessors.

#### Curses / Dark Burn (B/BR, Tier 3)

Niche black strategies using Curse of Misfortunes or burn-style reach with Bump in the Night and similar effects. Fringe but occasionally effective in unexpected metagames.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Combo (hand disruption dismantles combo before it assembles), greedy mana bases ([Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) + Hymn is devastating), control (resource denial out-attrites them)
- **Unfavorable**: [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22)/Energy (they recover from resource denial with token generation), blue tempo (cantrips rebuild faster than you can strip), resilient threat decks
- **Even**: Fair midrange (depends on card quality and sequencing), Lands (both play the long game)

#### Harvest Combo (Gx, Tier 3)

Uses Aetherflux Reservoir or similar payoffs with creature-based mana generation and card draw loops.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Other fair creature decks (bigger threats, toolbox answers), Stompy ([Collector Ouphe](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Collector+Ouphe%22), [Endurance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Endurance%22)), Burn (lifegain creatures, large blockers)
- **Unfavorable**: Combo (too slow without blue interaction), Delver/Tempo ([Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22) + [Daze](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Daze%22) punishes mana creatures), control (sweepers destroy the board)
- **Even**: Lands (depends on variant), [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) (creature quality matchup)

## 17. Cloudpost / Ramp

**Strategy**: Generate massive mana through [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Cloudpost%22)/[Glimmerpost](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Glimmerpost%22) lands or Tron pieces, then cast overwhelming threats like Ugin, Karn, or Emrakul.
**Colors**: Green, Blue, or Colorless
**Tier**: 2.5
**Meta Share**: ~1.0% (combined family)

### Overview

[Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Cloudpost%22) decks use the Locus lands (Cloudpost taps for mana equal to the number of Loci in play, [Glimmerpost](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Glimmerpost%22) gains life) alongside [Expedition Map](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Expedition+Map%22), [Crop Rotation](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Crop+Rotation%22), or Sylvan Scrying to assemble a mana engine that produces 4-8+ mana per land. The payoffs are format-warping: [Primeval Titan](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Primeval+Titan%22), [Ugin, the Spirit Dragon](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ugin%2C+the+Spirit+Dragon%22), and Emrakul, the Promised End. Tron variants use Urza's Mine/Power Plant/Tower for similar big-mana effects.

**Strategy**: Use [Cephalid Illusionist](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Cephalid+Illusionist%22) + equipment or targeting effects to mill your entire library, then win with a graveyard-based combo.
**Colors**: UW or UWx
**Tier**: 3
**Meta Share**: ~0.4%

### Overview

[Cephalid Illusionist](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Cephalid+Illusionist%22) mills 3 cards whenever it becomes the target of a spell or ability. Combined with [Shuko](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Shuko%22) (equipment that equips for 0) or [Nomads en-Kor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Nomads+en-Kor%22) (target for 0), you can mill your entire library. The win condition is typically [Thassa's Oracle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thassa%27s+Oracle%22) or [Narcomoeba](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Narcomoeba%22) + [Dread Return](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dread+Return%22) into a finisher. The deck runs [Stoneforge Mystic](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stoneforge+Mystic%22) to find Shuko and operates as a UW creature-combo deck with [Force of Will](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Force+of+Will%22) protection.

## 27. Stiflenought

**Strategy**: Deploy [Phyrexian Dreadnought](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Phyrexian+Dreadnought%22) (12/12 trample for 1 mana) and use [Stifle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stifle%22) to counter its sacrifice trigger, creating a turn-1 12/12.
**Colors**: Blue (UB or UW)
**Tier**: 3
**Meta Share**: <0.5%

### Overview

[Phyrexian Dreadnought](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Phyrexian+Dreadnought%22) is a 1-mana 12/12 with a drawback: sacrifice it unless you sacrifice 12 power of creatures. [Stifle](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stifle%22) counters the triggered ability, keeping the Dreadnought in play. The deck also uses [Dress Down](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dress+Down%22) (removes all abilities, so Dreadnought enters with no trigger) and [Torpor Orb](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Torpor+Orb%22) for redundancy. White Dreadnought variants use [Solitude](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Solitude%22) and [Flickerwisp](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Flickerwisp%22) alongside the combo. Vesuvan Duelist copies opponents' creatures while the Dreadnought applies pressure.

## 28. Jund / Fair Midrange

**Strategy**: Combine efficient threats ([Tarmogoyf](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tarmogoyf%22), [Dragon's Rage Channeler](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dragon%27s+Rage+Channeler%22)) with hand disruption ([Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22), [Hymn to Tourach](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Hymn+to+Tourach%22)) and versatile removal.
**Colors**: BRG, BRx, or UBRx
**Tier**: 2.5
**Meta Share**: ~1%

### Overview

Jund is Legacy's classic non-blue fair deck, using [Thoughtseize](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thoughtseize%22), [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22), [Abrupt Decay](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Abrupt+Decay%22), and [Tarmogoyf](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Tarmogoyf%22) to play an attrition-based game. Without blue cantrips, the deck compensates with raw card quality and proactive disruption. Rakdos variants drop green for a leaner mana base with [Death's Shadow](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Death%27s+Shadow%22) or aggressive creatures. Grixis midrange variants add blue for cantrips and [Murktide Regent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Murktide+Regent%22), overlapping with tempo strategies. Arclight Phoenix builds use cantrips and looting effects to recur Phoenix from the graveyard.

| [Lightning Bolt](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Lightning+Bolt%22) | Premium removal and reach |

### Variants

Jund, Jund Aggro, Rakdos variants (Aggro, Channeler, [Death's Shadow](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Death%27s+Shadow%22), Discard, Goyfs, Helm, Moonshadow), Grixis variants (Basim, Cauldron, Frog, Goyfs, Junk, Lutri, Phoenix), and Arclight Phoenix builds all fall under this umbrella.

- **Even**: [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) (depends on builds), Stompy (depends on lock pieces drawn)

## 29. Creativity / Polymorph

**Strategy**: Use [Indomitable Creativity](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Indomitable+Creativity%22) or [Polymorph](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Polymorph%22) to destroy your own token, then reveal cards until you hit the only creature/artifact in your deck -- a game-ending threat.
**Colors**: Jeskai or 4 Color
**Tier**: 3
**Meta Share**: <0.5%

### Overview

Creativity decks run token generators ([Fable of the Mirror-Breaker](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Fable+of+the+Mirror-Breaker%22), [Dwarven Mine](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Dwarven+Mine%22), hard-cast Prismari Command) alongside [Indomitable Creativity](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Indomitable+Creativity%22) or [Polymorph](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Polymorph%22) with a single creature in the deck (typically Emrakul or [Archon of Cruelty](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Archon+of+Cruelty%22)). Since there's only one creature, Creativity always finds it. Creative Technique uses the demonstrate mechanic for a similar effect. The Jeskai shell provides countermagic and cantrips to protect the combo.

## 30. Cascade

**Strategy**: Use cascade spells to automatically cast a specific powerful spell by manipulating the deck's mana curve.
**Colors**: 4 Color or Temur
**Tier**: 3
**Meta Share**: <0.5%

### Overview

Cascade decks ensure that cascade always hits a specific devastating spell by running no other spells below the cascade spell's CMC. [Shardless Agent](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Shardless+Agent%22) (cascade at CMC 2 or less) guarantees hitting Ancestral Vision, [Hymn to Tourach](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Hymn+to+Tourach%22), or similar. Violent Outburst enables [Hypergenesis](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Hypergenesis%22) or Living End. Valki/Tibalt cascade exploits MDFC rules. Marauder Cascade uses Bloodbraid Marauder's delirium cascade. Cascade Crash uses [Crashing Footfalls](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Crashing+Footfalls%22) for free 4/4 Rhinos.

## 32. Esper / Other Midrange

**Strategy**: Various midrange strategies that combine efficient threats with interaction, not fitting cleanly into other categories.
**Colors**: Varies (primarily Esper, Dimir, Orzhov, Izzet, Jeskai)
**Tier**: 2.5-3
**Meta Share**: ~2% (combined)

### Overview

This catch-all category covers midrange strategies that don't belong to the Delver, Ocelot, Stoneblade, or Jund families. Esper Midrange pairs [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22) with white removal and blue cantrips. Dimir Midrange is a slower, non-Delver UB build focused on card advantage. [Thopter Foundry](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thopter+Foundry%22) decks use the Thopter-Sword combo (Thopter Foundry + Sword of the Meek) for incremental value. Faeries uses [Bitterblossom](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Bitterblossom%22) and Spellstutter Sprite for a tribal-tempo approach.

| Card | Role |
|------|------|
| [Orcish Bowmasters](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Orcish+Bowmasters%22) | Punishes cantrips; generates Orc Army tokens |
| [Thopter Foundry](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thopter+Foundry%22) + Sword of the Meek | Infinite Thopter loop with enough mana |

### Variants

#### Esper Midrange (WUB, Tier 2.5)

Bowmasters, [Stoneforge Mystic](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Stoneforge+Mystic%22), cantrips, and premium removal. Sub-variants include Ketramose (Teferi-focused), Aggro (faster clock), and Bowmasters-centric builds. Stronger than Stoneblade in some metagames due to Bowmasters' impact.

#### Dimir Midrange / Lessons (UB, Tier 3)

Non-Delver UB builds using Lórien Revealed, Lessons package, or [The One Ring](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22The+One+Ring%22) for card advantage. Slower and grindier than Dimir Tempo with fewer cheap threats and more card advantage engines.

#### Orzhov variants (WB, Tier 3)

Dauthi Voidwalker builds, Evoke ([Grief](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Grief%22)/[Solitude](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Solitude%22)), Convoke, Sacrifice (Goblin Bombardment), Saga, Samwise, and Initiative versions. Each exploits different WB synergies.

#### Izzet / UR variants (UR, Tier 3)

Izzet Coin (Frenetic Efreet / coin flip), Izzet Cutter, UR Spells ([Monastery Mentor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Monastery+Mentor%22) / [Young Pyromancer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Young+Pyromancer%22)). Jeskai Nulldrifter uses the evoke creature for card advantage.

#### Thopter Foundry / Counter Top (UWx, Tier 3)

[Thopter Foundry](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Thopter+Foundry%22) + Sword of the Meek generates infinite 1/1 fliers with enough mana. Countertop Thopters combines this with the [Counterbalance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Counterbalance%22) lock. Pure Counter Top decks play the Counterbalance + top manipulation game without the Thopter combo.

#### Faeries / Faerie Stompy (UB/U, Tier 3)

[Bitterblossom](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Bitterblossom%22), Spellstutter Sprite, and Mistbind Clique provide a tribal tempo package. Faerie Stompy uses Sol lands and Chalice alongside the Faerie package for a hybrid prison-tempo approach.

#### Fringe Midrange (various, Tier 3)

Zoo (aggressive multi-color creatures), Hammer Time (Colossus Hammer + Sigarda's Aid), [Goblin Welder](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Goblin+Welder%22) midrange, and other unique builds that combine threats with interaction.

### Matchups

- **Favorable**: Other fair decks (card quality dependent), Burn (interaction + threats), creature mirrors (depends on build)
- **Unfavorable**: Fast combo (not fast enough to clock, not enough disruption), Lands (hard to interact with), greedy builds lose to [Wasteland](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Wasteland%22)
- **Even**: Delver (close, build-dependent), [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22) (depends on specific variant), control (grind matchup)

## Summary

This guide covers **32 parent archetypes** encompassing over **200 individual deck variants** across the full Legacy metagame. From the dominant Dimir Tempo (14.6% meta share) to fringe strategies like Battle of Wits, the format's depth is unmatched in competitive Magic.

**Tier 1 (>5% combined meta share)**: Delver/Tempo (~23%), [Ocelot Pride](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Ocelot+Pride%22)/Energy (~15%), [Show and Tell](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Show+and+Tell%22) (~10%), Oops/Belcher (~10%)

**Tier 1.5 (2-5% combined)**: Storm (~5%), Control (~5%), Painter (~4.1%), Lands (~3.8%), [Mystic Forge](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Mystic+Forge%22) (~3.7%), Prison/Stompy (~15% combined sub-archetypes), Reanimator (~2%), [Doomsday](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Doomsday%22) (~2.7%)

**Tier 2 (1-2% combined)**: Stoneblade (~2%), Death and Taxes (~2%), Dredge (~1.8%), Pox/Mono Black (~3.3%), [Natural Order](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Natural+Order%22)/Green Toolbox (~2.5%), [Aluren](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Aluren%22) (~1.2%), [Cloudpost](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Cloudpost%22) (~1.0%), Esper/Other Midrange (~2%)

**Tier 2.5-3 (<1% combined)**: Tribal, [Food Chain](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!%22Food+Chain%22), Infect, Burn, Helm Combo, Blink, Cephalid Breakfast, Stiflenought, Jund, Creativity, Cascade, Miscellaneous

Legacy's metagame is self-correcting: as any single strategy gains dominance, hate cards and counter-strategies emerge to check it. This guide serves as both a deckbuilding reference and a matchup resource for navigating the format's extraordinary diversity.
